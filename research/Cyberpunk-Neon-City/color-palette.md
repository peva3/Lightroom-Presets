## Color Theory Foundation

### The Cyberpunk Color Wheel

The cyberpunk neon palette operates on a **split-complementary** arrangement centered around 300° (magenta):

```
Primary:   Magenta / Hot Pink  (290°–330°)
Secondary: Cyan / Teal         (170°–200°)
Accent:    Electric Blue       (210°–250°)
Accent:    Yellow-Green (rare) (70°–90°) — used sparingly in UI elements
Neutral:   Deep Blue-Black     (240°–260°, low luminance)
Neutral:   Warm White          (40°–60°, low saturation) — practical light sources
```

### Why Magenta + Cyan?

| Color | Wavelength | Cyberpunk Meaning |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| Magenta (#FF00FF) | ~380-450 THz | Non-spectral color — doesn't exist in nature. The most "synthetic" color. |
| Cyan (#00FFFF) | ~490-520 nm | Associated with computer screens, holography, digital displays |
| Deep Blue (#000033) | ~450-485 nm | Night sky, deep water, the datasphere |

Magenta is an **extra-spectral color** — it has no single wavelength in the visible spectrum. The brain invents magenta to bridge red and violet. This makes it the *perfect* cyberpunk signifier: a color that is literally a neurological illusion, just as replicants are illusions of humanity.

## Blade Runner 2049 — Scene-by-Scene Color Analysis

### Los Angeles — The "Cyan City"
- **Dominant**: Cyan/Teal (`#00B4D8` to `#0077B6`)
- **Accent**: Warm practicals (headlights, window light)
- **Atmosphere**: Heavy particulate, constant rain/snow-like ash
- **Key Hex**: `#03045E` (deep sky), `#00B4D8` (cyan neon), `#CAF0F8` (bloom highlights)

### Las Vegas — The "Orange Wasteland"
- **Dominant**: Toxic orange/amber (`#FF6B35` to `#E85D04`)
- **Accent**: None. Monochromatic orange. Oppressive.
- **Atmosphere**: Dust storm, particulate, radioactive haze
- **Key Hex**: `#DC2F02` (deep orange), `#FAA307` (dust sky), `#9D0208` (dark rust)

### Wallace Corporation — "Sterile White"
- **Dominant**: Near-white with golden warmth (`#FFF3E0` to `#FFE0B2`)
- **Accent**: Water reflections (golden ripples)
- **Atmosphere**: Minimalist, architectural, god-like light beams
- **Key Hex**: `#FFFAF0` (brightest white), `#D4A373` (warm gold), `#FAEDCD` (wall tone)

### K's Apartment / Joi Scenes — "Magenta Intimacy"
- **Dominant**: Magenta/Pink (`#FF006E` to `#8338EC`)
- **Accent**: Cyan from city windows bleeding in
- **Atmosphere**: Claustrophobic, intimate, artificial warmth
- **Key Hex**: `#FF006E` (Joi's pink), `#3A0CA3` (deep purple shadow), `#7209B7` (purple midtone)

### Sapper Morton's Farm — "Dead Earth"
- **Dominant**: Desaturated brown/grey (`#8B7355` to `#6B5B4F`)
- **Accent**: Grey sky, muted greens (the only green in the film — and it's dying)
- **Atmosphere**: Overcast, oppressive, the end of nature
- **Key Hex**: `#A9A9A9` (overcast sky), `#6B5B4F` (dead soil)

## Blade Runner (1982) — Scene-by-Scene Color Analysis

### Tyrell Corporation Pyramid — "Golden Power"
- **Dominant**: Warm gold/amber (`#FFD700` to `#B8860B`)
- **Accent**: Deep browns, polished surfaces
- **Atmosphere**: Cathedral-like, religious lighting, god-rays
- **Key Hex**: `#FFD700` (golden light), `#8B4513` (warm wood tones)

### Street Level — "Neon Bazaar"
- **Dominant**: Cool cyan/blue ambient (`#1E3A5F`)
- **Accent**: Multicolored neon (pink, red, cyan, yellow-green)
- **Atmosphere**: Constant rain, steam, crowds, chaos
- **Key Hex**: `#FF1493` (pink neon), `#00CED1` (cyan neon), `#1E3A5F` (rain-wet night)

### Bradbury Building / Sebastian's Apartment — "Decaying Grandeur"
- **Dominant**: Warm amber interior light through dusty windows
- **Accent**: Deep shadows, cool fill from exterior
- **Atmosphere**: Cluttered, decaying, beautiful ruin
- **Key Hex**: `#DEB887` (dusty light), `#2F1B14` (deep shadow)

### Roy Batty's Death Scene — "Tears in Rain"
- **Dominant**: Cool cyan/blue (`#4682B4`)
- **Accent**: Warm practicals in background windows
- **Atmosphere**: Rain, release, transcendence
- **Key Hex**: `#4682B4` (rain light), `#191970` (night sky)

## Cyberpunk Color Grading LUT Reference

### Common LUT Approaches

| LUT Style | Description | Hex Range |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Teal/Orange Blockbuster | Standard Hollywood grade; teal shadows, warm skin | Not cyberpunk but a base layer |
| Magenta/Cyan Inversion | Swap the standard teal/orange for magenta/cyan | Cyberpunk signature |
| Cineon Film Print | Film emulation that crushes blacks and creates rich color | Base for many presets |
| Kodak 2383 / Fuji 3513 | Film print LUTs with warm highlights, cool shadows | Often used as starting point |
| ACES 1.0 | Academy Color Encoding System transform | Reference standard for film |

### Building the Cyberpunk LUT Chain

```
1. Input Transform:    Camera LOG → ACEScct or Davinci Wide Gamut
2. Creative Grade:     Balance, contrast, saturation (pre-LUT)
3. Look LUT:           Magenta/Cyan creative LUT at ~60-80% opacity
4. Post-LUT Adjust:    HSL refinement, calibration tweaks
5. Output Transform:   ACEScct → Rec.709
```
