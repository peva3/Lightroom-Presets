# Film References — Cyberpunk Neon City Visual Language

> Key films, their visual language, and how they built the cyberpunk aesthetic.

---

## Blade Runner (1982) — The Ur-Text

**Director:** Ridley Scott
**Cinematographer:** Jordan Cronenweth
**Production Designer:** Lawrence G. Paull
**Concept Artist:** Syd Mead
**Composer:** Vangelis

### Visual DNA

*Blade Runner* established the foundational visual language that all subsequent cyberpunk media draws from:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Perpetual Night/Rain** | The film takes place almost entirely at night, in constant rain. This creates reflective surfaces that double every light source. |
| **"Hong Kong on a very bad day"** | Scott's own description. Dense, vertical, multilingual signage, chaotic street life, extreme wealth/poverty juxtaposition. |
| **Retrofitting** | Future technology layered on top of old architecture. The Bradbury Building (1893) becomes 2019 Los Angeles. Past and future coexist. |
| **Sodium Vapor + Neon** | A mix of warm sodium streetlights and cool neon signage creates the signature color contrast. |
| **Volumetric Lighting** | Shafts of light cutting through smoke/steam/rain. Light as a physical presence in the frame. |
| **The Tyrell Pyramid** | Massive ziggurat structure — corporate power literally towering above the city. Inspired by Mayan/Aztec architecture and Fritz Lang's *Metropolis*. |

### Color Strategy

- **Warm interiors** (Tyrell's office, Deckard's apartment) vs. **Cool exteriors** (streets, rooftops)
- **Pools of colored light** in otherwise dark frames — the city is illuminated by advertising, not by the sun
- **Golden hour never exists** — the sun is never seen. The sky is perpetually overcast and polluted.
- **Key palette**: Deep blue-black, cyan, amber, magenta accents

### Influences Cited by Scott

- Edward Hopper's painting *Nighthawks* (1942) — isolated figures in pools of artificial light
- *Métal Hurlant* (Heavy Metal) magazine — Jean "Moebius" Giraud's sci-fi illustrations
- Fritz Lang's *Metropolis* (1927) — vertical city, class stratification, the Machine
- Antonio Sant'Elia's futurist architecture — industrial, monumental, mechanical
- The industrial landscape of northeast England (Scott's childhood home)

### Production Facts
- Budget: $30 million
- Shot on: 35mm film (Panavision) with extensive miniatures and matte paintings
- The "Hades landscape" opening was created with practical flame effects and miniature refinery models
- The eye is a recurring motif (the opening eye, the Voight-Kampff test, Tyrell's glasses, Roy's death)

## Blade Runner 2049 (2017) — The Sequel That Expanded the Palette

**Director:** Denis Villeneuve
**Cinematographer:** Roger A. Deakins
**Production Designer:** Dennis Gassner
**Composers:** Hans Zimmer, Benjamin Wallfisch

### Visual Evolution from the Original

| Original (1982) | Sequel (2017) |
|----------------|---------------|
| Crowded, chaotic streets | Empty, vast, monumental spaces |
| Warm amber + cool cyan | Distinct color zones per location |
| Practical miniatures | CGI + practical hybrids |
| Intimate noir close-ups | Epic wide compositions |
| The city as character | The landscape as character |

### Color Zones

*Blade Runner 2049* is structured around distinct color territories:

#### Los Angeles — Cyan / Grey
- Perpetual overcast, snow-like ash falling
- Massive brutalist architecture (inspired by Barbican Estate, London; Beijing smog; Bangladeshi shipyards)
- Cyan-heavy color grade, sterile and cold
- Vast scale — humans are tiny against the architecture

#### Sapper Morton's Farm — Dead Earth / Brown
- The only scene with natural light (overcast)
- Desaturated browns, muted greens of dying crops
- The last organic life — and it's failing
- Introduction of the dead tree / flower motif

#### Wallace Corporation — Gold / Water
- Warm golden light reflecting off water surfaces
- Minimalist, architectural, almost religious
- Water as a recurring motif (life, memory, the womb)
- Jared Leto's Wallace lit like a deity — blind, seeing through drone cameras

#### Las Vegas — Toxic Orange
- Monochromatic orange/amber
- Radioactive dust storm
- Decayed luxury — holographic Elvis, crumbling casino statues
- The ghost of American excess

#### K's Apartment / Joi — Magenta / Pink
- Intimate magenta/pink lighting from Joi's holographic presence
- The warmest color in the film — but it's artificial warmth
- Joi shifts between pink, purple, and pale yellow depending on her "mood"
- The apartment is a cage with a view of the cyan city

### Technical Achievements
- Shot on: Arri Alexa XT Studio, 1.55:1 aspect ratio, Zeiss Master Prime lenses
- Won Academy Awards for **Best Cinematography** and **Best Visual Effects**
- Deakins used a single-camera setup (rejected multi-camera for quality control)
- 14mm and 16mm lenses for cityscapes; 32mm for close-ups
- Minimal CGI — extensive practical sets built in Budapest (Korda Studios, Origo Studios)
- Budapest Stock Exchange interior doubled for Las Vegas casino

### Deakins' Approach
- "I don't light spaces, I light faces" — emotional lighting drives the aesthetic
- Shot in 3.4K ARRIRAW, mastered in 4K
- Deliberate use of negative fill (removing light) rather than adding light
- The ash/snow was practical — cellulose-based particles falling on set

## Cyberpunk Visual Language — The Common Threads

### Architecture
- **Brutalism**: Raw concrete, massive scale, inhuman proportions
- **Vertical Stratification**: The wealthy live above, the poor below (Tyrell pyramid, Wallace water-temple)
- **Retrofitting**: Future tech bolted onto old buildings
- **Asian Megacity Influence**: Tokyo (Shinjuku/Shibuya), Hong Kong (Kowloon Walled City), Chongqing — dense, vertical, neon-saturated
- **Industrial Decay**: Abandoned factories, refineries, power plants repurposed

### Lighting
- **No Sunlight**: The sun either doesn't exist or is toxic/irrelevant
- **Artificial Light Only**: Neon, LED, holography, practical lamps
- **Motivated Color**: Light color tells you whose territory you're in (corporate = cool, street = warm, synthetic = magenta)
- **Volumetric Atmosphere**: Smoke, rain, fog, ash — light becomes visible in the air
- **Low-Key**: Deep shadows, pools of light, noir tradition

### Technology Visualization
- **Holographic Interfaces**: Floating UI elements in physical space
- **CRT / Scanline Aesthetics**: Even in advanced futures, low-res displays persist
- **Cybernetic Bodies**: The line between human and machine is blurred
- **Giant Video Billboards**: Advertising as architectural-scale light pollution
- **Flying Vehicles**: Spinners, AVs — traffic is three-dimensional

### The Human Figure
- **Trench Coats**: Deckard's coat became the cyberpunk uniform (noir detective heritage)
- **Synthetic Materials**: Plastic raincoats, translucent umbrellas, vinyl
- **Body Modification**: Visible cybernetics, scars, implants
- **Isolation**: Figures are alone in crowds, dwarfed by architecture

### Sound Design Correlates
- **Vangelis (BR 1982)**: Synthesizer + saxophone — melancholic, romantic, synthetic
- **Zimmer/Wallfisch (BR 2049)**: Massive bass, industrial percussion, distorted synths — brutal, overwhelming
- **Cyberpunk 2077**: Synthwave + industrial + hip-hop hybrid
- **Common thread**: Electronic music with emotional melancholy (the "sad robot" feeling)

## Reference Stills for Color Grading Study

For building Lightroom presets, study these specific scenes:

### Blade Runner (1982)
1. **Opening Hades landscape** — Fire against black sky, the eye reflection
2. **Deckard at the noodle bar** — Crowded street, neon signs, rain
3. **Tyrell's office** — Warm gold interior, shafts of light
4. **Bradbury Building interiors** — Dusty light, amber + cyan contrast
5. **"Tears in rain" rooftop** — Rain, blue light, dying neon

### Blade Runner 2049 (2017)
1. **Opening farm scene** — Grey overcast, dead soil, muted greens
2. **K flying over LA** — Cyan cityscape, ash/snow, massive scale
3. **Wallace Corporation** — Golden water reflections, minimal architecture
4. **Las Vegas orange wasteland** — Toxic monochrome, holographic decay
5. **Joi on the rooftop** — Pink hologram against cyan city, rain
6. **Sea wall finale** — Grey ocean, crashing waves, muted palette

### Key References Beyond Blade Runner
- *Akira* — The opening motorcycle chase through Neo-Tokyo
- *Ghost in the Shell (1995)* — The city montage (set to "Making of a Cyborg")
- *Altered Carbon* — Bay City aerial establishing shots
- *Cyberpunk 2077* — Night City skyline from the badlands
