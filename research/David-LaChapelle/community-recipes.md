# Community Recipes — David LaChapelle

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/postprocessing
- LaChapelle's hyper-real saturation is both admired and considered "too much" depending on taste
- His work is frequently compared to pop art, Jeff Koons, and high-fashion advertising
- Retouching is a major discussion point — LaChapelle's images are heavily retouched

### From YouTube Tutorials
- "Pop art color grading" tutorials overlap with LaChapelle aesthetics:
  - High saturation across warm spectrum (red, orange, yellow, magenta)
  - High contrast with clarity boost
  - Cool shadow undertone for color contrast
- Some creators note: this look is intentionally artificial — don't try to make it "natural"

### From Commercial Photography Forums
- LaChapelle's lighting is as important as his color — multi-source, sculptural, often HDR-like
- The surreal color comes from lighting ratios as much as post-processing
- His transition from film to digital changed his palette — digital LaChapelle is more saturated than film LaChapelle

### Community Consensus
- Maximum saturation that doesn't clip — push red/orange/yellow/magenta hard
- Contrast must support the saturation (aggressive but not clipping)
- This preset is for maximalist images — apply at 70-85% for most photos
- The look is intentionally "too much" — it's a statement, not a mistake
