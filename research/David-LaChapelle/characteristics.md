# David LaChapelle

## Biography
- **Name**: David LaChapelle (born 1963)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Era**: 1980s–present

## Philosophy
- Pop culture as religion — celebrity, consumerism, and spectacle photographed like Renaissance paintings
- "I'm trying to make pictures that you can't stop looking at."
- Trained under Andy Warhol at Interview magazine in the 1980s
- Blends high art references (Michelangelo, Botticelli) with low culture (gas stations, fast food)
- Left commercial photography in 2006 for fine art

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: Medium format (Hasselblad, Mamiya), large format, digital
- **Film**: Kodak Ektachrome, various color films, later full digital
- **Lighting**: Elaborate multi-light setups; hyper-real, unnatural, sculptural lighting
- **Post-production**: Heavy retouching — skin made plastic-smooth, colors pushed hyper-real
- **Sets**: Diorama-scale constructions; every element controlled

## Visual Signature
- **Hyper-real saturation**: Colors pushed beyond natural into a pop art dimension. Red is RED. Blue is BLUE.
- **Vivid, punchy contrast**: High contrast with saturated highlights — the opposite of a muted film look
- **Pop art sensibility**: Campbell's soup can, gas station, fast food — elevated to religious iconography
- **Sculptural bodies**: Bodies lit and retouched to look like marble or plastic — inhuman perfection
- **Surreal juxtaposition**: A model in couture at a dusty gas station; Jesus in a fast food joint
- **No subtlety ever**: Every image is a maximalist assault — color, composition, concept all at maximum intensity

## Iconic Images
- *Amanda Lepore as Andy Warhol's Marilyn* (2007)
- *The Raft of Illusion: Raging Toward Truth* (2017)
- *Alexander McQueen and Isabella Blow* (1996)

## Preset Design Notes
- Highest saturation preset in the Photographer collection
- Red, orange, yellow, and magenta saturation driven up (pop color spectrum)
- Moderate warmth via highlight and midtone color grading
- Cool shadow undertone to create color contrast against warm saturation
- Slight clarity boost for definition
- Positive exposure and contrast for the punchy, bright look
- Blending at 75 for clean color separation
