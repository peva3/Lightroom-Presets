# Rollei Retro 400S — Film Characteristics

## Overview
Rollei Retro 400S is a super-panchromatic 400-speed black-and-white film, the faster sibling of Retro 80S. Also based on Agfa Aviphot aerial surveillance emulsion, it extends sensitivity into the near-infrared range. Sold under multiple brand names globally including JCH StreetPan 400, Astrum Foto 400, and Rollei IR 400.

## Key Characteristics
- **ISO**: 400 (nominal); can be shot at 200-800 depending on filtration and development
- **Grain**: Moderate to heavy — more pronounced than Retro 80S due to higher speed
- **Sharpness**: Good — retains the aerial surveillance resolution heritage
- **Contrast**: Moderate-high — punchier than Retro 80S
- **Spectral Sensitivity**: Super-panchromatic — extended red + near-IR sensitivity to ~750nm
  - Extremely bright reds and oranges
  - Very dark blue skies (natural IR sky darkening)
  - Bright vegetation (IR reflectance from chlorophyll)
- **Exposure Latitude**: Moderate — requires attention to exposure with extended red sensitivity
- **Development**: Compatible with all standard developers; Rodinal, HC-110, and D-76 are popular
- **Anti-Halation Layer**: Clear polyester base — halation effects present

## Historical Context
- Based on Agfa Aviphot 400S aerial film — "S" stands for "Surveillance"
- Aerial film designed for high-altitude haze penetration and target identification
- Rollei/Maco distributes as "Retro 400S" and "IR 400" (same film, different packaging)
- Japan Camera Hunter (Bellamy Hunt) sells as "JCH StreetPan 400"
- Astrum (Ukraine) sells as "Astrum Foto 400"
- Svema (Ukraine) sells as "Svema Foto 400"
- Truly international film — one Belgian master roll, many global brands

## Visual Signature
- Dramatic sky darkening without filters — the defining characteristic
- Bright, luminous skin tones (extended red penetration through epidermis)
- Glowing vegetation highlights (near-IR chlorophyll reflectance)
- Pronounced grain with an organic, high-acutance character
- High contrast between sky and ground typical of aerial surveillance
- A bold, dramatic, high-impact look — not subtle
- Ritchie Roesch (Fuji X Weekly) created a full-spectrum IR recipe inspired by this film
