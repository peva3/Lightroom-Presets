# Kodak Vericolor III 160 — Film Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

> Professional portrait film (pre-1998). Extremely low contrast, very pastel, magenta-leaning shadows. The wedding and school portrait standard for 20+ years. Introduced 1983, replaced by Portra 160 in 1998.

## Historical Context

Kodak introduced the Vericolor line in the early 1970s. Vericolor III arrived in 1983 as the third-generation professional color negative film. Two versions existed: the daylight-balanced Vericolor III 160 Type-S (later just "Vericolor III 160") and the tungsten-balanced Vericolor III 100 Type-L (later renamed Ektacolor Pro Gold 100T, then Portra 100T).

Vericolor III was THE standard for professional portrait, wedding, and school photography throughout the 1980s and 1990s. Its extremely low contrast and pastel palette were engineered to produce flattering skin tones across diverse lighting conditions. The magenta-leaning shadow cast was a deliberate design choice to neutralize the greenish cast that skin can pick up in shade.

In the late 1980s, Kodak introduced Vericolor III 400 for higher-speed needs. Both 160 and 400 were replaced in 1998 by Portra 160 and Portra 400 respectively.

## Key Color Signatures

1. **Contrast**: Dramatically lower than any modern color negative film. This is the #1 defining trait.
2. **Shadows**: Distinctive cool-magenta cast (not green, not blue, not neutral).
3. **Saturation**: Very pastel — colors are gentle and subdued, never bold.
4. **Skin tones**: Soft, low-contrast, extremely forgiving. Redness is suppressed, warmth is moderate.
5. **Highlight behavior**: Long, gentle shoulder. Specular highlights bloom rather than clip.
6. **Blacks**: Never truly black — lifted, pastel base.
7. **Grain**: Effectively invisible at normal sizes.

## Fuji X Weekly Recipe (2026)

The [Fuji X Weekly recipe by Ritchie Roesch](https://fujixweekly.com/2026/02/20/) uses Nostalgic Neg as the base film simulation and incorporates WB 3200K with +7 Red & -8 Blue shift to create the cool-magenta shadow character. Strong Color Chrome Effect and Color Chrome FX Blue compress the color gamut for the pastel Vericolor look.

## Relevance for Digital Emulation

Vericolor III is fundamentally defined by contrast polarity — its -30 contrast is the most extreme in any Color-Negative preset. No other film stock comes close to its low-contrast, pastel character. The magenta shadow cast (achieved via Color Grading shadows at Hue 320°) is the second defining trait.
