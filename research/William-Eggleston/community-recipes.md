# Community Recipes — William Eggleston

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/analog
- Eggleston's dye-transfer prints are the holy grail of color reproduction
- Dye-transfer is essentially unreproducible from digital originals — the process controls each dye layer separately
- Expired film + overcast light + mundane subjects = the Eggleston formula according to fans

### From YouTube Tutorials
- Limited tutorials due to Eggleston's subtlety — "There's no slider for genius"
- Some creators suggest copy-stand reproductions of his book prints as reference for color grading
- The consensus is: don't over-think the edit. Eggleston shot what was there.

### From Color Grading Forums
- The "democratic color" concept means: treat all colors equally, don't favor one over another
- Slight global warmth is the only consistent finding
- Dye-transfer saturation is deep but not neon-bright — this is the hardest effect to capture in a preset

### Community Consensus
- Eggleston's photos succeed because of what he chose to photograph, not the processing
- The preset provides a warm, slightly saturated starting point that honors the ordinary
- Avoid dramatic edits — this is anti-Instagram
