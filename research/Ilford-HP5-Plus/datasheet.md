## Characteristic Curves (from Ilford datasheet)

### D-Log E Curves
- **Shoulder**: Gentle roll-off in highlights — forgiving with overexposure
- **Linear region**: Long straight-line section, roughly Zone III to Zone VIII at box speed
- **Toe**: Moderate toe — good shadow separation, holds detail down to ~3 stops under
- **Gamma (ID-11 Stock)**: ~0.56 at recommended development
- **Gamma (ID-11 1+1)**: ~0.52 — slightly softer gradation for scanning

### Effective Film Speed vs Development
- **EI 400** (box speed): recommended dev times in all developers
- **EI 800** (+1 push): ~10.5 min ID-11 Stock, ~9 min DD-X (1+4)
- **EI 1600** (+2 push): ~14 min ID-11 Stock, ~12 min DD-X (1+4)
- **EI 3200** (+3 push): MICROPHEN Stock ~16 min, DD-X ~14 min

## Spectral Sensitivity

HP5 Plus is a **panchromatic** film with extended red sensitivity (not quite orthopanchromatic). The sensitivity curve shows:

- Peak sensitivity in the **green-yellow** region (~550nm)
- Slight dip in blue-green (~490nm) — renders blue skies slightly darker than ortho films
- Strong red sensitivity past 640nm — renders caucasian skin tones naturally
- Sensitivity falls off sharply above ~660nm
- **No IR sensitivity** — safe for IR-frame counting in modern cameras

### Recommended Filters & Exposure Factors

| Filter | Factor | f/stop increase |
|---|---|---|
| Light Yellow | 1.5 | +0.5 |
| Yellow | 2 | +1 |
| Yellow-Green | 2.5 | +1.3 |
| Green | 3 | +1.7 |
| Orange | 4 | +2 |
| Red | 8 | +3 |
| Deep Red | 10 | +3.3 |
| Light Blue | 1.5 | +0.5 |
| Polarizer | 3.5 | +1.8 |

## Grain Data

- **RMS Granularity** (ID-11 Stock): ~10 (medium-fine for a 400-speed film)
- **Grain appearance**: Cubic, "classic" grain structure — visible at 8×10 from 35mm, pleasant salt-and-pepper texture
- **Grain vs speed**: Smoother than Kodak Tri-X at equivalent speed; grain transitions more gradually into mid-tones
- **At EI 800**: Grain becomes more prominent but remains well-controlled
- **At EI 1600**: Noticeable grain, crisp edges, classic pushed look
- **At EI 3200**: Heavy, gritty grain — desired aesthetic for some documentary work

## Disposable Camera Configuration

Ilford produces a B&W single-use camera loaded with 27 exposures of HP5 Plus. The camera uses:
- Fixed aperture ~f/9.5
- Shutter speed ~1/100s
- Fixed focus from ~1m to infinity
- Effective EI matched to bright daylight (ISO 400) and electronic flash
