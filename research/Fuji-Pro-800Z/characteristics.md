# Fuji Pro 800Z — Full Aesthetic Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

> High-speed professional portrait film. Fujifilm's answer to Portra 800 — warm tones, moderate grain, low-light mastery.

---

## Executive Summary

Fuji Pro 800Z (marketed as Fujicolor PRO 800Z) was Fujifilm's professional 800-speed color negative film. It was designed for low-light portrait and wedding work — indoor ceremonies, receptions, concert photography. Warm-biased with moderate, structured grain and surprisingly fine color fidelity for its speed class. Discontinued around 2010. This document serves as a reference for creating a Lightroom preset that emulates Pro 800Z.

---

## 1. The Color Palette

| Color Region | Pro 800Z Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Skin Tones** | Warm, flattering. The defining feature — 800Z was a portrait film first. |
| **Whites** | Clean neutral. Holds highlight detail well despite high speed. |
| **Shadows** | Warm-tending, open. Not crushed. Moderate shadow contrast. |
| **Greens** | Natural, slightly warm-green. Not cyan-cool like 400H. |
| **Blues** | Moderately saturated, natural. Handles mixed light well. |
| **Reds/Yellows** | Warm with good saturation. Not aggressive. |

---

## 2. Contrast & Tonality

- **Moderate contrast** — slightly higher than 160NS/400H, fitting the 800-speed design
- Professional roll-off in highlights (less latitude than 400-speed, but still graceful)
- Good shadow detail — open, not blocked
- Designed for mixed artificial + natural light scenarios (weddings, events)

---

## 3. Grain Structure

- **Moderate, visible grain** — characteristic of 800-speed films
- Structured, even grain pattern (not clumpy)
- Finer than consumer 800-speed films, coarser than 400-speed PRO line
- Grain is part of the aesthetic — adds texture without being distracting
- RMS granularity: ~10–12

---

## 4. Comparison: Pro 800Z vs Key Films

| Aspect | Pro 800Z | Portra 800 | Pro 400H |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Speed** | 800 | 800 | 400 |
| **Color bias** | Warm-neutral | Warm (amber) | Cool (mint) |
| **Contrast** | Moderate | Moderate-high | Low |
| **Grain** | Visible, structured | Visible | Fine |
| **Skin tones** | Warm, flattering | Warm, golden | Cool, pastel |
| **Best for** | Low-light portraits | Low-light general | Weddings, editorial |

---

## 5. Digital Emulation Targets

1. Warm-biased color palette (portrait-first)
2. Moderate visible grain (Amount 35, Size 30)
3. Slightly soft contrast with clean highlight roll-off
4. Warm, flattering skin tones (Orange Lum +15)
5. Natural blue/green rendering — not desaturated dramatically
6. Open shadows with warmth
