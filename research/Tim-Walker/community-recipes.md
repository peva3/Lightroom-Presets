# Community Recipes — Tim Walker

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/fashionphotography
- Walker's set design is discussed more than his editing
- The "pastel fashion" look is widely requested but hard to achieve without the physical set design
- High-key lighting technique is the foundation — overexpose and lift everything

### From YouTube Tutorials
- "Tim Walker style" tutorials focus on:
  - High key exposure (+0.5 to +1.0 EV)
  - Lifted black point (faded shadows)
  - Negative clarity and dehaze for dreamy softness
  - Pastel color grading: mint greens, powder blues, blush pinks
- Julia Trotti (200K+): Light and airy editing overlaps significantly with Walker aesthetics

### From Fashion Editing Communities
- The pastel palette requires: desaturated greens shifted toward mint, muted blues, preserved warm skin
- "Less is more" in color — the pastel comes from what you remove, not what you add
- The softness (negative clarity) is what separates Walker's look from standard fashion brightening

### Community Consensus
- High key + pastel = Tim Walker
- Negative clarity + negative dehaze + negative texture = the softness trifecta
- Skin must stay warm and luminous against the cool pastel environment
- Not for every image — works best on bright, airy scenes with controlled color palettes
