# Tim Walker

## Biography
- **Name**: Tim Walker (born 1970)
- **Nationality**: British
- **Era**: 1990s–present

## Philosophy
- Fashion photography as fairy tale — every image is a miniature world
- "The most interesting thing in fashion is fantasy. Fantasy is truth."
- Builds elaborate sets and props; minimal post-production — capture it in-camera
- Inspired by Cecil Beaton, surrealism, and English pastoral romanticism
- Former assistant to Richard Avedon (contrast: Avedon's stark minimalism vs. Walker's maximal whimsy)

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: Pentax 67 medium format, Hasselblad, large format 8x10, later digital
- **Film**: Kodak Portra (color negative), large format
- **Lighting**: Soft, diffused, often natural light augmented with HMI
- **Sets**: Enormous physical constructions — giant dolls, pink castles, balloon clouds
- **Color palette**: Deliberately designed before shooting — wardrobe, set, and film all coordinated

## Visual Signature
- **Pastel palette**: Lavender, mint, blush pink, butter yellow, powder blue — never saturated, always soft
- **High key**: Everything is bright. Shadows exist only as gentle definition, never as darkness.
- **Soft, diffused light**: Like an overcast English summer day, even in studio
- **Dreamy atmosphere**: Slight haze, gentle blur at edges, negative clarity — a waking dream
- **Whimsical props**: Giant swans, teacup boats, flower suits — the impossible made physical
- **Gentle contrast**: Nothing punches, nothing is harsh. Tonal transitions are silky smooth.

## Iconic Images
- *Lily Cole in Giant Birdcage* (2005)
- *Tilda Swinton* series (Vanity Fair)
- *Story Teller* (2012 book)

## Preset Design Notes
- High key: significant exposure boost with lifted black point
- Negative contrast for soft tonal transitions
- Negative clarity, texture, and dehaze for the dreamy softness
- Pastel color achieved through negative global saturation + selected hue shifts
- Green shifted toward aqua/mint (pastel green)
- Shadows brightened and warmed slightly
- Blending at 75 for stylistic pastel separation
