# Kodak Pro Image 100 — Full Aesthetic Breakdown

## Overview

Kodak Professional Pro Image 100 is a medium-speed color negative film designed specifically for tropical/warm climate markets. Introduced in the late 1990s and particularly popular in Latin America, it was engineered for heat and humidity resistance — maintaining color accuracy and contrast even when stored without refrigeration in tropical conditions.

Despite its "Professional" designation, Pro Image 100 sits between consumer and professional films: it has pro-level skin tone rendering combined with consumer-grade contrast and saturation. It's often described as a warmer, more contrasty Portra — or a more refined ColorPlus/Ultramax.

---

## 1. Color Palette

| Channel | Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Reds** | Warm, natural — not overly saturated |
| **Oranges** | Pleasant skin tones — the portrait strength |
| **Yellows** | Golden-warm |
| **Greens** | Moderate, slightly warm |
| **Blues** | Subtle, clean — not muted, not vivid |
| **Magentas** | Controlled — portrait-friendly |

### Color Temperature
- Daylight balanced (~5500K)
- Slightly warm overall
- Designed to render skin tones beautifully in tropical light
- Warmer than Portra, cooler than Gold/Ultramax

---

## 2. Contrast Characteristics

- **Medium contrast** — professional portrait film
- Good midtone separation without harshness
- Shadows lifted for detail (portrait-friendly)
- Highlights controlled (tropical sun robustness)
- Less flat than Portra, more refined than Ultramax

---

## 3. Grain Structure

- **Fine grain for consumer/pro hybrid** — finer than Ultramax 400
- ISO 100 speed keeps grain minimal
- Professional-level smoothness in skin areas
- Pleasant texture without being dominant

## 4. Fuji X Weekly Reference Recipe

**Source:** [Fuji X Weekly — Pro Neg Recipe](https://fujixweekly.com/2025/07/22/pro-neg-fujifilm-x-t3-x-t30-x-trans-iii-film-simulation-recipe/) (Ritchie Roesch)

The "Pro Neg" recipe was designed to create a "generic Kodak-ish color negative film aesthetic, perhaps something similar to ColorPlus 200 or Pro Image 100." Roesch explicitly names both emulsions as inspiration:
- Film Simulation: PRO Neg. Hi
- DR400; Grain: Weak
- Color Chrome Effect: Off
- WB: 9100K (-7 Red, +7 Blue)
- Highlight +1; Shadow +1; Color +1
- Sharpness -1; High ISO NR -4
- Exposure Compensation: +1/3 to +1

Roesch notes: "I think there can be similarities at times to both of those film stocks, but generally speaking this Recipe isn't a replication of either — or any — film."

---

## 5. Tropical Climate Design

Pro Image 100 was specifically formulated to:
- Resist heat degradation (no cold storage needed)
- Maintain consistent color in high humidity
- Perform reliably in equatorial/direct sunlight
- Be the "all-day event" film for tropical wedding photographers

This practical robustness made it the film of choice for event/wedding photographers in Latin America and Southeast Asia.

---

## 6. Comparison

### Pro Image 100 vs Portra 160
- Warmer tones — less clinical
- Slightly higher contrast — more "pop"
- Consumer-grade pricing vs pro

### Pro Image 100 vs ColorPlus 200
- Pro Image is one stop slower (100 vs 200)
- Finer grain
- Warmer, more portrait-optimized
- Similar warm/golden bias

### Pro Image 100 vs Ultramax 400
- Pro Image is two stops slower
- Much finer grain
- Lower contrast, more natural skin
- Less saturated

---

## 7. Summary

> **Kodak Pro Image 100 is a warm, medium-contrast professional portrait film designed for tropical climates — delivering natural skin tones with a slight golden bias, fine grain, and excellent heat/humidity resistance that made it the go-to event film for photographers across Latin America.**
