# Kodak Pro Image 100 — Community Lightroom Recipes

> **Compiled from:** Fuji X Weekly (Ritchie Roesch — Pro Neg Recipe), r/analog, r/Lightroom, r/postprocessing, Latin American photography communities.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy

Pro Image 100 emulation centers on:
1. Warm portrait tones — skin is the priority
2. Medium contrast — professional but not flat
3. Fine grain — ISO 100 cleanliness
4. Subtle, natural saturation — not "consumer pop"
5. Clean, controlled color across all channels

---

## 2. Fuji X Weekly PRO Neg Recipe Translation

The Pro Neg recipe (designed to evoke ColorPlus 200 AND Pro Image 100):

| Fuji Parameter | Value | LR Translation |
|---|---|---|
| PRO Neg. Hi | — | Adobe Color + warm portrait base |
| Color +1 | — | Saturation +5 to +8 |
| Highlight +1 | — | Highlights modest |
| Shadow +1 | — | Shadows +10 to +20 |
| Grain: Weak | — | Amount 20-30, moderate |
| WB: 9100K (-7R, +7B) | — | Extreme warm; not applied per STYLEGUIDE |

Note: The 9100K WB is extremely warm — this is the "Kodak warmth" dialed to maximum for in-camera JPEGs. Pro Image 100 is warm but not *this* warm — the recipe targets a blend of looks.

## 3. Community Slider Ranges

### Basic Panel
- Exposure: +0.10 to +0.20 (slight overexposure sweet spot)
- Contrast: -5 to -10 (medium — portrait-friendly)
- Highlights: -35 to -45
- Shadows: +15 to +25
- Whites: -5 to +5
- Blacks: 0 to +10

### HSL — Portrait-Optimized Palette
- Orange Sat: 0 to +10 (skin tones — modest)
- Orange Lum: +5 to +15 (brighten skin)
- Red Sat: 0 to +5
- Yellow Sat: 0 to +5
- Green Sat: -5 to -15
- Blue Sat: -5 to -15
- Magenta Sat: -10 to -20
- Green Hue: +5 to +15 (warm greens)

### Color Grading
- Shadows: H40-50, Sat 5-10 (warm, not teal)
- Highlights: H35-45, Sat 5-10 (golden)
- Midtones: H0, Sat 0 (protect skin)
- Blending: 50

### Grain
- Amount: 20-30 (fine, ISO 100)
- Size: 20-25
- Roughness: 40-50

## 4. Common Mistakes

1. **Too much contrast** — this is portrait film, not slide film
2. **Aggressive split toning** — Pro Image is warm overall, not teal/orange
3. **Crushed blacks** — portrait films preserve shadow detail
4. **Strong saturation** — it's not Ultramax; saturation is moderate
5. **Cool shadows** — this film is warm everywhere
