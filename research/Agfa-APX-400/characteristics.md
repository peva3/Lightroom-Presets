# Agfa APX 400 — Film Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

## Overview
Agfa APX 400 was the high-speed member of Agfa's classic APX black-and-white film family. Sitting above APX 25 and APX 100, it offered a versatile combination of speed, moderate grain, and good contrast for general-purpose photography. Originally produced by Agfa-Gevaert (Leverkusen, Germany), it was later revived under AgfaPhoto branding by Lupus Imaging with a different emulsion. This document serves as a reference for creating a Lightroom preset that emulates APX 400.

## Key Characteristics
- **ISO**: 400 (nominal)
- **Grain**: Moderate — visible but controlled, more prominent than APX 100
- **Sharpness**: Good — retains detail well for a 400-speed film
- **Contrast**: Moderate — more punch than APX 100, less aggressive than Delta 400
- **Tonal Range**: Smooth midtones, slightly compressed shadows at box speed
- **Spectral Sensitivity**: Standard panchromatic — balanced across visible spectrum
- **Development**: Excellent in Rodinal (classic combination), D-76, XTOL; pushes well to 800 and 1600
- **Exposure Latitude**: Good — tolerates moderate over/underexposure

## Historical Context
- Original Agfa-Gevaert emulsion produced in Leverkusen, Germany
- Discontinued 2005 when AgfaPhoto went bankrupt
- Current "AgfaPhoto APX 400" (Lupus Imaging, post-2013) is reportedly Kentmere Pan 400 emulsion
- The original APX 400 was beloved for its balance of speed, grain, and price
- Ritchie Roesch (Fuji X Weekly) created a Fujifilm Acros-based recipe approximating the APX 400 look

## Visual Signature
- Moderate, organic grain with classic cubic-grain structure
- Good shadow detail without being muddy
- Pleasing skin tone rendering for a 400-speed film
- More "vintage" character than modern T-grain 400-speed films
- Classic European B&W aesthetic — natural, unforced, honest
- Works well for street photography, documentary work, and pushed portraiture
