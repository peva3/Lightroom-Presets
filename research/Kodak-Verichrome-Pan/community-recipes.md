# Kodak Verichrome Pan — Community Recipes

## Community Notes (from forums)
- No dedicated Fuji X Weekly or t3mujinpack recipe exists for Verichrome Pan
- The film was designed for box cameras with approximately f/11 fixed aperture and ~1/50s shutter
- Typical exposure was for bright sun only — the film's wide latitude handled everything else
- Near-orthopanchromatic sensitivity means reds render darker than modern films, greens slightly brighter
- The film was very forgiving of development errors — typical of consumer films
- Many found-photo archives (e.g., "Vintage Photos" subreddits) consist largely of Verichrome Pan negatives
- The "old family photo" aesthetic is partly the film, partly the simple camera optics, partly age

## Derived from Box Camera Characteristics
- Box cameras had approximately f/11-f/16 fixed apertures
- Single-element meniscus lenses with significant softness at edges
- The film's latitude compensated for the primitive cameras
- "Sunny 16 rule" was essentially the camera's metering system
- Negatives were contact-printed (no enlargement), so grain was less critical

## LR Preset Design Notes
- Near-orthopanchromatic sensitivity: GrayMixer Red -5 (slightly darkens skin naturally), Blue -20
- Moderate-low contrast (+18) — the forgiving, unassuming character
- Fine but visible grain: Amount=20, Size=20, Roughness=45
- Gentle blacks (-10) — nothing too dramatic
- Slight highlight reduction (-15) for the compressed consumer-film highlight range
- Modest shadow lift (+12) to preserve the "nothing is lost" look
- All STYLEGUIDE rules: Sharpness=10 with grain, Clarity/Texture/Dehaze=0, no Calibration, no WB
- The slightly darker skin tones (negative Red in GrayMixer) are the defining characteristic
