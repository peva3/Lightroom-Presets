# Kodak Verichrome Pan — Film Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

## Overview
Kodak Verichrome Pan was a medium-speed black-and-white negative film designed for simple box cameras and consumer use. Introduced in 1956 (updating the earlier orthochromatic Verichrome), it was known for extremely wide exposure latitude — it could produce usable negatives with exposure errors of several stops. Discontinued 2002, it was the last of Kodak's classic-era B&W films. This document serves as a reference for creating a Lightroom preset that emulates Verichrome Pan.

## Key Characteristics
- **ISO**: 125 (nominal)
- **Grain**: Moderate — visible but not prominent, typical of 1950s consumer emulsions
- **Sharpness**: Moderate — adequate for contact prints and modest enlargements
- **Contrast**: Low to moderate — designed to produce printable negatives from simple cameras with fixed exposure
- **Tonal Range**: Smooth midrange, slightly compressed highlights and shadows
- **Spectral Sensitivity**: Near-orthopanchromatic — reduced red sensitivity (deliberately, to make skin tones look natural under the simple optics of box cameras)
- **Exposure Latitude**: Legendary — usable results from 3 stops under to 5 stops over
- **Development**: Forgiving in any standard developer

## Historical Context
- The film inside millions of Brownie box cameras
- "Verichrome" name derives from "veri" (true) + "chrome" (color) — marketing claim of accurate tonal reproduction
- Discontinued 2002 after 46 years of production
- The definitive family snapshot film of mid-20th century America
- Many found-photo collections consist largely of Verichrome Pan negatives

## Visual Signature
- Gentle, forgiving tonality — nothing is ever truly blown or blocked
- Slightly soft, nostalgic rendering (partly due to box camera optics, partly the film)
- Skin tones render naturally with slight darkening (reduced red sensitivity)
- Blue skies render lighter than modern pan films (also reduced red = relatively brighter blue)
- Overall "gentle" look that forgives technical imperfections
- The quintessential "old family photo" aesthetic
