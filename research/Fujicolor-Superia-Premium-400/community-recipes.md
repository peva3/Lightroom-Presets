### FxW Recipe → Lightroom Translation

Superia Premium 400 was the Japan-exclusive refined Superia — finer grain, better skin tones, less aggressive than X-TRA 400. Translation priorities: neutral-cool Fuji balance, fine grain, clean skin tones, moderate contrast.

- **Exposure**: +0.15 (slight brightness for consumer baseline)

- **Highlights**: -25 (controlled highlight recovery)
- **Shadows**: +15 (open premium shadow detail)

- **Blacks**: -10 (gentle black — premium films don't crush)

## Community Consensus

Superia Premium 400 is well-known among Japanese-market film shooters:
1. **"Japan-only gem"**: Never officially exported, creating mystique
2. **Better than X-TRA 400**: Finer grain, better skin tones, more refined color
3. **The "JP domestic" look**: Japanese-market films traditionally have cooler, cleaner rendering for Asian skin tones
4. **Grain comparison**: Noticeably finer than standard Superia 400
5. **Discontinued legacy**: Like all Superia variants, now discontinued, adding to collectibility
