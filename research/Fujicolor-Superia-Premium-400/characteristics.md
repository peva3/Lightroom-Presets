# Fujicolor Superia Premium 400 — Aesthetic Characteristics

## Overview

Fujicolor Superia Premium 400 was a Japan-exclusive variant of Fujifilm's Superia 400 line, manufactured specifically for the domestic Japanese market. While standard Superia X-TRA 400 was sold globally, the "Premium" designation indicated higher-quality emulsion technology, finer grain, and improved color reproduction. It was positioned as a step above consumer Superia but below the professional Pro 400H line. Discontinued along with most Fuji consumer films.

## Core Aesthetic Signature

### Color Palette

- **Primary Color Cast**: Neutral-cool with Fuji's characteristic slight cyan bias. More refined than standard Superia, with better color separation.
- **Reds**: Clean, slightly cool reds — less orange than Kodak equivalents. Good saturation without bleeding.
- **Yellows & Oranges**: Subdued compared to Kodak. Skin tones are neutral rather than golden — more flattering for Asian skin tones (the domestic Japanese market consideration).
- **Greens**: Excellent green response with Fuji's 4th color layer. Foliage is lush and well-separated with slight blue-green bias.
- **Blues**: Very clean, slightly vivid. Skies render beautifully with subtle cyan depth.
- **Skin Tones**: The standout feature. "Premium" meant better skin tone rendering — neutral, clean, without the yellow cast of consumer Kodak or the green cast of standard Superia.

### Contrast & Tonality

- **Overall Contrast**: Medium. Less aggressive than standard Superia 400 — the "premium" designation meant more controlled contrast.
- **Highlight Roll-off**: Controlled and smooth. Better highlight latitude than consumer-grade 400 films.
- **Shadow Detail**: Good. The premium emulsion retained shadow separation better than standard consumer films.
- **Midtone Contrast**: Moderate — images feel refined rather than punchy.

### Grain

- **Grain Level**: Fine for an ISO 400 film. The "Premium" formulation's primary advantage over standard Superia was reduced grain.
- **Grain Character**: Fine, regular structure. Similar to Kodak Portra 400 in grain visibility.
- **Comparison**: Finer than Superia X-TRA 400, approaching Fuji Pro 400H grain levels.

### Exposure Behavior

- **Box Speed (ISO 400)**: Refined, clean color rendering with fine grain.
- **Overexposure (+1 stop, EI 200)**: Even finer grain, slightly pastel shifts in highlights, excellent shadow detail.
- **Underexposure (-1 stop)**: Grain increases but remains controlled. Color shifts toward cool/green.

## Summary

```
Neutral-cool Fuji color balance with refined rendering
Fine grain — the "premium" differentiator from standard Superia
Clean skin tones optimized for Japanese domestic market
Excellent green/blue separation via 4th color layer
The bridge between consumer Superia and professional Pro 400H
```
