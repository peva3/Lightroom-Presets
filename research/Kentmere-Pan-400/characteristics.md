# Kentmere Pan 400 — Film Characteristics

## Overview
Kentmere Pan 400 is a budget black-and-white negative film produced by Harman Technology (parent company of Ilford). Introduced around 2009, it is marketed toward photography students and budget-conscious shooters. "Pan" was added to the name a few years ago. The emulsion is understood to be related to Ilford HP5+ but with less silver content, making it more affordable.

## Key Characteristics
- **ISO**: 400 (nominal)
- **Grain**: Pronounced, characteristic — more visible than HP5+ due to lower silver content
- **Sharpness**: Moderate; less resolving power than premium films
- **Contrast**: Moderate — similar to HP5+ in general character
- **Tonal Range**: Good midrange, slightly less density in shadows
- **Silver Content**: Reduced compared to HP5+ — this is how Harman achieves the lower price
- **Development**: Behaves well in standard developers (D-76, ID-11, Rodinal)

## Historical Context
- Introduced 2009 by Harman Technology
- Kentmere originally made photographic paper before Harman's acquisition
- Positioned as the entry-level/student B&W film
- Related to AgfaPhoto APX 400 (2013+) and possibly Rollei RPX 400 — these may share the same or similar emulsions
- Ilford also makes a "Pan 400" film, adding to the confusion about Kentmere's exact provenance

## Visual Signature
- Noticeable grain with organic character — less "clean" than premium films
- Slightly lighter/more open shadows than HP5+
- Good general-purpose B&W look without the premium price
- Grain is part of the aesthetic — this isn't a "clean" film
- Works well for street photography, documentary work, and learning exposure/development
