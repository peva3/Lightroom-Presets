# Kentmere Pan 400 — Community Recipes

## Primary Source: Fuji X Weekly (Dan Allen, March 2024)

Dan Allen developed this recipe by shooting Kentmere Pan 400 side-by-side with his Fujifilm X-Pro3. Using X RAW Studio, he matched the digital files to the Kentmere scans.

### Recipe
- **Film Simulation**: Acros+G
- **Monochromatic Color (Toning)**: Off (WC 0 & MG 0)
- **Grain Effect**: Strong, Large
- **Color Chrome Effect**: Off
- **Color Chrome FX Blue**: Off
- **White Balance**: Auto, +7 Red & +6 Blue
- **Dynamic Range**: DR-Auto
- **Highlight**: +1
- **Shadow**: +1
- **Sharpness**: +3
- **High ISO NR**: -4
- **Clarity**: +1
- **ISO**: up to ISO 12800
- **Exposure Compensation**: 0 to +2/3 (typically)

Source: https://fujixweekly.com/2024/03/15/

### Compatibility
- X-Trans IV (except X-T3/X-T30) and all X-Trans V cameras
- Also usable on newer GFX models (may render slightly differently)

### Community Notes
- Named "Pan" to distinguish it, though the emulsion may not have changed from the original Kentmere 400
- Some forums report it pushes well to 800 and 1600 with additional grain and contrast
- The budget nature means more grain — this is part of the film's charm
- Related to HP5+ family but with less silver = more visible grain structure

### LR Preset Design Notes
- Pronounced grain is the defining characteristic: GrainAmount=50 (Strong in Fuji terms), Size=40 (Large)
- Less contrast than premium 400-speed films — modest +20 Contrast
- Modest Highlight/Shadow balance reflects student-film character
- GrayMixer slightly reduces yellow (for natural foliage) and greens (depth)
- All STYLEGUIDE rules: Sharpness=10, Clarity/Texture/Dehaze=0, no Calibration, no WB
- The grain structure carries this preset — it's the signature element that distinguishes Kentmere from cleaner films
