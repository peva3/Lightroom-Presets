# Kodak T-Max 100 — Community Recipes

## Primary Source: Fuji X Weekly (Dan Allen, October 2024)

Dan Allen shot Kodak T-Max 100 film side-by-side with a Fujifilm X-T5 to create these recipes. He found significant frame-to-frame variations in the film and created two variants.

### Hard Tone Variant
- **Film Simulation**: Monochrome+G
- **Grain Effect**: Weak, Large
- **Color Chrome Effect**: Off
- **Color Chrome FX Blue**: Off
- **White Balance**: Daylight, -6 Red & -3 Blue
- **Dynamic Range**: DR200
- **Highlight**: +0.5
- **Shadow**: +2.5
- **Sharpness**: -1
- **High ISO NR**: -4
- **Clarity**: -1
- **ISO**: up to 6400
- **Exposure Compensation**: -2/3 to +2/3

Source: https://fujixweekly.com/2024/10/04/

### Soft Tone Variant
- **Film Simulation**: Monochrome+G
- **Grain Effect**: Weak, Large
- **Color Chrome Effect**: Off
- **Color Chrome FX Blue**: Off
- **White Balance**: Daylight, -6 Red & -3 Blue
- **Dynamic Range**: DR200
- **Highlight**: -0.5
- **Shadow**: +1.5
- **Sharpness**: -1
- **High ISO NR**: -4
- **Clarity**: -1
- **ISO**: up to 6400
- **Exposure Compensation**: -2/3 to +2/3

Source: https://fujixweekly.com/2024/10/07/

### Community Recommendation (theBitterFig on X-Pro3)
Split the difference for cameras without half-steps: Highlight 0, Shadow +2.

## LR Preset Design Notes
- Hard Tone has brighter highlights and deeper shadows (more contrast)
- Soft Tone has softer highlights and lifted shadows (less contrast)
- Our XMP uses Hard Tone as primary reference with moderate contrast balance
- T-Max 100 grain is very fine — "Weak" grain effect in Fuji terms
- Sharpness reduced to -1 in Fuji recipe to avoid digital edge enhancement on grain
- Our XMP uses Sharpness=10 per STYLEGUIDE grain+sharpening rule
- Our XMP zeros Clarity/Texture/Dehaze and caps grain gently for the 100-speed character
