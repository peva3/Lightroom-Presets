# Film Soup Lightroom Preset — Complete Aesthetic Breakdown

## Core Aesthetic Signature

The film soup Lightroom preset recreates this look digitally using Lightroom's color grading tools for authentic film emulation. Film souping is the practice of deliberately damaging unprocessed film by soaking it in various liquids (lemon juice, wine, coffee, soda, detergent, boiling water) before development. The process creates unpredictable color shifts, emulsion damage, texture artifacts, and chemical staining. Different liquids produce different effects: acidic liquids eat the emulsion (creating texture), colored liquids stain the film base (creating color casts), heat accelerates chemical reactions (creating intensified artifacts).

## Historical Context

**Era:** 2010s–present (Instagram/Tumblr experimental photography)

Film canister/bulk roll → soaked in chosen liquid (hours to days) → dried → processed normally → the liquid partially eats the emulsion, stains the base, or alters development kinetics. Results are wildly unpredictable. Common soups: lemon juice (acid damage + yellow cast), red wine (purple stain + texture), coffee/tea (brown stain), detergent (rainbow artifacts), boiling (extreme grain + contrast).

## Visual Characteristics

Unpredictable color shifts — rainbow artifacts, color casts in random image regions. Visible emulsion texture — cracks, bubbles, uneven development. Chemical staining — yellow, purple, brown patches. Altered grain structure — clumped, irregular grain. Dark or light streaks where liquid pooled during drying. No two souped rolls look the same.

## Emotional & Artistic Vocabulary

Experimental, anarchic, punk. Film souping is the ultimate rejection of photographic purity — the chemistry IS the aesthetic. Each image is a collaboration between photographer, chemistry, and chance.

## Technical Notes for Digital Emulation

This preset is designed to capture the essential visual character of the film soup process using Lightroom's slider-based toolset. It prioritizes the emotional/aesthetic impact over perfect chemical accuracy, which would require a 3D LUT or DCP camera profile.

### Limitations of Slider-Based Simulation
- True process-specific spectral sensitivity cannot be replicated with sliders
- Chemical stain patterns are simulated through grain and color grading
- Paper/plate texture is approximated through grain settings
