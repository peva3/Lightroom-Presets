# Kodak Portra 160NC — Full Aesthetic Characteristics

> "Natural Color" — the lower-contrast, more neutral variant of Portra 160. The choice for accuracy-first commercial and fine art photographers.

---

## Executive Summary

Kodak Portra 160NC was the "Natural Color" sibling to Portra 160VC ("Vivid Color"), both discontinued when Kodak consolidated into a single "Portra 160" in 2010. 160NC was prized for its **exceptionally neutral color rendering, lower contrast, and softer tonality** — ideal for commercial product photography, fine art reproduction, and portrait work where absolute color accuracy mattered more than "pop."

---

## 1. The Color Palette

| Color Region | Portra 160NC Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Skin Tones** | Very neutral, accurate. Less warm than standard Portra 160. |
| **Whites / Highlights** | Clean, neutral. No warm cream cast. |
| **Greens** | Natural, accurate. Less yellow shift than standard Portra. |
| **Blues** | Accurate, slightly muted. Sky tones are natural cerulean. |
| **Reds** | Muted, accurate. Not punchy or saturated. |
| **Shadows** | Open, neutral. Less warmth than standard Portra. |
| **Overall** | Lower saturation + lower contrast = flatter, more neutral starting point. |

---

## 2. Contrast & Tonality

- **Very low contrast** — the lowest in the Portra line
- Scene-referred tonal accuracy — designed for accurate reproduction
- Very long tonal scale — shadows open, highlights compressed gracefully
- Ideal for scanning and digital post-production (flat neg = more latitude)

---

## 3. Grain Structure

- **RMS ~4** — exceptionally fine grain (finer than 400-speed films)
- Essentially invisible in 120 format
- Even, tight grain structure

---

## 4. Comparison: Portra 160NC vs Portra Family

| Aspect | 160NC | 160 (current) | 160VC | 400NC |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Contrast** | Lowest | Low | Moderate | Low |
| **Saturation** | Lowest | Low-Moderate | Higher | Low |
| **Color bias** | Neutral | Warm | Warm-saturated | Neutral |
| **Skin** | Most accurate | Warm-flattering | Warm-vibrant | Accurate |
| **Best for** | Commercial, reproduction | General | Fashion, vivid | Low-light accurate |

---

## 5. Digital Emulation Targets

1. Lower contrast than standard Portra 160 (Contrast -20 vs -15)
2. Near-zero saturation bias — desaturated from standard
3. Neutral color balance — no warm cast
4. Lifted blacks for open shadow detail
5. Very fine grain (Amount 18, Size 20)
6. Subtle cool shadow tendency (less warm than standard)
