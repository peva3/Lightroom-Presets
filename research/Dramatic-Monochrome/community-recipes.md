# Dramatic Monochrome — Community Recipes

## Primary Source: Fuji X Weekly (Ritchie Roesch, February 2020)

Ritchie Roesch created this recipe after studying the iconic photographs of great 1930s-1950s photographers. He was drawn to their high-contrast aesthetic and set out to replicate it in-camera.

### Recipe
- **Film Simulation**: Monochrome (+Y, +R, +G)
- **Dynamic Range**: DR200
- **Highlight**: +4
- **Shadow**: +4
- **Grain**: Strong
- **Color Chrome Effect**: Strong
- **Toning**: 0
- **Sharpening**: +3
- **Noise Reduction**: -4
- **ISO**: Auto up to ISO 12800
- **Exposure Compensation**: 0 to +2/3 (typically)

Source: https://fujixweekly.com/2020/02/11/

### Compatibility Notes
- X-Trans III cameras (no Color Chrome Effect): similar but slightly less dramatic results
- X-Trans III: use Sharpening +2 instead of +3
- X-Trans IV: add optional +1 toning for subtle sepia (like a quick darkroom sepia bath)
- Ritchie specifically chose the Monochrome film simulation over Acros — the "old-fashioned" Monochrome has a grittier character

### LR Preset Design Notes
- Extreme contrast (+40) — the defining characteristic of this preset
- Strong grain (GrainAmount=55, Size=40, Roughness=70) for vintage "push-processed" look
- Deep blacks (-30) and pushed whites (+25) create the dramatic tonal range
- Aggressive blue suppression (-45) for dramatic, dark skies
- All STYLEGUIDE rules: Sharpness=10, Clarity/Texture/Dehaze=0, no Calibration, no WB
- This preset is intentionally aggressive — it's designed to be dramatic, not subtle
- Best used on images with strong compositional structure and deliberate light/shadow
