# Foma Fomapan 200 — Film Characteristics

## Overview
Fomapan 200 (also sold as Fomapan 200 Creative) is a unique medium-speed black-and-white film from Foma Bohemia. Unlike Fomapan 100 and 400 which use traditional cubic-grain emulsions, Fomapan 200 utilizes a hybrid T-grain/cubic emulsion — a distinctive approach that combines the sharpness of T-grain with the organic character of cubic grain.

## Key Characteristics
- **ISO**: 200 (nominal)
- **Grain**: Moderate-fine — hybrid grain structure produces finer grain than expected for 200-speed
- **Sharpness**: Good — T-grain component provides excellent edge acutance
- **Contrast**: Moderate — similar to Fomapan 100, slightly more controlled highlights
- **Tonal Range**: Good separation across all zones, slightly smoother midtones than Fomapan 100
- **Spectral Sensitivity**: Traditional panchromatic with slightly extended blue sensitivity
- **Development**: Behaves differently from Fomapan 100/400 — the hybrid emulsion responds uniquely to developers
- **Exposure Latitude**: Good — handles overexposure better than Fomapan 100

## Historical Context
- Foma's answer to Kodak's T-Max and Ilford's Delta emulsions
- The hybrid emulsion was Foma's innovation — a different path to modern grain structure
- Not as widely distributed as Fomapan 100/400 but has a dedicated following
- Some batches have been noted for quality control variations (emulsion defects)
- When good: a unique film that bridges traditional and modern B&W

## Visual Signature
- Finer grain than expected for ISO 200 — the T-grain component delivers
- More organic than pure T-grain films (Ilford Delta, Kodak T-Max) — the cubic component adds character
- Slightly cooler tonality than other Fomapan films (extended blue sensitivity)
- Good highlight control with smooth rolloff
- A "best of both worlds" aesthetic: modern sharpness with traditional grain character
- Less dramatic than Fomapan 100 in Rodinal, more controlled and refined
