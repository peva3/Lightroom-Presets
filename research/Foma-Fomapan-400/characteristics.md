# Foma Fomapan 400 — Film Characteristics

## Overview
Fomapan 400 is the high-speed member of Foma Bohemia's classic black-and-white film family. Using a traditional cubic-grain emulsion, it delivers the classic 400-speed look with pronounced grain, good contrast, and the distinctive Eastern European character that defines the Fomapan line. One of the most affordable 400-speed films available.

## Key Characteristics
- **ISO**: 400 (nominal)
- **Grain**: Pronounced — classic cubic grain, more visible than modern T-grain 400 films
- **Sharpness**: Moderate — grain structure competes with fine detail
- **Contrast**: Moderate-high — punchier than most 400-speed films at box speed
- **Tonal Range**: Good midtones; deeper blacks, slightly compressed highlights
- **Spectral Sensitivity**: Traditional panchromatic — slightly reduced red sensitivity
- **Development**: Highly responsive to developer choice — Rodinal for dramatic grain, XTOL for control, D-76 for balance
- **Exposure Latitude**: Good at box speed; underexposure produces heavy grain and contrast increase

## Historical Context
- Produced continuously by Foma Bohemia since the Cold War era
- Originally the "fast film" for Eastern Bloc photographers
- Became globally popular in the 1990s after the Czech Republic opened to Western markets
- Known for its affordability — often half the price of Kodak/Ilford 400-speed films
- Popular with students, experimental photographers, and budget-conscious professionals
- Quality control has improved significantly over the decades

## Visual Signature
- Pronounced, characterful grain — this is not a "clean" film
- Deep, dramatic blacks with good shadow separation
- In Rodinal: massive grain, extreme acutance, very dramatic — the "poor man's pushed Tri-X"
- In XTOL: controlled, more modern, still with visible grain character
- Slightly darker skies than Western 400 films (reduced red sensitivity)
- A bold, honest, unpretentious look — what you see is what you get
- At EI 800-1600: becomes very grainy and contrasty, useful for dramatic effect
