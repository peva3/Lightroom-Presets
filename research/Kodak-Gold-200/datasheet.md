# Kodak Gold 200 — Technical Datasheet

> **Note:** Kodak Gold 200 is a consumer-grade film and has less publicly available technical documentation than Kodak's professional line (Portra, Ektar). The following is compiled from Kodak Alaris product pages, retail descriptions, and lab documentation.

---

## Basic Specifications

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Film Type** | Color Negative (C-41 Process) |
| **ISO Speed** | 200 (medium-speed, daylight-balanced) |
| **Formats** | 35mm (36 exp, 24 exp), 120 (introduced April 2022) |
| **Process** | C-41 standard color negative |
| **White Balance** | Daylight (5500K) |
| **RMS Granularity** | Not publicly disclosed (estimated similar to Ultramax 400, slightly finer) |
| **Resolving Power** | Not publicly disclosed for consumer line |

---

## Exposure Characteristics

### Exposure Latitude
- **-2 stops underexposure** to **+3 stops overexposure**
- Excellent highlight retention — holds detail in skies and bright areas
- Shadow detail preserved well even in high-contrast scenes
- Very forgiving; widely recommended as a beginner film

### Recommended Use
- **Daylight** — primary design target; warm, saturated results
- **Electronic Flash** — performs well with on-camera flash
- **Tungsten (3200K)** — usable with 80A blue filter
- **Photo lamps (3400K)** — usable with 80A blue filter

### Lighting Performance
- Loves light — thrives in bright, sunny conditions
- Overcast conditions: still usable; warmth compensates for flat light
- Golden hour: exceptionally beautiful; warm cast amplified
- Underexposure: increased grain, muddy shadows, loss of saturation
- Overexposure: pastel-leaning tones, reduced contrast (still pleasing, often deliberately used by community for "dreamy" look)

## Color Characteristics (Official)

Per Kodak Alaris / Film Photography Project product description:

- **Saturated colors** — vibrant primaries, especially reds and yellows
- **Bright, colorful prints** — designed for consumer minilab printing
- **Fine grain** and **high sharpness** for its class
- Great for enlargements
- High-quality results when scanned for digital output
- "Outstanding combination of color saturation, fine grain, and high sharpness"

## Physical Properties

| Property | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Base** | Acetate (35mm), dimensions per ISO 1007 |
| **Anti-halation** | Remjet-free (standard C-41) |
| **Spectral Sensitivity** | Panchromatic with red emphasis (characteristic Kodak warm bias) |
| **Shelf Life** | Consumer-grade stability; refrigerate for long-term storage |
| **Push/Pull** | Not officially rated; community pushes to +1 (EI 400) with acceptable results |
| **Reciprocity** | Not officially published; community recommends +1 stop for exposures >1 sec |

## Lineage & Positioning

Gold 200 sits in Kodak's consumer color negative lineup:

```
Kodak ColorPlus 200  →  Kodak Gold 200  →  Kodak Ultramax 400
(entry-level)           (mid consumer)     (high-speed consumer)

                         ↓ (step up to pro line)
                    Kodak Portra 160 / 400
                    Kodak Ektar 100
```

- **Gold 200 vs ColorPlus 200**: Gold has higher saturation, slightly finer grain, and a richer "golden" signature. ColorPlus is more muted/washed.
- **Gold 200 vs Ultramax 400**: Gold is warmer, more saturated, less grainy; Ultramax is cooler, punchier, grainier.
- **Gold 200 vs Portra 160**: Portra is finer grain, more neutral/flat (designed for scanning & post work), softer contrast. Gold has more "character" — warmer, more saturated straight out of the camera.

## Official Kodak Features Summary

From product packaging and retail copy:

1. Medium-speed (ISO 200) daylight color negative film
2. Process C-41
3. Saturated color reproduction
4. Fine grain and high sharpness
5. Wide exposure latitude (2 under, 3 over)
6. Designed for general outdoor and flash photography
7. Excellent for scanning and digital workflows
