# Cyanotype Tea-Toned Lightroom Preset — Complete Aesthetic Breakdown

## Core Aesthetic Signature

The cyanotype tea toned Lightroom preset recreates this look digitally using Lightroom's color grading tools for authentic film emulation. Tea-toned cyanotypes are classic cyanotypes that have been bleached and re-toned in tannic acid (tea). The process converts Prussian blue to a warm brown-black iron-tannate complex while retaining the characteristic cyanotype tonal scale. This was discovered in the 1840s and became popular among pictorialist photographers seeking warmer alternatives to the stark blue original.

## Historical Context

**Era:** 1840s–1900s

Prussian blue → bleached with sodium carbonate/ammonia → Fe³⁺ ions → toned in tannic acid (tea) → iron-tannate (brown-black). The degree of bleaching controls the warmth: partial bleaching yields blue-brown split tones; full bleaching yields pure brown.

## Visual Characteristics

Warm brown-black shadows transitioning to pale cream highlights with subtle blue undertones where bleaching was incomplete. The warm-cool tension between residual blue and introduced brown creates unique depth. Matte surface, slightly softer contrast than classic cyanotype.

## Emotional & Artistic Vocabulary

Nostalgic, antique, warm-scientific. Combines the documentary precision of cyanotype with the warmth of 19th-century albumen and salt prints. Feels like a Victorian naturalist's field journal.

## Technical Notes for Digital Emulation

This preset is designed to capture the essential visual character of the cyanotype tea-toned process using Lightroom's slider-based toolset. It prioritizes the emotional/aesthetic impact over perfect chemical accuracy, which would require a 3D LUT or DCP camera profile.

### Limitations of Slider-Based Simulation
- True process-specific spectral sensitivity cannot be replicated with sliders
- Chemical stain patterns are simulated through grain and color grading
- Paper/plate texture is approximated through grain settings
