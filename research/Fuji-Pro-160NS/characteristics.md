# Fujicolor Pro 160NS — Full Aesthetic Characteristics

> The definitive breakdown of Fujifilm's pastel portrait film — cool-cyan shadows, low contrast, and the "pro wedding photographer" palette.

---

## Executive Summary

Fujicolor PRO 160NS (2010–2021) was Fujifilm's professional-grade 160-speed color negative film. Preceded by PRO 160S (2005–2010) and NPS 160 (1990s). It is a **pastel, low-contrast portrait film** with a distinctive **cool cyan-green shadow bias** and **neutral-cool skin tones**. Unlike Kodak Portra's warm-amber skin rendering, 160NS delivers clean, faithful color with a subtle cool undertone that wedding and editorial photographers prized for its timeless, airy look.

---

## 1. The Color Palette

| Color Region | Pro 160NS Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Whites / Highlights** | Clean, neutral white. No warm cream cast. Holds highlight detail gracefully. |
| **Skin Tones** | Neutral-cool with very subtle pink/magenta bias. Faithful, not "warm/golden." Flattering but honest. |
| **Greens** | Slightly cool-leaning, accurate. Not warm/yellow-green like Portra. Moderate saturation. |
| **Blues** | Natural, slightly muted. Sky tones go soft cerulean, never punchy or electric. |
| **Reds / Pinks** | Muted, natural. Pastel reds, never aggressive. |
| **Yellows** | Soft, butter-yellow, never saturated gold. |
| **Shadows** | The defining feature: **cool cyan-green cast in deep shadows**. Not muddy, not warm — a clean, editorial coolness. |
| **Neutral Grays** | Faithful neutral rendering — grays stay gray across the tonal range. |

### The Cool Cyan-Green Shadow Signature

This is the #1 identifying marker of Pro 160NS. Where Portra shadows trend warm amber and Fuji Pro 400H shadows go mint-green, 160NS shadows carry a **subtle cyan-green coolness** that gives images an airy, editorial quality. This originates from the 4th color layer's handling of the cyan-green spectral region — Fujifilm's proprietary technology for cleaner color separation.

---

## 2. Contrast & Tonality

- **Very low contrast** — lower than both Pro 400H and Portra 400
- **Smooth, continuous gradation** from shadow to highlight
- **Soft shoulder** — highlights roll off gradually without clipping
- **Gentle toe** — shadows are open, never blocked up
- Wide exposure latitude — rated at ISO 160 but can be overexposed 1–2 stops without color shift

---

## 3. Grain Structure

- **RMS granularity: ~3–4** — extremely fine for a color negative film
- Grain is virtually invisible in 120 format, subtle fine texture in 35mm
- Even, uniform grain structure with no clumping

---

## 4. Overexposure Behavior

| ISO Setting | Effect |
|---|---|
| **ISO 160** (box speed) | Neutral, balanced, moderate pastel quality. Clean. |
| **ISO 100** (+2/3 stop) | Sweet spot. Pastels bloom, contrast softens further, shadows lift into cool tones. |
| **ISO 80** (+1 stop) | Maximum pastel, very airy, ethereal. Highlights hold with no clipping. |

Unlike Portra 400 which goes golden-warm when overexposed, 160NS stays neutral-cool — the pastel bloom is clean, not warm-toned.

## 5. Comparison: Pro 160NS vs Key Films

| Aspect | Pro 160NS | Pro 400H | Portra 160 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **White balance** | Neutral-cool | Cool (magenta bias) | Neutral-warm |
| **Skin tones** | Neutral-cool, faithful | Cool pink/magenta | Warm golden |
| **Shadows** | Cool cyan-green | Mint-green | Warm brown |
| **Contrast** | Lowest | Low | Low-moderate |
| **Saturation** | Low-moderate | Low-moderate | Moderate |
| **Overexposure** | Clean pastel bloom | Mint pastel bloom | Warm golden wash |
| **Best for** | Portraits, editorial, fine art | Weddings, lifestyle | Universal |

## 6. Lighting Behavior

| Lighting Condition | Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Daylight (5500K)** | Native white point. Pastels render cleanly, skin looks natural. |
| **Overcast / Soft Light** | Ideal. Soft light + low contrast = maximum airy quality. Cool shadow cast is subtle. |
| **Golden Hour** | Warm tones get pulled toward neutral — no golden amber flood. Skin stays clean. |
| **Shade / Blue Hour** | Cool bias becomes more pronounced. Beautiful atmospheric tones. |
| **Studio Flash** | Clean whites, neutral skin. Excellent for controlled portrait work. |
| **Mixed Light** | 4th layer handles mixed sources well. Colors stay cohesive. |

## 7. Anders Lindborg's Push/Pull System (Fuji X Weekly, 2021)

Swedish photographer Anders Lindborg created 7 variants of a Pro 160NS recipe using PRO Neg. Hi, establishing a push/pull system for adapting to lighting conditions:

| Variant | HL | SH | Color | Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Pulled -2** | -2 | -1 | -1 | Brightest sun, harsh light |
| **Pulled -1** | -1 | -1 | 0 | Sunny, contrasty |
| **Box Speed** | 0 | 0 | 0 | Moderate sun, balanced |
| **Pushed +1** | +1 | 0 | 0 | Slight shadow, overcast |
| **Pushed +2** | +2 | 0 | +1 | Cloudy, soft light |
| **Pushed +3** | +2 | +1 | +2 | Heavy overcast, dull |
| **Pushed +4** | +3 | +2 | +3 | Deep shade, very flat light |

Base: PRO Neg. Hi, DR400, Grain Weak, WB Daylight 0/0, NR -4, Sharpness -3

## 8. Digital Emulation Targets

1. **Cool cyan-green shadow cast** (H 195° ±10, subtle saturation)
2. **Pastel highlight rolloff** (no hard clip, soft shoulder)
3. **Neutral-cool white balance** (no warm cast)
4. **Low overall contrast** — softer than Portra emulations
5. **Fine, subtle grain** (essentially invisible in hi-res)
6. **Greens shifted slightly toward cyan** (the 4th layer signature)
7. **Skin tones: neutral-cool, faithful, never orange/amber**
