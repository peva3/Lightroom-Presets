# Community Recipes — Steve McCurry

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/postprocessing
- "McCurry color" is a common editing request — warm, rich, documentary
- Kodachrome emulation is the foundation — several Reddit threads on replicating K64/K200 digitally
- Red saturation and warm split toning are the most cited sliders

### From YouTube Tutorials
- Pat Kay (200K+): Has specific tutorials on McCurry-style color grading
- Recommendations: warm highlights, slight blue shadow pull, red saturation boost
- Many tutorials reference the "Afghan Girl" red-green color contrast

### From Lightroom Preset Communities
- Several commercial preset packs include "McCurry" or "Kodachrome Portrait" presets
- Common HSL moves: Orange Luminance (skin), Red Saturation, Green desaturation
- Warm highlight split toning is universal across interpretations

### Community Consensus
- Documentary warmth, not cinematic orange/teal drama
- Red saturation is the defining color move (Kodachrome legacy)
- Skin tones must remain natural — this is a portrait preset first
- Global saturation should be subtle; the color comes from specific channels
