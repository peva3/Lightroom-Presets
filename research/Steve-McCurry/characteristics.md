# Steve McCurry

## Biography
- **Name**: Steve McCurry (born 1950)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Era**: 1970s–present

## Philosophy
- "Most of my images are grounded in people. I look for the unguarded moment, the essential soul peeking out."
- Documentary photography as human connection, not observation
- Drawn to color: "Color is a language. In the developing world, color is everywhere — it's life."
- Magnum Photos member
- Travels constantly; decades-long relationships with subjects

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: Nikon 35mm (FM2, F3), 105mm f/2.5 (his favorite portrait lens), later Nikon DSLR
- **Film**: Kodachrome 64 (his defining film), later E100VS, now digital
- **Color signature**: Warm, saturated but natural — Kodachrome's distinctive red/warm bias
- **Lighting**: Available light almost exclusively; window light, open shade, golden hour

## Visual Signature
- **Afghan Girl palette**: Warm skin tones against cool/neutral backgrounds. The red-green contrast.
- **Rich reds and earth tones**: Kodachrome's red rendering was legendary. Dust, fabric, skin — all warm.
- **Documentary but beautiful**: Not glamorized, but every frame is composed for aesthetic impact.
- **Shallow depth of field**: Subject isolation via wide apertures on the 105mm
- **Golden hour warmth**: Even in non-golden scenes, there's a warm undertone from Kodachrome's palette
- **Eyes**: Sharp, luminous, direct — the eyes are always the anchor

## Iconic Images
- *Afghan Girl* (1984) — National Geographic cover
- *Monsoon* series (India)
- *Dust Storm* (Rajasthan)

## Preset Design Notes
- Warm highlights via ColorGrade (not WB — avoids cascade issues)
- Red saturation boosted (Kodachrome's red signature)
- Green desaturated slightly (warms the overall palette)
- Slight exposure lift for luminous skin
- Orange luminance boost for creamy skin tones
- Documentary Blending at 50 — subtle, natural
