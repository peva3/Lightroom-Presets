# Diana F+ — Complete Aesthetic Breakdown

## Core Aesthetic Signature

The Diana camera, manufactured by the Great Wall Plastic Factory in Hong Kong in the 1960s, was a cheap plastic novelty camera sold as a giveaway. Rediscovered in the 2000s and reissued as the Diana F+ by Lomography in 2007. Like the Holga, it uses a plastic lens and produces soft, dreamy, vignetted images — but with a more ethereal, pastel character. The Diana shoots 6×6 cm squares but can be modified for 6×4.5 cm rectangles.

## Historical Context

**Era:** 1960s (original) | 2007–present (Diana F+ revival)



## Visual Characteristics

Square format. Uniformly soft focus across the frame (unlike Holga's center-sharpness). Gentle, graduated vignette. Slightly cool, pastel color palette. Less aggressive light leaks than Holga — Diana's body is somewhat better sealed. Dreamy, ethereal quality. Low-moderate contrast. The softness is the defining characteristic.

## Emotional & Artistic Vocabulary

Dreamy, ethereal, romantic. The Diana creates images that feel like half-remembered dreams. Softer and gentler than the Holga's punchy character.

## Technical Notes for Digital Emulation

This preset is designed to capture the essential visual character of the diana f+ process using Lightroom's slider-based toolset. It prioritizes the emotional/aesthetic impact over perfect chemical accuracy, which would require a 3D LUT or DCP camera profile.

### Limitations of Slider-Based Simulation
- True process-specific spectral sensitivity cannot be replicated with sliders
- Chemical stain patterns are simulated through grain and color grading
- Paper/plate texture is approximated through grain settings
