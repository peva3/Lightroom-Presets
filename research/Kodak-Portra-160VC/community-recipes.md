# Kodak Portra 160VC — Community Recipes

> Compiled from t3mujinpack .dtstyle reference data, Reddit (r/Lightroom, r/analog), and community forums. The VC (Vivid Color) variant was discontinued in 2010 but remains sought-after for its punchier wedding/fashion look.

---

## t3mujinpack .dtstyle Reference

The t3mujinpack collection contains a dedicated Portra 160VC preset in Darktable's .dtstyle format. Key characteristics extracted:

- **Purpose**: Pre-2010 vivid color Portra variant
- **Profile base**: Standard color matrix, no WB shift
- **Contrast**: Positive (unlike standard Portra which uses negative contrast)
- **Saturation**: Boosted globally with selective color channel adjustments
- **Grain**: Fine-grain structure matching ISO 160 RMS

## Community Consensus Values

### Contrast & Tone
- Contrast: Positive (+3 to +8) — the defining departure from standard Portra's negative contrast
- Blacks: +5 to +10 (moderately lifted, not crushed)
- Highlights: -40 to -50 (soft shoulder)
- Shadows: +20 to +30

### Color & Saturation
- Global Saturation: +5 to +10 (Vivid Color designation)
- Green Saturation: -15 (Portra green suppression)
- Blue Saturation: -10 (sky control)
- Orange Luminance: +10 to +15 (skin brightness)
- Red Saturation: +5 to +10 (warmth injection)

### Color Grading
- Highlights: Warm golden (Hue 40-50°, Sat 8-12)
- Shadows: Cool teal (Hue 200-220°, Sat 5-8)
- Blending: 50 (natural transition)

### Grain
- Amount: 18-25 (ISO 160 fine grain)
- Size: 20-25
- Roughness: 35-45

## Key Differentiator from Portra 160/NC

The community consistently identifies one core distinction: **contrast polarity**. Standard Portra and NC use negative contrast (-15 to -20) for soft tonal transitions. VC uses positive contrast (+3 to +8) for a punchier, more commercially viable look while retaining Portra's skin tone signature.
