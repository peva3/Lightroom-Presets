# Kodak Portra 160VC — Film Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

> Pre-2010 "Vivid Color" Portra variant. Higher saturation and contrast than standard Portra, less pastel. The bridal/fashion photographers' choice before the current unified Portra line.

---

## Core Aesthetic Identity

| Attribute | Portra 160VC Characteristic | This document serves as a reference for creating a Lightroom preset that emulates Portra 160VC.
|-----------|---------------------------|
| Contrast | Medium (higher than standard Portra 160) |
| Saturation | Elevated — "Vivid Color" designation |
| White balance | Neutral-warm (daylight balanced) |
| Skin tones | Warm, saturated, flattering — stronger than Portra 160 |
| Greens | Desaturated, shifted toward yellow-olive |
| Blues | Medium saturation — more present than standard Portra |
| Grain | Very fine (ISO 160, RMS ~6) |
| Highlight roll-off | Moderate shoulder — less forgiving than standard Portra |
| Shadow detail | Open but with more density — less lifted than NC |

## Historical Context

Portra 160VC was introduced in the late 1990s alongside Portra 160NC (Natural Color) as part of Kodak's segmented professional line. The "VC" variant was marketed for situations where more color saturation and contrast were desirable — weddings, fashion editorials, and commercial portrait work. The sharper contrast curve distinguished it from the flatter NC variant.

In 2010, Kodak unified the Portra line, incorporating the VC color science into what became the current Portra 400 (which inherited the warmth and saturation) and Portra 160 (which inherited the finer grain). The original 160VC was discontinued as a separate product.

## Key Color Signatures

1. **Skin tones**: Warm and saturated — more golden than Portra 160, less pastel. Not orange.
2. **Sky blues**: More present and saturated than standard Portra, but still natural. Not cyan.
3. **Greens**: Yellow-olive shift, moderately saturated. Not teal.
4. **Shadows**: Denser than Portra 160/NC. Hold more color saturation.
5. **Contrast**: Higher midtone contrast than standard Portra — images feel "punchy" without being harsh.
6. **Grain**: Virtually invisible at normal viewing sizes (ISO 160 fine-grain emulsion).

## Portra 160VC vs Portra 160NC

| Characteristic | 160VC (Vivid Color) | 160NC (Natural Color) |
|---------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Contrast | Medium-High | Low |
| Saturation | Elevated | Subdued |
| Skin tones | Warm, saturated | Neutral, accurate |
| Best use | Bridal, fashion, editorial | Portrait, wedding, studio |
| Shadow behavior | Denser, more color | Lifted, flatter |
| Grain | Very fine | Very fine |

## Relevance for Digital Emulation

Portra 160VC's look bridges the gap between Portra's gentle pastel character and the punchier commercial aesthetic that wedding photographers sought. In Lightroom, the key differentiators from standard Portra 160 are: positive contrast, higher saturation, warmer color grading, and slightly more aggressive highlight roll-off.
