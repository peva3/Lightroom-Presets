# Ilford Delta 400 — Film Characteristics

## Overview
Ilford Delta 400 is a medium-speed T-grain (tabular grain) black-and-white negative film. Part of Ilford's "Delta" professional line alongside Delta 100 and Delta 3200. It uses epitaxial core-shell crystal technology for high sharpness and fine grain at 400 speed.

## Key Characteristics
- **ISO**: 400 (nominal)
- **Grain**: Fine for a 400-speed film — comparable to Kodak T-Max 400
- **Sharpness**: Excellent resolving power due to T-grain technology
- **Contrast**: Moderate — can be adjusted significantly through development
- **Tonal Range**: Smooth midtones, good highlight/shadow separation
- **Development**: Responds well to Ilford DD-X, ID-11, and Microphen developers
- **Exposure Latitude**: Good — EI 200-800 with normal development

## Historical Context
- Part of Ilford's "Delta" professional series introduced in the 1990s
- Delta technology is Ilford's answer to Kodak's T-Max (T-grain) films
- Uses epitaxial core-shell crystals — silver halide crystals grown in controlled layers
- Positioned between the traditional-grain HP5+ and the ultra-fine Delta 100
- Still in production as of 2024 (Harman Technology/Ilford Photo)

## Visual Signature
- Clean, modern B&W look — less "vintage" than HP5+
- Fine grain structure; significantly cleaner than HP5+ at the same speed
- Neutral-contrast character that takes well to filtration and post-processing
- Less characterful grain than traditional films — some find it "too perfect"
- Excellent for scanning — the fine grain structure digitizes cleanly

## Comparison to HP5+ 400
- Delta 400 has noticeably finer grain (T-grain vs. traditional)
- HP5+ has more forgiving exposure latitude
- HP5+ has more "character" and traditional B&W feel
- Delta 400 is sharper with better resolving power
- Delta 400 has a slightly more "clinical" rendering
