# Ilford Delta 400 — Aesthetic Characteristics

## Overview

Ilford Delta 400 is a modern T-grain (epitaxial) medium-speed B&W film at ISO 400. It uses the same core-shell crystal technology as Delta 100 but optimized for higher speed — delivering noticeably finer grain than traditional HP5+ while maintaining good speed. It represents the "modern professional" B&W aesthetic.

## Grain

- **RMS Granularity ~11** — fine for a 400-speed film
- Epitaxial T-grain structure: more uniform, less clumpy than traditional films
- Noticeably finer grain than HP5+ at the same speed
- At 35mm: visible but fine texture at 8×10", smooth at 5×7"
- At 120: near-grainless at all print sizes

## Contrast & Tonal Characteristics

- **Moderate native contrast** (gamma ~0.58-0.63)
- More linear-than-classic tonal curve
- Good shadow detail — true ISO 400 speed
- Clean, smooth midtone transitions
- Excellent highlight handling

## Spectral Sensitivity

- Full panchromatic with slight extension into red
- Skin tones: luminous and clean
- Blue skies: moderately dark, good cloud separation
- Green foliage: rich and detailed
- Overall: slightly more modern, cleaner rendering than HP5+

## Delta 400 vs HP5+ (Key Differences)

| Property | Delta 400 | HP5+ |
|----------|-----------|------|
| Crystal tech | T-grain (epitaxial) | Traditional cubic |
| Grain | Finer, cleaner, more uniform | Coarser, more characterful |
| Sharpness | Higher | Moderate |
| Tonal curve | More linear | More classic S-curve |
| Push behavior | Good but grainier | Excellent — classic push look |
| Character | Modern, clean, clinical | Classic, forgiving, versatile |

## Developer Effects

| Developer | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| ID-11/D-76 1:1 | Reference standard |
| DD-X | Maximum sharpness, excellent grain |
| Microphen | Speed-enhancing, good push |
| HC-110 | Snappier contrast |

## Best Uses

- General-purpose professional photography
- Editorial and commercial work
- Portraiture (cleaner skin than HP5+)
- Travel photography
- Any situation where speed + fine grain are both needed
