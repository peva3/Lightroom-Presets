# Community Recipes — Alec Soth

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/largeformat
- Soth's work is discussed for its documentary approach more than its editing
- Large format photographers note the natural rendering — Portra in 8x10 doesn't need processing tricks
- "Honest color" is the consensus description — neither warm nor cool, just present

### From YouTube Tutorials
- Very limited — Soth's editing is too subtle for tutorial content
- Some creators reference his work when discussing "restrained editing" as a counterpoint to over-processed looks

### From Magnum/Photojournalism Resources
- Documentary photographers emphasize: don't impose a look on the world, let the world impose its look on you
- Soth's printing is noted as faithful to the scene — he matches the print to his memory of the place
- Large format Portra needs minimal adjustment — the film does the work

### Community Consensus
- The most restrained preset in any collection
- Slight desaturation of greens and blues for naturalism
- No contrast adjustments — large format has natural contrast
- The editing philosophy: "If you notice the preset, it's wrong"
