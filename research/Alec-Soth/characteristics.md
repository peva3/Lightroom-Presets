# Alec Soth

## Biography
- **Name**: Alec Soth (born 1969)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Era**: 1990s–present

## Philosophy
- "I'm interested in the line between documentary and poetry."
- Magnum Photos member (full member 2008)
- American road trip tradition: Robert Frank, Stephen Shore, Joel Sternfeld
- Photography as a way to connect with strangers — the picture as excuse for encounter
- Large format slowness as a way of being present

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: 8x10 large format (early work), 4x5, medium format (Mamiya 7), digital
- **Film**: Kodak Portra (color negative), various B&W
- **Process**: Slow, methodical — one subject per day sometimes
- **Printing**: Inkjet prints; deliberate, muted color palette

## Visual Signature
- **Honest, muted color**: No color grading tricks. The color is what was there, rendered faithfully and quietly.
- **Large format stillness**: The 8x10 perspective — wide but not distorted, everything in focus
- **American landscape as character**: The Mississippi River, small towns, industrial decay
- **Portrait + environment**: Subjects shown in their spaces — the room tells as much as the face
- **Muted winters**: Snow, bare trees, gray skies — the palette of the American Midwest
- **No drama**: No deep shadows, no blazing highlights. Even light. Even tone. The subject is enough.

## Iconic Images

- *Niagara* (2006)

## Preset Design Notes
- Very subtle adjustments overall — this is the most restrained color preset
- Slight warmth from shadow color grading, barely perceptible
- Green and blue saturation pulled back slightly for naturalism
- No contrast boost — large format renders naturally
- Documentary Blending at 50 — invisible transitions
- No clarity, no dehaze, no grain (large format is virtually grainless)
