# Ferrania Solaris FG 400 — Full Aesthetic Breakdown

## Overview

Ferrania Solaris FG 400 is an Italian consumer color negative film produced by Ferrania (a historic Italian film manufacturer owned by 3M for much of its history). The Solaris line (ISO 100, 200, 400) was produced in various versions from the 1980s through the early 2000s. FG 400 specifically was the late-1990s/early-2000s version.

The film is known for a very specific **Southern European / Mediterranean color signature**: warm tones, muted blues, orange-biased reds, and moderate contrast with low overall saturation. It was described as looking somewhat unusual for a European film — warmer than expected, with a slight cast.

---

## 1. Color Palette

| Channel | Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Reds** | Orange-biased, warm — not deep crimson |
| **Oranges** | Warm Mediterranean earth tones |
| **Yellows** | Warm, golden |
| **Greens** | Slightly muted, warm-biased |
| **Blues** | Distinctly muted — "muted blues" is a defining trait |
| **Magentas** | Suppressed |

### Color Temperature
- Daytime balanced (~5200K)
- Unusually warm for a European film (community notes)
- Slight warm cast overall — like shooting with a warming filter

---

## 2. Contrast Characteristics

- **Moderate-high contrast** for a color negative film (community consensus)
- Snappier than typical consumer films
- Shadows drop quickly (Fuji recipe: Shadow -2)
- Good highlight retention
- "A lot of contrast for a color negative film" — Ritchie Roesch

---

## 3. Grain Structure

- **Moderate consumer grain** — visible but not overwhelming
- Finer than Kodak consumer films of the era
- European emulsion character — slightly different from Kodak/Fuji grain
- ISO 400 grain is present but controlled

---

## 4. Low Saturation Character

This is a defining and somewhat unusual trait. Despite being a consumer color negative film (which typically boosts saturation), Solaris FG 400 has **notably low saturation**. Community descriptions consistently note:
- "low saturation"
- "muted blues"
- "not vibrant"
- The warmth comes from hue bias, not saturation boost

---

## 5. Fuji X Weekly Reference Recipe

**Source:** [Fuji X Weekly — Ferrania Solaris FG 400](https://fujixweekly.com/2021/11/05/fujifilm-x-e4-x-trans-iv-film-simulation-recipe-ferrania-solaris-fg-400/) (Ritchie Roesch)

An unintentional discovery — this recipe was originally a Portra 400 v2 variant with Eterna Bleach Bypass film simulation:
- Film Simulation: Eterna Bleach Bypass
- DR400; Highlight 0; Shadow -2; Color +2
- Grain: Strong, Small
- Color Chrome Effect: Strong; Color Chrome FX Blue: Weak
- WB: 5200K (+1 Red, -6 Blue)
- Sharpness -2; Clarity -2; NR -4
- Exposure Compensation: +1/3 to +1

Roesch notes the recipe is "a bit too warm compared to the film, more like shooting with a warming filter."

---

## 6. Distinction from Later Versions

The "Plus" version (Ferrania Solaris FG 400 Plus) had a significantly different look — more similar to Fuji Superia. This recipe targets the original FG 400, not the later Plus variant.

---

## 7. Summary

> **Ferrania Solaris FG 400 is a distinctive Italian consumer color negative film with unusually warm tones for a European emulsion, notably low saturation, muted blues, orange-biased reds, moderate-high contrast, and moderate consumer grain — the color signature of Mediterranean film photography.**
