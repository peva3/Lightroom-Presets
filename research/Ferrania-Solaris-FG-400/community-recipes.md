# Ferrania Solaris FG 400 — Community Lightroom Recipes

> **Compiled from:** Fuji X Weekly (Ritchie Roesch), r/analog, r/Lightroom, Photrio.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy

Solaris FG 400 emulation requires:
1. Warm Mediterranean color cast (unusual for European film)
2. Notably low saturation — the defining photographic trait
3. Muted blues specifically
4. Orange-biased reds
5. Moderate-high contrast
6. Moderate consumer grain

## 3. Community Slider Ranges

### Basic Panel
- Exposure: +0.05 to +0.15
- Contrast: +5 to +15 (moderate-high)
- Highlights: -20 to -30
- Shadows: -10 to -20 (drops quickly)
- Whites: +5 to +15
- Blacks: -5 to -15

### HSL — Mediterranean Low-Sat Palette
- Red Hue: +5 (orange bias)
- Red Sat: +5 to +15
- Orange Sat: 0 to +10
- Yellow Sat: 0 to +10
- Green Sat: -5 to -10
- Blue Sat: -10 to -20 (MUTED — defining trait)
- Aqua Sat: -5 to -15
- Magenta Sat: -10 to -20

### Color Grading
- Shadows: H35-45, Sat 8-12 (warm shadows — Mediterranean feel)
- Highlights: H25-35, Sat 5-10
- Blending: 50

### Grain
- Amount: 25-35
- Size: 25-30
- Roughness: 45-55

---

## 4. Common Mistakes

1. **Too much saturation** — this film is notably low-saturation
2. **Rich blues** — muted blues are the defining negative trait
3. **Cool shadows** — Solaris FG 400 has warm shadows
4. **Low contrast** — this is higher contrast than typical color neg
5. **Fuji-like magenta** — suppress magenta, this is Italian, not Japanese

---

## 5. Note on Variants

The Solaris FG 400 Plus (later version) looked different — more like Fuji Superia. This recipe targets the original FG 400. If your reference images look more "Fuji-like," you may be looking at the Plus variant.
