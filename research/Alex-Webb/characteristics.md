# Alex Webb

## Biography
- **Name**: Alex Webb (born 1952)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Era**: 1970s–present

## Philosophy
- "I'm always looking for that moment when the elements in the frame come together in some complex and interesting way."
- Layered compositions: foreground, midground, background all active simultaneously
- Photographing in complex, chaotic places (Haiti, Mexico, Istanbul, the US-Mexico border)
- Started in B&W, switched permanently to color after a trip to Haiti: "The light and color were so overpowering that B&W felt like a lie."
- Magnum Photos full member

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: Leica M, 35mm rangefinder
- **Film**: Kodachrome 64, later Ektachrome, now digital
- **Light**: "I learned to work in the harsh tropical light — shadows become graphic elements, not enemies."
- **Composition**: Multiple planes of action — waits for elements to align across the frame

## Visual Signature
- **Complex layered compositions**: 3+ distinct planes of action in a single frame — each could be its own photograph
- **Tropical warmth**: Kodachrome in equatorial light — warm, saturated, the humidity visible in the color
- **Deep, graphic shadows**: In harsh tropical sun, shadows go DEEP and geometric — they become compositional elements
- **Vibrant but not artificial**: Saturation comes from the place, not the processing. Red, orange, yellow are prominent.
- **Color as structure**: Color blocks organize the chaos — a red wall here, a blue door there, a yellow shirt in between
- **Fragmented narratives**: No single "subject" — the relationships BETWEEN subjects create the meaning

## Iconic Images
- *Hot Light/Half-Made Worlds* (1986)
- *The Suffering of Light* (2011 monograph)
- *La Calle* (Mexico series)

## Preset Design Notes
- Warm tropical palette via highlight and midtone color grading
- Deep shadows (Blacks -25) for graphic shadow geometry
- Red, orange, yellow boosted (tropical color spectrum)
- Green desaturated (controls the tropical foliage from overwhelming)
- Blue pulled back (tropical sky can be too dominant in color)
- Slight underexposure with shadow lift — the paradox of harsh tropical light: bright highlights AND deep shadows
- Blending at 75 for clean color separation
