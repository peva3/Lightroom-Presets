# Community Recipes — Alex Webb

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/streetphotography
- Webb's layered compositions are the most discussed element — color is secondary in many discussions
- "Hot Light/Half-Made Worlds" is the color reference — tropical, harsh, saturated
- The transition from B&W to color is studied as an artistic turning point

### From YouTube Tutorials
- "Tropical color grading" tutorials overlap with Webb's palette:
  - Deep, rich shadows
  - Warm saturated highlights
  - Film-like saturation in reds and earth tones
- Some street photography tutorials reference Webb's Kodachrome palette

### From Magnum Resources
- Webb's own writing about color is essential: "The color was so overpowering that B&W felt like a lie"
- He works in harsh equatorial light that most photographers avoid
- Kodachrome in tropical light produced his signature palette — the preset aims for this

### Community Consensus
- Deep shadows are as important as bright highlights — the contrast ratio is extreme
- Red/orange/yellow warmth defines the tropical palette
- Green must be controlled — tropical foliage can overwhelm
- The preset works best on images with strong light/shadow division and warm ambient light
