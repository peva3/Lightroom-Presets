# Agfa Optima 200 — Aesthetic Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

## The Film

Agfa Optima 200 was the faster variant of the Agfa Optima consumer color negative family. Produced from the mid-1990s through the mid-2000s, it offered photographers an extra stop of speed over the Optima 100 while maintaining the family's characteristic European color palette and golden warmth. This document serves as a reference for creating a Lightroom preset that emulates Optima 200.

## Core Aesthetic

### 1. Faster Optima, Same Soul
- **Golden warmth** — the same subtle golden midtone glow as Optima 100
- **Slightly more contrast** than Optima 100 — the faster emulsion had marginally more punch
- **More visible grain** — RMS ~10-11 vs Optima 100's ~9-10
- **Warmer saturation** — the ISO 200 emulsion had slightly warmer, slightly more saturated rendering than ISO 100

### 2. Color Palette by Hue

| Hue | Agfa Optima 200 Rendering |
|-----|---------------------------|
| **Blue (sky)** | Warm muted blue, slightly more present than Optima 100 |
| **Green (foliage)** | Warm yellow-green, similar to Optima 100 |
| **Red** | Slightly more saturated than Optima 100 |
| **Skin tones** | Warm golden, slightly more saturated — still flattering |
| **Yellow/Orange** | More pronounced golden warmth than Optima 100 |
| **Neutrals** | Warm-tinted, golden cast |

### 3. Contrast & Dynamic Range
- **Low-moderate contrast** — slightly more contrast than Optima 100
- **RMS ~10-11** — fine grain, slightly more visible than ISO 100 variant
- **Wide exposure latitude** — ±2+ stops, forgiving consumer film
- Good shadow detail for a consumer film

### 4. Differentiation from Optima 100
The key differences from its slower sibling:
- 15-20% more saturation
- Slightly warmer golden cast
- Marginally more contrast
- Finer grain structure expectations (but actually slightly coarser due to speed)
- Better performance in lower light / overcast conditions

## Comparison

| Attribute | Agfa Optima 100 | Agfa Optima 200 |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|
| Color balance | Golden warmth | Golden warmth (stronger) |
| Saturation | Restrained | Slightly more |
| Contrast | Low | Low-moderate |
| Grain | RMS ~9-10 | RMS ~10-11 |
| Warmth intensity | 7/10 | 8/10 |
