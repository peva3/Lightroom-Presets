# Ilford Ortho Plus 80 — Aesthetic Characteristics

## Overview

Ilford Ortho Plus 80 is a modern orthochromatic black-and-white negative film. Unlike panchromatic films (which are sensitive to all visible wavelengths), orthochromatic film is insensitive to red light — it only responds to blue, green, and yellow wavelengths. This gives it a distinctive early-20th-century aesthetic: dark skies, light foliage, and a haunting, vintage character. ISO 80 speed with fine grain. It can be handled under red safelight, making it popular for darkroom experimentation and alternative processes.

## Core Aesthetic Signature

### Spectral Sensitivity

- **Blue (400–480nm)**: Very high sensitivity. Blue skies render as bright — the opposite of panchromatic films. Skies appear white/light grey without filtration.
- **Green (500–550nm)**: High sensitivity. Foliage renders brightly — landscapes have an ethereal, luminous quality.
- **Yellow (560–590nm)**: Moderate sensitivity. Warm tones render medium grey.
- **Orange (590–620nm)**: Low sensitivity. Orange objects darken.
- **Red (620–700nm)**: Zero sensitivity. Red objects render as pure black — this is the defining orthochromatic characteristic. Red lipstick, roses, and stop signs appear black.

### Contrast & Tonality

- **Overall Contrast**: Medium. Ortho Plus has a well-behaved characteristic curve with good midtone separation.
- **Highlight Rendering**: Clean and controlled. The film handles highlights without excessive compression.
- **Shadow Rendering**: Good detail in shadows with a smooth toe. Less shadow contrast than panchromatic films.
- **Midtone Character**: Smooth, vintage — the ortho rendering creates a distinctive tonal palette.

### Grain

- **Grain Level**: Fine. ISO 80 with modern emulsion technology produces very fine grain.
- **Grain Character**: Fine, regular, tight structure. Not as ultra-fine as Delta 100 but very clean.
- **Comparison**: Finer than FP4+ 125, approaching Delta 100 levels.

### Practical Effects of Ortho Sensitivity

- **Portraits**: Skin renders differently — skin blemishes, freckles, and red areas appear darker. Lips appear dark (red insensitivity). Eyes appear more luminous. The result is a haunting, "Victorian" portrait look.
- **Landscapes**: Blue skies appear white/light grey. Green foliage is luminous and bright. Red flowers/objects appear black. The world looks fundamentally different from normal B&W.
- **Architecture**: Red brick buildings render very dark. Blue sky backgrounds wash out — subjects "pop" differently.

### Comparison to Panchromatic Films

| Feature | Ortho Plus | Panchromatic (FP4+) |
|---------|-----------|---------------------|
| Red sensitivity | None | Normal |
| Blue sky rendering | White/light grey | Medium to dark grey |
| Foliage | Luminous bright | Medium grey |
| Skin (Caucasian) | Darker, textural | Natural grey |
| Red objects | Pure black | Dark grey |
