# Palladium Print Lightroom Preset — Complete Aesthetic Breakdown

## Core Aesthetic Signature

The palladium print Lightroom preset recreates this look digitally using Lightroom's color grading tools for authentic film emulation. Palladium printing uses palladium salts instead of platinum, producing warmer, brown-black tones. Palladium became the primary metal after WWI when platinum was diverted to explosives manufacturing. Palladium prints have a distinctive warm, almost sepia-black tonality that distinguishes them from the more neutral platinum prints.

## Historical Context

**Era:** WWI–present

Same chemistry as platinum printing but using potassium tetrachloropalladite or sodium tetrachloropalladite. Palladium produces a warmer image tone than platinum. Often mixed with platinum in varying ratios to fine-tune warmth.

## Visual Characteristics

Warm brown-blacks rather than neutral greys. The deepest shadows have a rich, almost velvety warmth. Midtones are warmer than platinum but retain the same luminous quality. Highlights tend toward warm cream. The warmth is most pronounced in the shadows and becomes progressively more neutral in highlights.

## Emotional & Artistic Vocabulary

Rich, warm, painterly. Palladium prints feel more emotionally accessible than the cooler platinum prints. The warmth recalls 19th-century albumen while the tonal scale remains modern.

## Technical Notes for Digital Emulation

This preset is designed to capture the essential visual character of the palladium print process using Lightroom's slider-based toolset. It prioritizes the emotional/aesthetic impact over perfect chemical accuracy, which would require a 3D LUT or DCP camera profile.

### Limitations of Slider-Based Simulation
- True process-specific spectral sensitivity cannot be replicated with sliders
- Chemical stain patterns are simulated through grain and color grading
- Paper/plate texture is approximated through grain settings
