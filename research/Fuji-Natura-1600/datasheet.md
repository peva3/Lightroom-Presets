## Technical Specifications (from Nihon Camera magazine, Jan 2018 test)

Tested on Canon EOS-1V + EF 50mm f/1.4 USM at f/4, ±2 stop bracket. 51 color films compared.

| Metric | Score (out of 5) |
|---|---|
| **Grain** | ★ (1/5 — worst among tested C-41 films) |
| **Resolution** | ★★ (2/5) |
| **Latitude** | ★★★ (3/5 — none of the tested films achieved 4–5 stars) |
| **Vividness** | ★★★ (3/5) |

> Note: These scores reflect scientific measurement against slow, fine-grain films. In practice, the grain was considered *remarkably good for a 1600-speed film* — the finest-grain 1600 ISO color film ever made.

## Development

- **Process**: Standard C-41 (no special handling required)
- **Push processing**: Known to push well to 3200 with increased contrast and grain
- **Pull processing**: Can be shot at ISO 800 with normal development; retains excellent shadow detail
- **Exposure latitude**: Very wide for a high-speed film
  - Can be overexposed up to 5 stops (rated at EI 50) and still produce usable images
  - Recommended overexposure: 1/3 to 1 full stop (EI 800–1250) even at night for rich shadows
  - Daytime rating preference: EI 200–400 for pastel, clean look

## Physical Characteristics

- **Base**: Orange mask (standard Fuji C-41)
- **Cassette**: Fuji standard 35mm DX-coded (ISO 1600)
- **Edge markings**: "FUJI NATURA 1600" or "FUJI SUPERIA 1600"
- **Reciprocity failure**: No published data; community practice adds ~1 stop for exposures over 1 second, ~2 stops for exposures 30+ minutes

## Relationship to Other Films

| Film | Relationship |
|---|---|
| **Superia 1600** | Nearly identical emulsion; different branding for non-Japanese markets |
| **Superia Venus 800** | One stop slower; remained in Asia-Pacific production until ~2020s |
| **Superia 400** | Two stops slower; cooler-than-Portra palette, shares Fuji color DNA |
| **Portra 800** | Closest currently-available equivalent (one stop slower, warmer, cleaner) |
| **Lomo 800** | Rebranded Kodak stock; popular pushed-to-1600 alternative |
| **Cinestill 800T** | Tungsten-balanced; very different color palette |

## Legacy

- **Last true ISO 1600 color negative film** ever produced
- Ranked **#3 most-missed discontinued film** in r/AnalogCommunity survey (August 2023); got 50 upvotes, behind only "cheap drugstore film" (59) and "high speed slide film" (56)
- No replacement has been produced by any manufacturer since
- Remaining cold-stored stock on eBay/forums is tightly held and heavily priced
