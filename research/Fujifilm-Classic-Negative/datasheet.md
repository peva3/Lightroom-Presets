## Official Fujifilm Description

**Tagline:** "A Memory of a Day Gone By"

There are two types of negative film. Professional negative, which were high-quality emulsions stored and developed under controlled conditions, and **consumer negative**, which were used to record memories and make photographs for family photo albums. Consumer negative films were often left in the cameras for long periods of time, and **colors were biased toward cyan-green in the shadows and magenta in the highlights**. As a result, these photographs are characterized by **hard contrast and color contrast**.

### Key Official Points:
- Modeled after Fujifilm's **Fujicolor Superia** line of consumer negative film
- Emulates the look of consumer-grade film left in cameras for extended periods
- **Cyan-green bias in shadows**
- **Magenta bias in highlight areas**
- "Hard contrast and color contrast"
- "Colorful even in the midtones"
- Described as "unbalanced and lo-fi, but nonetheless comforting"
- Works well with added grain
- Effective for snapshots and "full-auto" settings
- Gives images a nostalgic, memory-like quality

## Technical Color Science (from Fuji's documentation)

Classic Negative is based on Fujifilm's **4th Color Layer Technology** used in their Superia consumer films. Key color science properties:

1. **Shadow Color Bias:** Cyan-green shift in darker tones (hallmark of consumer negative film)
2. **Highlight Color Bias:** Slight magenta cast in overexposed/highlight areas
3. **Red Rendering:** Reds shift toward orange (warmer)
4. **Green Rendering:** Greens are subdued, deep, rich but muted
5. **Skin Tones:** Warm cast with slight amber quality
6. **Blue Rendering:** Blues become slightly teal/cyan shifted

### Exposure Behavior:
- The look changes significantly with exposure level
- **Overexposure** produces more pastel/magenta highlight quality
- **Underexposure** deepens the cyan-green shadow bias
- This exposure-dependent behavior mimics how Superia film responds

## Introduction History

- **Introduced:** October 2019 with the Fujifilm X-Pro3
- **Sensor generation:** X-Trans IV and later
- **Not available on:** X-Trans III and earlier cameras (X-T3, X-T30, X-Pro2, etc.)
- **Available on all** X-Trans IV cameras except X-T3 & X-T30 (firmware limitation)
- **Available on all** X-Trans V cameras
- **Available on** GFX cameras from GFX100S onward

## Position Among Film Simulations

Classic Negative sits between:
- **Classic Chrome** (more saturated, less green shadow bias, designed for documentary/reportage)
- **Nostalgic Negative** (more amber/warm overall cast, different highlight rendering)
- **PRO Neg. Std** (more neutral, less color contrast, professional portraiture)

It is considered the **second most popular** Fujifilm film simulation after Classic Chrome (per Fuji X Weekly usage data).
