# Agfa Ultra 100 — Full Aesthetic Breakdown

## Overview

Agfa Ultra 100 was a short-lived (2003–2005) ultra-saturated consumer color negative film from Agfa that gained cult status after discontinuation. It was the "saturation king" of consumer color neg — more saturated than Ektar 100 but with consumer-grade contrast and grain. Think of it as Ektar's louder, less refined cousin: punchy primaries, strong red bias, and colors that blast off the print.

---

## 1. Color Palette

| Channel | Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Reds** | Deep crimson, intensely saturated — the defining trait |
| **Oranges** | Warm, highly saturated |
| **Yellows** | Golden, saturated; warm-biased |
| **Blues** | Strong, deep — not muted |
| **Greens** | Slightly subdued, warm-biased |
| **Magentas** | Suppressed (Agfa palette, not Fuji) |

### Color Temperature
- Daylight balanced (~5500K)
- Warm overall from saturated red/orange channels
- Slightly warmer than neutral

---

## 2. Contrast Characteristics

- **High contrast** — among the highest of any consumer color neg
- Snappy midtone separation
- Deep blacks without crushing
- Highlights hold reasonably but can clip in extreme light
- Designed to pop on consumer minilab prints

---

## 3. Grain Structure

- Consumer-grade grain, more visible than Portra/Ektar
- Noticeable but not distracting at normal print sizes
- Adds to the aggressive "pop" aesthetic
- Coarser than Agfa Vista series

---

## 4. Fuji X Weekly Reference Recipe

**Source:** [Fuji X Weekly — Agfa Ultra 100 v2](https://fujixweekly.com/2024/02/20/agfa-ultra-100-v2-fujifilm-x-t5-x-trans-v-film-simulation-recipe/) (Ritchie Roesch)

Recipe developed from fresh-frozen samples shot by a long-term Agfa Ultra 100 collector:
- Film Simulation: Nostalgic Neg.
- DR400; Grain: Weak, Large
- Color Chrome Effect: Weak; Color Chrome FX Blue: Weak
- WB: 5800K (-3 Red, -3 Blue)
- Highlight +1.5; Shadow +1; Color +3
- Sharpness -2; High ISO NR -4; Clarity -2
- Exposure Compensation: 0 to +1

Described as warmer and more vibrant than the v1 Classic Negative-based recipe. The Nostalgic Neg. base gives it a print-like quality while Color +3 pushes vibrancy to match Ultra 100's saturation level.

---

## 5. "Saturation King" Characteristics

- Higher saturation than Ektar 100
- Primary colors dominate — reds, blues, yellows are massive
- Not subtle — this film was designed to make drugstore prints pop
- Greens are more subdued (Agfa color science vs Kodak)
- Magenta channel suppressed (unlike Fuji consumer films)

---

## 6. Summary

> **Agfa Ultra 100 was the most saturated consumer color negative film ever made — an ultra-vivid, high-contrast stock with deep crimson reds, punchy primaries, and a warm color bias that made every frame look like a vacation highlight reel.**
