# Agfa Ultra 100 — Community Lightroom Recipes

> **Compiled from:** Fuji X Weekly (Ritchie Roesch), r/analog, r/Lightroom, r/postprocessing, Photrio.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy

Agfa Ultra 100 emulation must deliver:
1. Extreme saturation — the "saturation king" designation
2. Punchy primary colors (reds, blues, yellows)
3. High consumer-grade contrast
4. Deep crimson reds (not orange-reds)
5. Visible consumer grain

## 3. Community Slider Ranges

### Basic Panel
- Exposure: +0.10
- Contrast: +15 to +25 (high consumer contrast)
- Highlights: -25 to -35
- Shadows: +10 to +20
- Whites: +10 to +20
- Blacks: -10 to -20

### HSL — The "Saturation King" Palette
- Red Sat: +25 to +35 (defining trait — deep crimson)
- Red Hue: -5 to -10 (shift toward crimson)
- Orange Sat: +10 to +20
- Yellow Sat: +10 to +15
- Green Sat: -5 to -15 (subdued greens)
- Blue Sat: +10 to +20 (punchy primaries)
- Magenta Sat: -10 to -20 (suppressed)

### Color Grading
- Shadows: H240, Sat 5-10 (cool anchor)
- Highlights: H30-40, Sat 5-10
- Blending: 50

### Grain
- Amount: 20-30 (consumer grain)
- Size: 30-35
- Roughness: 45-55

---

## 4. Common Mistakes

1. **Not enough red saturation** — this is THE defining trait
2. **Using only global Saturation** — reds need specific HSL boost
3. **Low contrast** — Ultra 100 is high contrast, not flat
4. **Clean/no grain** — consumer grain is part of the character
5. **Magenta push** — Agfa suppresses magenta; Fuji does the opposite
