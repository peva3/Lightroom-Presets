# Lomography Metropolis — Characteristics

## Film Stock Profile
- **Type:** Desaturated color negative (ISO 100-400)
- **Manufacturer:** Lomography (custom emulsion)
- **Status:** In production
- **Format:** 35mm, 120, 110, 16mm

## Aesthetic Character
- **Primary:** Muted, desaturated urban color palette with a warm cast
- **Contrast:** High — strong tonal separation even with muted colors
- **Saturation:** Very low — "almost B&W color" with a single muted color dimension retained
- **Greens:** Become muted olive-brown, almost khaki
- **Blues:** Most retained color — slightly muted but present
- **Skin tones:** Pallid, subdued, desaturated — not a portrait film
- **Shadows:** Deep, crushed blacks with visible grain
- **Highlights:** Some warmth, slight golden cast
- **Overall vibe:** Gritty, urban, dystopian, fashion-editorial, lo-fi hi-fi hybrid

## Technical Notes
- Metropolis uses an unconventional dye coupler set that intentionally desaturates
- The warm cast comes from the base tint + coupler chemistry, not white balance
- Grain structure is prominent and contributes to the urban/gritty texture
- Performs best in overcast, urban, and low-color environments
- Not recommended for vibrant landscapes or skin-tone-critical work
