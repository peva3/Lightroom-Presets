# Community Recipes — Michael Kenna

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/analog
- Long exposure techniques are the primary discussion point — ND filters, exposure calculation
- Minimalist composition is harder to teach than exposure technique
- Kenna's work is more about what you exclude than what you include

### From YouTube Tutorials
- Michael Kenna is frequently referenced in long exposure tutorials
- High contrast with smooth water/sky is the common goal
- Some tutorials suggest negative dehaze for atmospheric softness
- Kenna's square format (Hasselblad) is noted as important to the composition

### From Landscape Photography Communities
- "Kenna style" = long exposure + minimalism + high contrast
- The combination of smooth water AND high contrast is challenging without looking flat
- Kenna's printing (selenium toned) adds a subtle warmth to B&W prints — hard to replicate digitally

### Community Consensus
- This preset works as a base for long exposure images
- High contrast is correct for Kenna — he prints dark
- Minimal grain (long exposure smooths everything)
- Best applied to images with strong negative space and geometric simplicity
