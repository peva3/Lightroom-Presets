# Lomography Color 100 — Aesthetic Characteristics

## Overview

Lomography Color 100 is Lomography's entry-level ISO 100 color negative film, part of their "Lomography Film" line. True to the Lomography brand philosophy of "don't think, just shoot," this film delivers saturated, punchy, slightly unpredictable results with the character of budget consumer color negative film. It's believed to be manufactured by Kodak (similar to ColorPlus 200 formulation) but with Lomography's custom finishing. The low ISO allows for fine grain and bright daylight shooting.

## Core Aesthetic Signature

### Color Palette

- **Primary Color Cast**: Warm, slightly yellow-gold. Unmistakably consumer-grade Kodak color science with a Lomo twist.
- **Reds**: Bold, saturated, slightly orange-leaning. Reds pop with typical consumer film saturation.
- **Yellows & Oranges**: Very warm and saturated. The golden-hour enhancement is strong — this film loves warm light.
- **Greens**: Moderately saturated, slightly warm. Less cyan/blue than Fuji consumer films. Foliage is rich but not electric.
- **Blues**: Vivid sky blues with good saturation. Slightly darker luminance — the classic consumer film "built-in polarizer."
- **Skin Tones**: Warm, slightly yellow. The Lomo philosophy embraces imperfection — skin tones are warm and nostalgic rather than clinically accurate.

### Contrast & Tonality

- **Overall Contrast**: Medium-high. Lomo films are designed for visual impact, not subtlety. Images have immediate pop.
- **Highlight Roll-off**: Moderate. Highlights compress with warm fringe. Slight halation-like character in strong light.
- **Shadow Detail**: Decent but not extensive. Shadows block up with warm cast. The "lo-fi" character is part of the appeal.
- **Midtone Contrast**: Strong — images feel three-dimensional and punchy.

### Grain

- **Grain Level**: Fine at ISO 100. The low speed keeps grain in check.
- **Grain Character**: Fine, slightly irregular — the consumer emulsion character is visible at 100% but not dominant.
- **Comparison**: Similar to Kodak ColorPlus 200 but slightly finer due to lower ISO.

### Exposure Behavior

- **Box Speed (ISO 100)**: Punchy, saturated, warm. Best for sunny/bright conditions.
- **Overexposure (+1 stop, EI 50)**: Pastel-warm rendering, reduced contrast, dreamy highlight rendering.
- **Underexposure (-1 stop)**: Grain becomes visible, colors desaturate slightly, shadows get muddy.

### Lomo Brand Philosophy

Lomography Color 100 is not designed for clinical accuracy — it's designed for character. The film embraces:
- Warm, nostalgic color rendering
- Slightly unpredictable exposure response
- Consumer-grade charm over professional precision
- "Happy accidents" in mixed lighting

## Summary

```
Warm, golden consumer color negative at ISO 100
Bold saturation — reds, yellows, and blues pop
Medium-high contrast for visual impact
Fine grain at low ISO
Lomography's "don't think, just shoot" philosophy in a film
```
