### FxW Recipe → Lightroom Translation

Lomo Color 100 is the budget Lomo consumer film — saturated, warm, punchy, slightly unpredictable. Translation priorities: strong saturation, warm golden cast, medium-high contrast, visible consumer grain.

#### Basic Panel
- **Exposure**: +0.20 (slight brightness — Lomo philosophy)
- **Contrast**: +25 (medium-high — punchy Lomo character)
- **Highlights**: -30 (warm consumer highlight roll-off)
- **Shadows**: +12 (moderate shadow lift — consumer film character)
- **Whites**: +8 (bright white point — Lomo pop)
- **Blacks**: -18 (rich consumer black)

- **Highlights**: Hue 45°, Sat 18 (warm golden — Lomo warmth)

## Community Consensus

Lomography Color 100 is popular among Lomo enthusiasts:
1. **"Happy accident" film**: The Lomo philosophy embraces unpredictability
2. **Golden hour specialist**: Warm tones are heavily amplified
3. **Better than expected**: Many shooters report it's sharper and finer-grained than expected for a budget Lomo film
4. **Kodak connection**: Believed to be manufactured by Kodak (similar to ColorPlus 200 formulation at lower ISO)
5. **Plastic camera favorite**: Popular in Holga, Diana, and Lomo LC-A cameras where the lens adds character
6. **Cross-processing potential**: Some shooters cross-process in E-6 for extreme saturation/shifts
