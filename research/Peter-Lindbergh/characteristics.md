# Peter Lindbergh

## Biography
- **Name**: Peter Lindbergh (1944–2019)
- **Nationality**: German
- **Era**: 1970s–2010s

## Philosophy
- Pioneer of the "supermodel" phenomenon — 1990 British Vogue cover with Turlington, Campbell, Crawford, Evangelista, Patitz
- Rejected retouching: "The first thing I say is, 'Take off all your makeup.'"
- Photography should liberate women from the "terror of perfection and youth"
- Cinematic storytelling in single frames
- Natural light whenever possible

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: Nikon 35mm, Pentax 67, Hasselblad, later digital
- **Film**: Kodak Tri-X 400 (his signature), Ilford HP5+
- **Processing**: Pushed development for grain structure
- **Printing**: Lifted midtones, soft contrast, heavy grain aesthetic

## Visual Signature
- **Lifted midtones**: Blacks are never truly black. Shadows breathe. The entire image floats in Zone IV–VII.
- **Soft, organic grain**: Prominent but never harsh — grain as texture, not noise
- **Low contrast**: The anti-Salgado. Transitions are gentle and atmospheric.
- **Skin-first tonality**: Skin tones placed in upper-midrange, glowing but not blown
- **Industrial backdrops**: Factories, warehouses, empty streets — beauty in the mundane
- **Natural, unvarnished beauty**: Freckles, wrinkles, lines — all celebrated

## Iconic Images
- *British Vogue January 1990* cover (the supermodels)
- *Wings* editorial with Helena Christensen
- Pirelli Calendar 1996 and 2002

## Preset Design Notes
- Lowest contrast in the B&W collection — lifted midtones everywhere
- Prominent grain with organic roughness
- Shadows lifted significantly (Shadows +35, Blacks only -12)
- No heavy sky darkening — the soft, atmospheric look avoids dramatic sky treatments
- Sharpness kept low to let grain carry texture
