# Community Recipes — Peter Lindbergh

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/fashionphotography
- Lindbergh's "no retouching" philosophy is widely discussed
- The lifted midtone look is the most copied aspect
- Grain as texture, not flaw — this is the key Lindbergh aesthetic

### From YouTube Tutorials
- "Peter Lindbergh style" tutorials consistently recommend:
  - Lift the black point (faded blacks)
  - Add significant grain
  - Keep contrast low
  - Skin tones should be mid-gray and luminous
- Some recommend negative clarity for the soft look (controversial — Lindbergh's images are sharp)

### From Fashion Photography Forums
- Lindbergh's assistants have revealed he shot Tri-X and pushed it slightly
- Natural light + industrial locations = his preferred setup
- The "soft" look comes from printing, not from soft focus while shooting

### Community Consensus
- Lifted midtones (Blacks > -20, Shadows pushed positive) is the signature move
- Grain is essential — don't be shy
- Sharpness must be reduced to let grain carry texture
- Low contrast overall, but contrast within midtones should be preserved
