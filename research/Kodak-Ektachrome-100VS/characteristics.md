# Kodak Ektachrome 100VS — Aesthetic Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

## Overview

Kodak Ektachrome 100VS (Vivid Saturation) was the punchy sibling of the standard E100G/E100 family. Where standard Ektachrome aimed for accurate, clean, neutral rendering, 100VS cranked the saturation and contrast for high-impact results. Discontinued around 2012, it was Kodak's answer to Fuji Velvia — a landscape and nature photographer's slide film. This document serves as a reference for creating a Lightroom preset that emulates Ektachrome 100VS.

## Core Aesthetic Traits

### 1. Vivid, Punchy Saturation
100VS is defined by its boosted saturation across all channels:
- **Reds**: Pushed toward warm, vivid crimson. Red flowers, autumn foliage, and warm-toned subjects render with spectacular intensity.
- **Yellows**: Golden and saturated — sunset and golden-hour scenes glow.
- **Greens**: Hyper-saturated, yellow-green bias. Foliage is intensely vibrant, similar to Velvia's green rendering.
- **Blues**: Deep, saturated, slightly cyan-leaning. Skies are dramatic and punchy.

### 2. High Contrast
Higher contrast than standard E100 — blacks are deep and rich, highlights clip with conviction. The S-curve is steeper, producing more "pop" and visual impact at the cost of shadow/highlight detail.

### 3. Warm White Balance
Warmer than standard Ektachrome E100 — the default white balance has a subtle golden warmth. This warmth, combined with boosted red/yellow saturation, gave 100VS its signature "rich" look that was popular for landscape and nature work.

### 4. Fine Grain (RMS 8)
Despite the vivid saturation, 100VS maintained Ektachrome's fine grain structure. The grain is nearly invisible at normal viewing sizes.

### 5. Difficult Skin Tones
Like Velvia, 100VS was not kind to skin. The warm bias + boosted red saturation rendered caucasian skin overly red/magenta. Portrait photographers avoided 100VS in favor of standard E100G.

## E100VS vs. Standard E100

| Characteristic | E100 (Standard) | E100VS (Vivid) |
|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Saturation** | Moderate | High |
| **White balance** | Cool-leaning (5500K) | Warmer (~5300K feel) |
| **Contrast** | High | Higher |
| **Reds** | True, moderate | Pushed, warm |
| **Greens** | Cool, clean | Hyper-saturated, yellow-green |
| **Blues** | Cool-cyan, strong | Deep, saturated |
| **Skin tones** | Usable (cool) | Difficult (red) |
| **Best for** | All-around, architecture | Landscapes, nature, high-impact |

## Comparison: E100VS vs. Velvia 50

| Characteristic | E100VS | Velvia 50 |
|---------------|--------|-----------|
| **Saturation** | High | Extreme |
| **Warm bias** | Golden-warm | Neutral-warm |
| **Green rendering** | Yellow-green, saturated | Emerald, hyper-saturated |
| **Blue rendering** | Deep, saturated | Electric, polarized |
| **Skin tones** | Poor (red) | Very poor |
| **Contrast** | High | Very high |
| **Grain** | RMS 8 | RMS 9 |
