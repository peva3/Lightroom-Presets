# Fuji Neopan 400 — Community Recipes

## Primary Source: t3mujinpack (Darktable Styles — Family Reference)

While t3mujinpack does not have a specific Neopan 400 style, it includes Fuji Neopan 1600 and Fuji Neopan Acros 100 in its B&W collection. These family-adjacent styles informed our understanding of Fuji's Neopan tonal character.

### t3mujinpack Approach (Neopan Family)
- Channel Mixer for Fuji-appropriate spectral response
- Lab Tone Curves tuned for Neopan contrast characteristics
- Neopan Acros 100: finer grain, sharper, more modern
- Neopan 1600: heavier grain, pushed contrast, dramatic

Source: https://github.com/t3mujinpack/t3mujinpack

### Community Notes (from forums)
- Less frequently discussed than Tri-X or HP5+, but respected by those who used it
- Often described as "Tri-X but slightly different" — similar character with Fuji's own spectral signature
- Works especially well for portraits in available light
- Some report it responds well to green filtration for skin tones
- Discontinued status makes it increasingly obscure; community recipes are rare

### LR Preset Design Notes
- Traditional cubic grain: pronounced GrainAmount=50, moderate Size=35, high Roughness=65
- Grain structure is the defining element — this is not a "clean" film
- Higher contrast (+30) and deep blacks (-30) for a dramatic, classic B&W look
- GrayMixer emphasizes strong red/orange lift (skin tones) with aggressive blue suppression (skies)
- All STYLEGUIDE rules: Sharpness=10, Clarity/Texture/Dehaze=0, no Calibration, no WB
- Positioned between Tri-X and HP5+ in character — Fuji's unique spectral sensitivity gives slightly different gray rendering
