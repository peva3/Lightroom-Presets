# Kodak Farbwelt 200 Expired — Full Aesthetic Breakdown

## Overview

Kodak Farbwelt 200 ("Farbwelt" = "Color World" in German) was Kodak consumer color negative film sold in the German market from 2000 until approximately 2012–2013. It was likely a rebranded or slightly retooled version of Kodak Gold 200 specifically for German/European distribution. There were also ISO 100, 400, and 800 versions.

This preset specifically targets the **expired** version of Farbwelt 200 — film that has aged past its development-by date. Expired color negative film takes on warm amber/brown tones, loses saturation, develops heavy base fog (lifting blacks), and shows more pronounced/irregular grain.

---

## 1. Color Palette

| Channel | Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Reds** | Shifted amber/brown; desaturated from age |
| **Oranges** | Dominant warm tones |
| **Yellows** | Golden-amber cast |
| **Greens** | Severely faded, brown-leaning |
| **Blues** | Heavily desaturated; almost absent in expired condition |
| **Overall** | Warm, soft, faded, brown-amber cast |

### Expired Film Characteristics
- **Base fog**: Chemical fog from age lifts the black point, reducing contrast
- **Color shifts**: Blue/yellow layer degrades fastest → warm amber shift
- **Desaturation**: All channels lose saturation over time
- **Grain increase**: Aging emulsion shows more prominent grain

---

## 2. Contrast Characteristics

- **Very low contrast** — expired film contrast collapses
- Heavy base fog lifts shadow region
- Highlights compressed
- Flat tonality overall
- Almost no true black exists

---

## 3. Grain Structure

- **Heavy expired grain** — larger, rougher than fresh stock
- Irregular clumping from aged emulsion
- Grain is the dominant texture feature
- More visible in midtones and sky areas

## 4. Fuji X Weekly Reference Recipe

**Source:** [Fuji X Weekly — Kodak Farbwelt 200 Expired](https://fujixweekly.com/2025/12/09/kodak-farbwelt-200-expired-a-fujifilm-film-simulation-recipe-for-x-trans-v-cameras/) (Thomas Schwab)

Created by matching against lab-developed prints from a roll expired 2004:
- Film Simulation: Classic Chrome
- DR100; Grain: Strong, Large
- Color Chrome Effect: Weak; Color Chrome FX Blue: Strong
- WB: 7500K (+1 Red, -4 Blue)
- Highlight: -2; Shadow: -2; Color: -3
- Sharpness: -4; High ISO NR: -4; Clarity: -4
- Exposure Compensation: 0 to -1 1/3 (underexpose to protect highlights)

Described as "soft warm tones — like a memory of summertime." Resembles the 1970's Summer recipe.

---

## 5. The "Expired Film" Aesthetic

- Warm amber/brown color cast
- Lifted, foggy blacks
- Desaturated overall with color shifts
- Heavy, rough grain
- Soft, dreamy, nostalgic feel
- "Like a memory of summertime" — the defining quote

---

## 6. Summary

> **Kodak Farbwelt 200 Expired renders the characteristic look of aged German-market consumer film: warm amber-brown overall cast, heavily desaturated colors, lifted base-fog blacks, very low contrast, and pronounced irregular grain — a soft, nostalgic "memory of summertime" aesthetic.**
