# Kodak Farbwelt 200 Expired — Community Lightroom Recipes

> **Compiled from:** Fuji X Weekly (Thomas Schwab / Ritchie Roesch), r/analog, r/Lightroom, expired film community.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy

Expired Farbwelt 200 emulation must deliver:
1. Warm amber-brown color cast (aged emulsion shift)
2. Extremely low contrast (expired film collapse)
3. Lifted blacks from base fog
4. Heavy desaturation, especially blue channel
5. Large, rough expired grain
6. Soft, dreamy tonality

## 3. Community Slider Ranges

### Basic Panel
- Exposure: +0.10 to +0.20 (compensate for base fog)
- Contrast: -15 to -25 (expired contrast collapse)
- Highlights: -55 to -65
- Shadows: +30 to +40 (lifted fogged shadows)
- Whites: -20 to -30
- Blacks: +15 to +25 (no true black)

### HSL — Expired Fade Palette
- Red Hue: +5 to +15 (amber shift)
- Red Sat: -5 to +5 (faded)
- Orange Sat: -5 to +5
- Yellow Sat: -5 to -10
- Green Sat: -15 to -25
- Blue Sat: -20 to -30 (blue channel degrades most)
- Magenta Sat: -25 to -35

### Color Grading (amber/brown warmth)
- Shadows: H25-35, Sat 10-20 (warm brown fog)
- Highlights: H40-50, Sat 5-15 (golden amber)
- Balance: -25 to -35 (shadow bias for base fog effect)
- Blending: 50

### Grain
- Amount: 50-60 (heavy expired grain)
- Size: 35-45
- Roughness: 65-75 (irregular aged grain)

---

## 4. Common Mistakes

1. **Normal contrast** — expired film has very low contrast
2. **True blacks** — base fog prevents true black
3. **Saturated blues** — blues are the first colors to fade in expired film
4. **Fine grain** — expired film grain is large and rough
5. **Not underexposing slightly** — community recommends negative exposure compensation to protect highlights
