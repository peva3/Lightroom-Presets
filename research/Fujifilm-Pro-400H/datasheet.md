# Fujifilm PRO 400H — Official Datasheet & Specifications

> **Status: DISCONTINUED** — Announced January 14, 2021

---

## Overview

**FUJICOLOR PRO 400H** was a professional-grade, daylight-balanced, ISO 400 color negative film designed for portrait and wedding photography. It featured Fujifilm's proprietary **fourth color layer technology** — a cyan-sensitive layer between the green-sensitive and red-sensitive layers — which was its defining technical characteristic and ultimately the reason for its demise.

**Official product page (archived):** `fujifilm.com/us/en/business/professional-photography/film/pro-400h`

## Key Specifications (per Fujifilm)

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Film type** | Color negative (C-41 process) |
| **ISO speed** | 400 |
| **Balancing** | Daylight (5500K) |
| **Formats** | 135 (35mm), 120 (medium format), sheet film |
| **Grain** | RMS granularity: 4 (very fine) |
| **Exposure latitude** | −1 stop to +3 stops (unique 4th layer provides exceptional overexposure tolerance) |
| **Base** | 135: cellulose triacetate; 120: clear polyester |
| **Reciprocity** | No compensation needed 1/4000–1s; +1/3 stop at 4s; +1/2 stop at 16s |
| **Processing** | Standard C-41 (also compatible with C-41RA rapid access) |

## Official Fujifilm Product Description

> *"High Speed and Wide Exposure Latitude. Superb Skin-Tone Reproduction. Fine skin-tone reproduction. Excellent Three-Dimensional Appearance. Faithful Color Reproduction."*

### Claimed Results

1. **Faithful reproduction of neutral grays** over a wide exposure range from underexposure to overexposure
2. **Superb skin-tone and hue reproduction** with continuously smooth gradation from the highlights to the shadows
3. **Clear colors in the highlights** and appropriately controlled color-saturation in the shadows to allow rendering of subjects with a feeling of three-dimensional realism
4. **Faithful color reproduction** of scenes under a wide variety of lighting
5. **Negative density level** for maximum printing uniformity and efficiency

---

## The Fourth Layer Technology

PRO 400H used Fujifilm's proprietary 4th color layer — a cyan-sensitive emulsion layer placed between the green and red sensitive layers. This was the same technology pioneered with **Fujifilm Reala 100** (the first consumer film with a 4th layer).

The 4th layer's purpose was to better handle the spectral overlap that occurs under fluorescent and mixed lighting, producing more accurate color — especially in the green-cyan range. This is what gave Pro 400H its characteristic cool rendering and pastel fidelity.

### Why It Was Discontinued

Per Fujifilm's official statement to The Phoblographer (January 14, 2021):

> *"FUJIFILM PRO 400H film is a unique product that is coated with a fourth layer – requiring specialized raw materials and chemicals. As it has become increasingly difficult to procure the raw materials needed to produce PRO 400H, Fujifilm has made the difficult business decision to discontinue PRO 400H."*

- 35mm production ceased **immediately** (January 14, 2021)
- 120 production continued through **end of 2021** (allocation-based)
- The specialized raw materials for the 4th layer became unobtainable, likely exacerbated by COVID-19 supply chain disruption

---

## Spectral Sensitivity Characteristics

PRO 400H was balanced for daylight (5500K). Its 4th layer design meant it handled the green-cyan region of the spectrum with unusual fidelity, producing:

- Accurate separation between greens and cyans (critical for foliage, mint tones, and pastel blues)
- More neutral rendering under fluorescent/mixed lighting than 3-layer films
- Cooler overall color palette compared to Kodak Portra (which has a warm bias)
- Better shadow color retention — shadows go mint/cool rather than muddy brown

---

## Reciprocity Characteristics

Fujifilm specified minimal reciprocity failure within normal shutter ranges. The film can be used at exposures from 1/4000s to 1 second without adjustment. Beyond 1 second, minor compensation is needed (+1/3 to +1/2 stop).

No color correction filters were recommended for reciprocity — the 4th layer inherently handled the color shifts that plague other films during long exposures.

---

## Post-Discontinuation Market

- NOS (New Old Stock) in 120 format continued appearing at retailers into 2023–2024 as Fujifilm's final production batch was distributed
- Expired 120 rolls were still sold cold-stored as of 2025
- 35mm became effectively extinct immediately
- Prices on secondary market (eBay, r/photomarket) escalated dramatically — often $25–40 per roll of 120
- No replacement has ever been announced or released
- Kodak Portra 400 is the de facto closest alternative, but has a warmer palette and different green rendering
