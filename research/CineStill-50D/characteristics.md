# CineStill 50D — Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

## Film Stock Profile
- **Type:** Daylight-balanced color negative cinema film (remjet removed) This document serves as a reference for creating a Lightroom preset that emulates 50D.
- **Base stock:** Kodak Vision3 50D (5203)
- **Manufacturer:** CineStill (converted from Kodak cinema stock)
- **ISO:** 50 (daylight)
- **Status:** In production
- **Format:** 35mm, 120

## Aesthetic Character
- **Primary:** Ultra-fine grain, narrow dynamic range, warm daylight cinema look with signature halation
- **Contrast:** Moderate to high — Vision3 cinema stock has punchy contrast for projection
- **Saturation:** Moderate, natural — slightly muted compared to stills color neg
- **Skin tones:** Natural, slightly warm — cinema skin tone rendering
- **Greens:** Neutral to slightly cool
- **Blues:** Clean, natural — daylight-balanced
- **Shadows:** Clean, open — no muddiness
- **Highlights:** The halation signature — red/orange glow around bright point sources when remjet is removed
- **Grain:** Nearly invisible — ISO 50 + Vision3 technology = finest grain in the CineStill lineup
- **Overall vibe:** Sun-drenched cinema, LA daylight, Kodak 5203 look, Christopher Doyle day exteriors

## Technical Notes
- Vision3 50D is Kodak's finest-grain cinema stock
- Remjet removal (CineStill process) causes:
  - Halation: light reflects off pressure plate into red-sensitive layer → red glow around highlights
  - Slightly reduced contrast (no anti-halation backing)
  - Slightly different color rendering (remjet filters some wavelengths)
- Needs abundant light — ISO 50 demands direct sunlight or strong studio lighting
- Narrower dynamic range than stills color neg — exposes more like slide film

## Comparison to CineStill 800T
| Aspect | 50D | 800T |
