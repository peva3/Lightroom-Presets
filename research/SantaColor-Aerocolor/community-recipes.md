# SantaColor (Kodak Aerocolor IV) — Community Lightroom Recipes

> **Compiled from:** Fuji X Weekly (Ritchie Roesch), r/analog, r/Lightroom, Santa Rae documentation.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy

SantaColor emulation requires:
1. Reddish-orange color cast (the noted film characteristic)
2. Exceptional sharpness — the defining surveillance heritage
3. Clean whites from no orange mask
4. Cool-crisp overall color balance under the warm cast
5. Very fine grain
6. Lifted shadows (aerial surveillance needs shadow detail)

## 3. Community Slider Ranges

### Basic Panel
- Exposure: 0 to +0.15
- Contrast: 0 to +10
- Highlights: -30 to -40
- Shadows: +35 to +50 (significant shadow lift)
- Whites: +5 to +15
- Blacks: -5 to -10

### HSL — Aerial Surveillance Palette
- Red Hue: 0 to +10 (reddish-orange cast)
- Red Sat: +10 to +20
- Orange Sat: +5 to +15
- Yellow Sat: 0 to +10
- Green Sat: -5 to -10
- Aqua Sat: 0 to +10
- Blue Sat: +5 to +15 (clean blues)
- Magenta Sat: -5 to -15

### Color Grading (subtle warmth over cool base)
- Shadows: H215-225, Sat 5-10 (cool aerial shadow)
- Highlights: H30-40, Sat 5-10 (warm highlight)
- Blending: 50

### Grain
- Amount: 15-25 (very fine — surveillance film)
- Size: 15-25
- Roughness: 35-45

---

## 4. Common Mistakes

1. **Excessive grain** — this is fine-grain surveillance film
2. **Deep shadows** — aerial films preserve shadow detail
3. **Over-saturated blues** — clean but not hyper-saturated
4. **Ignoring the red/orange cast** — it may be subtle but it's there
5. **Applying heavy clarity** — the sharpness comes from the emulsion, not post-processing
