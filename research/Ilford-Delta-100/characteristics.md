# Ilford Delta 100 — Aesthetic Characteristics

## Overview

Ilford Delta 100 is a modern T-grain (tabular/epitaxial) fine-grain B&W film at ISO 100. Unlike the traditional cubic-grain FP4+, Delta 100 uses core-shell crystal technology for exceptionally fine grain, high sharpness, and a clean, modern rendering. It represents the "digital-era" B&W aesthetic — cleaner, sharper, and more linear than traditional films.

## Grain

- **RMS Granularity ~7** — exceptionally fine, comparable to Pan F but at ISO 100
- Epitaxial (core-shell) T-grain structure: smaller, more uniform crystals
- Grain is virtually invisible in normal use
- Cleaner and more uniform than FP4+'s traditional grain
- At 35mm: grain-free up to 16×20" prints
- At 120: completely grainless

## Contrast & Tonal Characteristics

- **Moderate native contrast** (gamma ~0.60-0.65)
- More linear characteristic curve than traditional films
- Excellent shadow detail — true ISO 100 speed
- Clean, smooth midtone transitions
- Good highlight handling with defined shoulder

## Spectral Sensitivity

- Full panchromatic with balanced response
- Slightly more linear spectral sensitivity than traditional films
- Skin tones: clean, natural, luminous
- Blue skies: moderately dark, excellent cloud separation
- Green foliage: rich and detailed

## Delta 100 vs FP4+ (Key Differences)

| Property | Delta 100 | FP4+ |
|----------|-----------|------|
| Crystal tech | T-grain (epitaxial) | Traditional cubic |
| Grain | Finer, cleaner, more uniform | Slightly coarser, more "classic" |
| Sharpness | Higher | Moderate |
| Tonal curve | More linear/modern | More classic S-curve |
| Character | Clean, clinical, modern | Warm, classic, organic |

## Developer Effects

| Developer | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| ID-11/D-76 1:1 | Reference standard — excellent all-around |
| Perceptol | Ultra-fine grain, slightly lower contrast |
| DD-X | Maximum sharpness, good speed |
| Rodinal 1:50 | Higher acutance, slightly visible grain |

## Best Uses

- Fine art landscapes requiring maximum detail
- Architecture and product photography
- Portraiture where clean skin rendering is desired
- Any situation where a "modern film" aesthetic is preferred over classic grain
- Scanning-heavy workflows (T-grain films scan excellently)
