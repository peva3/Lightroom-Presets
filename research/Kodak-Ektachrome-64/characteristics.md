# Kodak Ektachrome 64 — Aesthetic Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

## The Film

Kodak Ektachrome 64 (E-6, daylight balanced at 5500K) was the mid-speed vintage Ektachrome slide film that predates the modern E100. Introduced as part of the Ektachrome family in the E-4 era and later converted to E-6 processing, it represents the classic American slide film look of the 1970s-1980s. It was eventually replaced/succeeded by Ektachrome 100 and later the E100 family. This document serves as a reference for creating a Lightroom preset that emulates Ektachrome 64.

## Core Aesthetic

### 1. Vintage Slide Character
- **Softer contrast** than modern E100 — the older emulsion technology produced more gradual highlight rolloff and less aggressive shadow crush
- **Cooler overall palette** than modern E100, with a noticeable blue-cyan lean in the shadows that was characteristic of earlier Ektachrome generations
- **Moderate saturation** — not as vivid as E100VS or Velvia, but more saturated than consumer negative films
- **Subdued reds** compared to later Ektachrome — slight magenta undertone rather than pure warm red

### 2. Color Palette by Hue

| Hue | Ektachrome 64 Rendering |
|-----|------------------------|
| **Blue (sky)** | Moderate depth, cyan-leaning (more cyan than E100) |
| **Blue (shadow)** | Pronounced cool blue cast — the defining vintage Ektachrome characteristic |
| **Red** | Slightly magenta-red, restrained |
| **Green (foliage)** | Natural, slight blue-green bias — cooler than modern E100 |
| **Yellow/Orange** | Accurate, not pushed |
| **Skin tones** | Cool-leaning, slightly pale — warm filter recommended for portraits |
| **Whites** | Clean but not brilliant — slightly cool tint |

### 3. Contrast & Dynamic Range
- **Moderate contrast** — lower than modern slide films due to older emulsion technology
- **RMS ~11-12** — fine grain for ISO 64, but coarser than modern E100 (RMS 8)
- **Dynamic range ~7 stops** — standard for slide film of the era
- Shadows go to deep blue-black rather than neutral black

### 4. The "Vintage Chrome" Look
Ektachrome 64 has a distinctive period-correct look:
- Less clinical than modern E100
- Pronounced cool shadow bias (more so than E100G or E100)
- A slight "faded" quality in the midtones that reads as "vintage" to modern eyes
- The blue channel response was more sensitive, leading to blue-biased neutrals in open shade

## Comparison: Ektachrome 64 vs E100 (2018)

| Attribute | Ektachrome 64 (vintage) | E100 (2018) |
|-----------|------------------------|-------------|
| Contrast | Moderate | High |
| Shadow bias | Strong cool blue | Neutral-cool |
| Saturation | Moderate | Moderate-high |
| Blue rendering | Cyan-leaning, pronounced | Clean cool |
| Skin tones | Cool, pale | Neutral-cool |
| Grain | RMS ~11-12 | RMS 8 |
| Dynamic range | ~7 stops | ~7-8 stops |
| Overall vibe | Vintage American slide | Modern architectural slide |
