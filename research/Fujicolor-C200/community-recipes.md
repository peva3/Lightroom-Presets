### FxW Recipe → Lightroom Translation

C200 is the beloved budget Fuji consumer film — slightly cool, moderate contrast, distinctive green undertone, cult following. Translation priorities: slight cool bias, warm saturation moderated, green/blue emphasis, budget grain.

#### Basic Panel
- **Exposure**: +0.15 (consumer film baseline)
- **Contrast**: +15 (medium — less than Kodak consumer films)
- **Highlights**: -25 (controlled consumer highlight roll-off)
- **Shadows**: +12 (open but not excessive — consumer shadow character)
- **Whites**: +5 (clean but not bright)
- **Blacks**: -10 (moderate — consumer film doesn't crush hard)

#### Color Grading
- **Highlights**: Hue 40°, Sat 5 (subtle warm highlight — not typically Fuji but adds balance)
- **Shadows**: Hue 160°, Sat 12 (green-cyan shadows — the defining Fuji consumer move)
- **Blending**: 50
- **Balance**: -15 (shadow bias for the cool Fuji look)

#### Grain (Consumer color neg: Amount 30-50)
- **Amount**: 30 (moderate — visible consumer grain)
- **Size**: 28
- **Roughness**: 50

#### STYLEGUIDE Compliance
- No Calibration
- No Temperature/Tint
- Sharpness=10, Clarity=0, Texture=0, Dehaze=0 (grain protection)
- LuminanceSmoothing=0
- Vibrance +3, Saturation -2 (gap = 5 — at boundary)
- Blacks -10 ≥ -30

## Community Consensus (Reddit r/analog, Flickr)

C200 is the most-discussed budget color film online:
1. **Coolness is the defining trait**: "C200 greens and blues hit different"
2. **Versatile**: Works in everything from point-and-shoots to SLRs
3. **Reformulation history**: Pre-2017 Fuji-manufactured vs. post-2017 Kodak-manufactured — both have fans
4. **Pushed C200**: Popular to shoot at EI 400 and push-process for extra contrast and grain
5. **Green shadow tendency**: Community widely notes green undertones in shadows — this is embraced as part of the character
6. **Budget hero**: "The cheapest film that still looks like film"
