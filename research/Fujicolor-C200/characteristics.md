# Fujicolor C200 — Aesthetic Characteristics

## Overview

Fujicolor C200 is Fujifilm's entry-level consumer ISO 200 color negative film, widely available globally and beloved for its low price and distinctive character. Often described as "cheap but magical," C200 has developed a cult following among budget-conscious film photographers who embrace its slight cool bias and nostalgic rendering. The film has undergone reformulations over the years, with the current version (post-2017) being manufactured by Kodak under contract but retaining Fuji's color science.

## Core Aesthetic Signature

### Color Palette

- **Primary Color Cast**: Slightly cool with a subtle green undertone. This is the defining "Fuji consumer" look — cooler than Kodak Gold, with a distinctive color personality.
- **Reds**: Moderate saturation, slightly magenta-leaning rather than the orange-red of Kodak films.
- **Yellows & Oranges**: Slightly subdued compared to Kodak equivalents. Skin tones are less golden but can appear slightly green in mixed light.
- **Greens**: Lush and prominent. Fuji's 4th color layer technology (in earlier versions) enhanced green separation. Foliage renders richly with slight blue-green bias.
- **Blues**: Cool, vivid sky blues. The Fuji cyan bias makes skies pop — this is one of C200's most-loved characteristics.
- **Skin Tones**: Slightly cool, less warm than Kodak. Can appear slightly pale or green-tinged depending on lighting and scanning.

### Contrast & Tonality

- **Overall Contrast**: Medium. Less punchy than Kodak Gold 200, with a slightly flatter midtone rendering.
- **Highlight Roll-off**: Moderate. Highlights retain some detail but can blow out with slight cyan fringe.
- **Shadow Detail**: Good for a consumer film. Shadows have a characteristic cool/green cast.
- **Midtone Contrast**: Moderate-low. This contributes to the "dreamy, nostalgic" feel.

### Grain

- **Grain Level**: Moderate. More visible than premium ISO 200 films, but typical for consumer grade.
- **Grain Character**: Slightly irregular, visible in sky and midtone areas. Not as structured as T-Grain films.
- **Comparison**: Similar to Kodak ColorPlus 200, slightly coarser than Gold 200.

### Exposure Behavior

- **Box Speed (ISO 200)**: Cool, balanced rendering with characteristic green undertone.
- **Overexposure (+1 stop, EI 100)**: Pastel-like rendering, cooler cast intensifies, grain reduces. Popular "dreamy" look.
- **Underexposure (-1 stop)**: Grain becomes prominent, green shadows deepen.

## Summary

```
Slightly cool color balance with subtle green undertone
Medium contrast with moderate saturation
Fuji "4th color layer" green separation
Cult following for its distinctive budget character
Cheap, cheerful, and unmistakably Fuji
```
