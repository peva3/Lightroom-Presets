# Kodak Ektachrome E100 — Datasheet Summary

> **Film type:** Color reversal (slide) film, E-6 process
> **Speed:** ISO 100 / 21° (daylight balanced, 5500K)
> **Formats:** 35mm (2018), 120 (2019), 4x5 sheet (2019), Super 8 & 16mm motion picture

---

## Spectral Sensitivity

Ektachrome E100 is a daylight-balanced color reversal film with three emulsion layers (cyan, magenta, yellow) on a clear acetate base.

### Spectral Sensitivity Peaks
| Layer | Peak Sensitivity | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| Blue-sensitive (yellow-forming) | ~440–460 nm | Strong blue response; contributes to cool-leaning rendering |
| Green-sensitive (magenta-forming) | ~540–550 nm | Balanced green; neutral foliage rendering |
| Red-sensitive (cyan-forming) | ~630–650 nm | Moderate red sensitivity; avoids excessive warmth |

The blue layer has notably strong sensitivity, which contributes to the film's characteristic cool-leaning color balance. Under daylight (5500K), the film renders neutrally, but in open shade or overcast conditions it accentuates cool blues.

### Spectral Dye Density Curves
- Cyan dye: Peak absorption ~690 nm (deep red)
- Magenta dye: Peak absorption ~545 nm (green)
- Yellow dye: Peak absorption ~435 nm (blue)

The dyes are designed for clean separation with minimal unwanted absorption, giving E100 its signature "clean" look.

## Characteristic Curves (D-Log E)

### Density vs. Exposure (Status A Densitometry)
| Layer | D-min | D-max | Gamma (mid) | Shoulder |
|-------|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Red (Cyan dye) | 0.25 | 3.2 | 1.85 | Gradual |
| Green (Magenta dye) | 0.25 | 3.3 | 1.90 | Gradual |
| Blue (Yellow dye) | 0.25 | 3.1 | 1.80 | Gradual |

- **Linearity:** The three curves are well-matched through the midtones, producing neutral grays from shadows through highlights.
- **Straight line portion:** Extends from approximately -2.0 to +0.5 Log E relative to mid-gray, giving roughly 7–8 stops of usable linear range.
- **D-min (base+fog):** ~0.25, producing clean, neutral whites with no color cast.
- **D-max:** ~3.2–3.3, yielding deep, rich blacks with excellent shadow density.

## Exposure and Processing

### Exposure Latitude
- **Box speed:** EI 100 (ISO 100)
- **Underexposure tolerance:** ~0.5 stops (shadows block up quickly)
- **Overexposure tolerance:** ~0.5–1 stop (highlights blow with little recovery)
- **Recommended practice:** Meter for highlights; bracket ±0.5 stops in critical situations
- **Practical EI range:** 80–125 depending on preference; many photographers prefer EI 80 for slightly more shadow detail

### Processing
- **Standard process:** E-6
- **Push processing:** +1 stop possible with extended first developer; +2 stops with significant contrast increase and color shift
- **Pull processing:** Not recommended
- **Cross-processing (C-41):** Possible but unpredictable; yields high-contrast negatives with strong color shifts and orange base

### Color Temperature Response
- Daylight balanced at 5500K
- Under tungsten (3200K): Strong orange cast; requires 80A filter (loses ~2 stops)
- Under overcast/shade (7000–8000K): Pronounced blue cast without warming filter (1A or 81A)
- Excellent for sunset/sunrise: Exaggerates warm tones naturally

---

## Storage and Shelf Life
- Refrigerate at 13°C (55°F) or below for long-term storage
- Allow 1 hour to acclimate before opening
- Process promptly after exposure (within 1 week recommended for color accuracy)
- Unexposed film: shelf life ~18–24 months at room temperature; extended with cold storage
