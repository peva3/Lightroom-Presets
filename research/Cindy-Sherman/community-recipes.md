# Community Recipes — Cindy Sherman

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/arthistory
- Sherman's editing is discussed in terms of period reproduction, not slider values
- The "film still" aesthetic is about reproducing the look of 1950s–70s cinema, fashion, and art photography
- Color palette varies dramatically by series — no single "Sherman color"

### From YouTube Tutorials
- "Vintage film still" tutorials overlap with Sherman's 1970s Film Stills palette:
  - Slight warmth, slight fade
  - Moderate grain
  - Low contrast
- "70s color grade" tutorials often reference the era Sherman was working in

### From Fine Art Photography Resources
- Sherman's printing has evolved: early work was standard C-prints, later work is large-format pigment prints
- The "vintage" quality of early Film Stills is partly the film/paper of the era, partly intentional aesthetic
- Her later digital work has a different color palette — cleaner, more "digital" but still period-aware

### Community Consensus
- This is a classic vintage film look — warmth + slight desaturation + grain
- Low contrast separates it from modern editing styles
- The grain structure should feel like 35mm color negative, not digital noise
- Works as a subtle period-appropriate overlay on many image types
