# Cindy Sherman

## Biography
- **Name**: Cindy Sherman (born 1954)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Era**: 1970s–present

## Philosophy
- "I'm trying to make people realize that they're always playing a role."
- Photography as performance — she is the model, makeup artist, stylist, director, and photographer
- Deconstructs female archetypes: film noir heroine, centerfold, Renaissance Madonna, society portrait
- Never autobiographical: "These are not self-portraits. These are characters I invent."
- The image is the final product — there are no negatives available, no outtakes, no process shown

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: 35mm camera, medium format, large format, digital
- **Film**: Kodak color negative (various), slide film, Polaroid
- **Lighting**: Imitates the light of whatever era she's referencing
- **Makeup/prosthetics**: Elaborate self-transformation; unrecognizable in many images
- **Printing**: C-prints, later digital prints; deliberately "aged" or period-appropriate

## Visual Signature
- **Film still aesthetic** (Untitled Film Stills, 1977–1980): Black-and-white, but her color work carries the same DNA — as if they're production stills from movies that don't exist
- **Vintage color cast**: Slight warmth, slight fade — not fresh, not modern
- **Period-specific color**: Her 1970s/80s work has the color of that era's film and printing
- **Slight grain**: 35mm originals with visible but not overwhelming grain structure
- **Institutional framing**: Centerfolds, society portraits, historical paintings — she copies the FRAMING conventions, not just the content
- **Uncanny discomfort**: Something is always slightly wrong — the makeup, the expression, the context

## Preset Design Notes
- Vintage/period feel via slight desaturation and warm shadow cast
- Subtle grain for 35mm film texture
- Green saturation pulled back (digital greens are too modern)
- No clarity, dehaze, or texture (vintage look avoids digital sharpness)
- Low contrast — film stills don't have dramatic contrast
- Documentary Blending at 50 for natural transitions
