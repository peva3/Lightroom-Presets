### FxW Recipe → Lightroom Translation

Royal Gold 400 was the "premium" consumer 400 — warmer, richer, and finer-grained than standard Gold 400. Translation priorities: warm golden cast, rich saturation, moderate-fine grain, medium-high contrast.

#### Basic Panel (from FxW JPEG settings)
- **Exposure**: +0.20 (slight brightening for consumer film baseline)
- **Contrast**: +22 (medium-high — punchy consumer premium but less than Ultramax)
- **Highlights**: -32 (warm golden highlight roll-off)
- **Shadows**: +20 (open shadows, premium characteristic)
- **Whites**: +5 (mild highlight compression)
- **Blacks**: -15 (rich black point, not crushed)

## Community Consensus

Royal Gold 400 is noted by expired film shooters for:
1. **T-Grain refinement**: The primary differentiator from standard Gold 400
2. **Premium color**: More saturated and richer than Gold 200/400
3. **Warmth without harshness**: Warmer than Portra but more refined than Ultramax
4. **Cult status**: Discontinued for ~20 years, highly sought after
