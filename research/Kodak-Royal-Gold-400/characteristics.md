# Kodak Royal Gold 400 — Aesthetic Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

## Overview

Kodak Royal Gold 400 was Kodak's premium consumer ISO 400 color negative film of the 1990s, positioned above the standard Gold 400. Part of the "Royal" line (alongside Royal Gold 100 and 200), it featured Kodak's T-Grain emulsion technology for finer grain at high speed. Discontinued in the early 2000s, it has attained cult status among film photographers seeking the "premium 90s" look. This document serves as a reference for creating a Lightroom preset that emulates Royal Gold 400.

## Core Aesthetic Signature

### Color Palette

- **Primary Color Cast**: Warm golden-biased, warmer and richer than Gold 200. The "Royal" designation indicated richer color saturation than standard Gold.
- **Reds**: Deep, rich, slightly orange-leaning. Reds have density without harshness.
- **Yellows & Oranges**: Very warm, saturated. Skin tones glow with golden warmth. More refined than Ultramax's consumer yellow.
- **Greens**: Moderately saturated, slightly warm-olive. Cleaner than standard Gold 200 green rendering.
- **Blues**: Vivid but not electric. Skies render naturally with good saturation — slightly darker luminance than neutral digital.
- **Skin Tones**: Warm golden skin tones — the defining premium consumer characteristic. Less aggressive than Ultramax, more warmth than Portra.

### Contrast & Tonality

- **Overall Contrast**: Medium-high. Punchier than Portra 400, less aggressive than Ultramax 400.
- **Highlight Roll-off**: Smooth with warm golden fringe. The T-Grain technology provided better highlight retention than traditional consumer films.
- **Shadow Detail**: Good at box speed with subtle warmth in the toe.
- **Midtone Contrast**: Strong — the "premium" character comes from well-separated midtones.

### Grain

- **Grain Level**: Moderate-fine. T-Grain technology made this the finest-grained ISO 400 consumer film of its era.
- **Grain Character**: Structured, tight, slightly more uniform than traditional cubic-grain consumer films.
- **Comparison**: Finer than Ultramax 400, slightly coarser than Portra 400.

### Exposure Behavior

- **Box Speed (ISO 400)**: Warm, punchy, rich colors with visible but controlled grain.
- **Overexposure (+1 stop, EI 200)**: Warmer, slightly pastel highlights, reduced grain, more open shadows.
- **Underexposure (-1 stop)**: Grain becomes prominent, shadows block up with warm cast.

## Summary

```
Warm golden daylight balance (slightly warmer than Gold 200)
Medium-high contrast with smooth highlight roll-off
Richly saturated reds, oranges, yellows
Moderate fine T-Grain (finer than standard consumer 400)
Premium midtone separation — the "Royal" difference
```
