### FxW Recipe → Lightroom Translation

Max 800 was the gritty high-speed consumer film — heavy grain, high contrast, amber warm cast. Translation priorities: aggressive contrast, heavy grain, warm amber tone, limited shadow detail (period-accurate).

#### Basic Panel
- **Exposure**: +0.10 (slight boost for consumer baseline)
- **Contrast**: +30 (high contrast — fast consumer film)
- **Highlights**: -25 (limited highlight latitude — hard clip characteristic)
- **Shadows**: +10 (shadow detail is limited — don't over-lift)
- **Whites**: +10 (bright specular highlights typical of flash photography)
- **Blacks**: -25 (deep consumer blacks, characteristically crushed)

#### Color Grading
- **Highlights**: Hue 42°, Sat 15 (warm amber highlights — flash characteristic)
- **Shadows**: Hue 200°, Sat 10 (slight cool shadow contrast)
- **Blending**: 50
- **Balance**: +15 (warm biased)

#### Grain (STYLEGUIDE consumer color neg: Amount 35-50, but Max 800 is extreme: Size 35-40, Roughness 55-65)
- **Amount**: 50 (heavy consumer 800-speed grain)
- **Size**: 40 (larger grain particles for high speed)
- **Roughness**: 65 (irregular — pushed consumer film character)

#### STYLEGUIDE Compliance
- No Calibration
- No Temperature/Tint
- Sharpness=10, Clarity=0, Texture=0, Dehaze=0 (grain protection)
- LuminanceSmoothing=0
- Vibrance +3, Saturation -2 (gap = 5 — at boundary)
- Blacks -25 ≥ -30

## Community Consensus

Max 800 is the rarest of the Kodak MAX line, noted for:
1. **Extreme grain**: Even by consumer 800 standards, Max 800 is gritty
2. **Flash photography heritage**: Designed specifically for flash use at parties/events
3. **Amber cast**: More amber-toned than Ultramax 400
4. **Limited latitude**: High speed + consumer emulsion = narrow exposure window
5. **Collector status**: Very few properly stored rolls remain
