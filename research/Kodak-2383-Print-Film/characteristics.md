# Kodak 2383 Print Film — Characteristics

## Film Background
Kodak Vision Color Print Film 2383 is the industry-standard print film for cinema. When you watch a movie in a theater, you're seeing light projected through this film. It replaced 2393 and has been the dominant print stock for decades.

### Physical Properties
- Polyester base (ESTAR) — incredibly durable
- Designed for contact printing from color intermediate negative
- Process: ECP-2D (standard color print film process)
- Sound track: cyan dye-only analog track (high magenta density)

### Color Science
The 2383 print film creates a specific "look" that's become synonymous with cinema:
1. **Warm highlights**: Projector lamps (~3200K xenon) filter through the print stock's warm D-min
2. **Cool cyan-green shadows**: The dye set creates complementary shadow colors
3. **Subtractive saturation**: Unlike digital additive color, film's dye clouds create density-dependent saturation
4. **Smooth rolloffs**: Both the toe (shadow detail) and shoulder (highlight compression) have gradual transitions

## Key Aesthetic
- The definitive "film look" for cinema
- Warm, golden highlights (D55/D60 white point)
- Cool cyan-green shadows for depth and color contrast
- Aggressive but smooth S-curve contrast
- Rich, dense blacks without crushing
- Gentle highlight compression
