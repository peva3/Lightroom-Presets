### FxW Recipe → Lightroom Translation

NPH was the professional portrait/wedding film preceding Pro 400H — low contrast, neutral skin tones, very fine grain. Translation priorities: ultra-low contrast, desaturated greens/reds, clean skin, wide latitude aesthetic.

#### Basic Panel
- **Exposure**: +0.25 (slight overexposure — portrait sweet spot)
- **Contrast**: -10 (ultra-low contrast — the wedding photographer's film)
- **Highlights**: -40 (long highlight shoulder — white dresses hold detail)
- **Shadows**: +25 (open shadows — black tuxedos retain texture)
- **Whites**: -5 (gentle white point)
- **Blacks**: +10 (lifted black point — never crush for portraits)

#### Color Grading
- **Highlights**: Hue 42°, Sat 5 (very subtle warm highlight)
- **Shadows**: Hue 50°, Sat 8 (warm brown shadow — not teal, for skin-tone safety)
- **Blending**: 50
- **Balance**: -15 (shadow bias for gentle tonality)

#### Grain (Fine professional: Amount 15-25)
- **Amount**: 20 (very fine — professional portrait grain)
- **Size**: 22
- **Roughness**: 45

#### STYLEGUIDE Compliance
- No Calibration
- No Temperature/Tint
- Sharpness=10, Clarity=0, Texture=0, Dehaze=0 (grain protection)
- LuminanceSmoothing=0
- Vibrance 0, Saturation 0 (gap = 0)
- Blacks +10 ≥ -30

## Community Consensus

NPH is known by wedding photographers who shot film:
1. **Low contrast is the defining trait**: Flat negatives for maximum printing flexibility
2. **Skin tone priority**: All color decisions are subordinate to skin rendering
3. **The 400H predecessor**: Pro 400H evolved from NPH's formula, adding 4th color layer
4. **Overexposure handling**: Regularly shot at EI 200 for luminous portraits
5. **Green desaturation**: Wedding foliage backgrounds tamed to keep focus on subjects
6. **Muted reds**: Wedding reds (flowers, dresses) desaturated to prevent skin tone clash
