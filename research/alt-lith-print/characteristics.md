# Lith Print Lightroom Preset — Complete Aesthetic Breakdown

## Core Aesthetic Signature

The lith print Lightroom preset recreates this look digitally using Lightroom's color grading tools for authentic film emulation. Lith printing is a specialized darkroom technique using conventional black-and-white photographic paper developed in highly dilute lithographic film developer (usually Kodak Lith A+B or Moersch EasyLith). The dilute developer creates 'infectious development' — once a silver grain starts developing, it catalyzes development of adjacent grains at an accelerated rate. This produces uniquely gritty shadow texture, warm highlight tones, and color shifts in the print depending on paper type, developer dilution, and development time.

## Historical Context

**Era:** 1970s–present (discovered by Anton Corbijn; refined by Tim Rudman)

B&W photographic paper → exposed normally → developed in highly dilute lith developer (1:10 to 1:40 dilution) for 5–20 minutes → 'snatch point' where shadow development accelerates exponentially → stopped suddenly in water → fixed normally. Infectious development in shadows creates the characteristic gritty 'pepper grain' texture.

## Visual Characteristics

Infectious development shadow grain — uniquely gritty, clustered texture in dark areas (not uniform like standard grain). Warm highlight tones (peach, cream, pink depending on paper). Cool or neutral midtones. Extremely steep transition at the 'snatch point' between developed shadows and undeveloped highlights. Overall image can be warm-brown, neutral, or even slightly cool depending on paper and chemistry.

## Emotional & Artistic Vocabulary

Dark, dramatic, painterly. Lith prints have a graphic, almost woodcut quality. The gritty shadows suggest decay, texture, and materiality.

## Technical Notes for Digital Emulation

This preset is designed to capture the essential visual character of the lith print process using Lightroom's slider-based toolset. It prioritizes the emotional/aesthetic impact over perfect chemical accuracy, which would require a 3D LUT or DCP camera profile.

### Limitations of Slider-Based Simulation
- True process-specific spectral sensitivity cannot be replicated with sliders
- Chemical stain patterns are simulated through grain and color grading
- Paper/plate texture is approximated through grain settings
