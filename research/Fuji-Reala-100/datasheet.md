## The 4th Color Layer (Fourth Layer Technology)

Fuji Reala 100 was the **last consumer C-41 film** to feature a **fourth color layer** — a cyan-sensitive emulsion layer in addition to the standard red-, green-, and blue-sensitive layers.

### How It Worked

Conventional color negative film uses three emulsion layers, each sensitive to one third of the visible spectrum:
- Blue-sensitive layer → forms yellow dye
- Green-sensitive layer → forms magenta dye
- Red-sensitive layer → forms cyan dye

Reala added a **4th cyan-sensitive layer** that was spectrally tuned to correct for the imperfect color separation of traditional three-layer films. This addressed a fundamental problem: the red-sensitive layer in conventional film has some unwanted sensitivity in the green region, causing color contamination (particularly affecting greens, cyans, and skin tones under fluorescent lighting).

### Technical Advantages

1. **Improved color separation** — The 4th layer captured color information that three-layer films could not, reducing metamerism (where two colors look the same in photos but different to the eye).

2. **Superior fluorescent light handling** — Under standard three-layer film, fluorescent lighting creates a green cast because the film's red-sensitive layer picks up unwanted green wavelengths. Reala's 4th cyan-sensitive layer compensated for this, delivering natural color under mixed lighting including fluorescent.

3. **Better reproduction of subtle greens, cyans, and purples** — Colors that are notoriously difficult for three-layer film to distinguish accurately were rendered with greater fidelity.

4. **More accurate skin tones** — The improved spectral sensitivity meant that the subtle pink/peach hues in skin (which depend on accurate red-cyan balance) were reproduced more faithfully.

### Technical Note

- The 4th layer was not completely unique to Reala — Fuji's pro films (NPS 160, NPH 400, NPC 160) also used 4th layer technology. However, Reala was the last **consumer-grade** C-41 film to carry it.
- Fuji Superia X-TRA 400 retained a 4th layer for a time, but the newest Fuji Superia datasheets show the **cyan layer removed** from spectral sensitivity curves (as noted by /r/AnalogCommunity users comparing old vs. new datasheets in 2025).
- Kodak never adopted 4th layer technology in their consumer or professional C-41 films.

## Why It Was Discontinued

- Declining film sales through the 2000s
- By ~2013, Fujifilm had shifted focus to Instax (instant film) as their primary analog revenue stream
- The complex 4-layer coating made Reala more expensive to manufacture than three-layer films
- As noted by Photrio community members: Fuji is "one of the best-managed companies in Japan" and very "hard-eyed about not spending resources on low or no-profit stuff"

## Known Spectral Characteristics

- **RMS Granularity**: ~4 (very fine grain for a 100-speed film)
- **Color balance**: Daylight (5500°K)
- **Contrast**: Moderate — lower contrast than Ektar 100, higher than Portra 160
- **Latitude**: Good (approximately -1 to +3 stops, best at box speed or +1)
- **Sharpness**: High
