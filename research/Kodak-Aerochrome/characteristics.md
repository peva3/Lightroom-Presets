# Kodak Aerochrome / Ektachrome EIR — Aesthetic Characteristics

## The Core Palette

Aerochrome produces a unique **false-color rendering** where the color assignments are deliberately alien to human vision:

| Subject | Actual Color (to eye) | Aerochrome Renders As |
|---------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Healthy vegetation** (chlorophyll-rich) | Green | **Magenta, hot pink, crimson, neon red** |
| **Stressed/dying vegetation** | Yellow-green, brown | Muted magenta, purple-brown |
| **Sky** | Blue | **Cyan, cerulean, turquoise** |
| **Water** | Blue/clear | **Deep indigo, navy, near-black** |
| **Clouds** | White | **White to pale pink** |
| **Soil, rocks, concrete** | Brown, gray | **Cream, pale yellow, off-white** |
| **Human skin** | Varies | **Pale waxy yellow, ghostlike cream** |
| **Red objects** | Red | Shifted toward orange/amber |
| **Blue objects** | Blue | Shifted toward cyan/deep teal |

## Why This Happens

The false-color is a consequence of the 3-layer IR emulsion structure:

1. **Healthy foliage reflects IR intensely** (chlorophyll effect at 700–900 nm) → exposes the **IR-sensitive cyan layer** → rendered as **red/magenta** in the positive.
2. **Clear sky scatters blue light** (blocked by filter) but also reflects some green → exposes the **yellow-forming layer** minimally → sky renders as **cyan/blue**.
3. **Non-vegetative surfaces** reflect IR weakly → mostly expose the red and green layers → rendered as **cream/white/neutral** tones.

## Key Aesthetic Qualities

### 1. Magenta Foliage
- The #1 defining characteristic of every Aerochrome image.
- Healthy, chlorophyll-rich vegetation glows **hot magenta to neon pink**.
- Deciduous trees, grass, crops, and tropical foliage are the most dramatic.
- Conifers (pine, spruce) reflect less IR → appear more **purple-magenta** than bright pink.
- Autumn leaves (less chlorophyll) render less magenta — more muted tones.
- The glow is **luminous** — almost appears backlit even in flat light.

### 2. Cyan/Turquoise Skies
- Blue sky turns a distinct **cerulean cyan or turquoise**.
- Very different from normal blue — it's a synthetic, surreal teal that immediately signals "this is not a normal photograph."
- Overcast sky → pale cyan-white. Stormy sky → deep indigo-cyan.
- Clouds remain white but take on a slight pink edge glow.

### 3. Extreme Color Separation
- Colors are **maximally separated** — green and blue do not blend into a natural palette.
- Vegetation pops violently against cyan skies — maximum contrast between subject and background.
- The separation is so extreme it reads as surreal, alien, or video-game-like.

### 4. Cream/Pale Non-Vegetative Objects
- Dirt, rocks, concrete, buildings, roads → pale cream to warm off-white.
- Creates an eerie negative-space effect — the built environment looks ghostly and unreal.
- This contributes heavily to the "dreamlike" quality.

### 5. Black/Navy Water
- Water absorbs IR almost completely → renders as deep navy, indigo, or pure black.
- Ponds, lakes, rivers become inky voids in the landscape.
- Creates dramatic contrast with magenta vegetation on shorelines.

### 6. Waxy/Ghostly Skin Tones
- Human skin renders as pale cream-yellow, waxy, slightly translucent-looking.
- Portraits on Aerochrome look **unsettling and alien** — not flattering, intentionally surreal.
- Eyes can look dark/black with pale irises.

### 7. Wood Effect (Enhanced Foliage in Sunlight)
- Foliage in direct sun glows with **maximum IR reflectance**.
- The "wood effect" — named after infrared photography pioneer Robert W. Wood — is the intense luminosity.
- Diffuse/overcast light produces a more muted but still dramatic effect.

## Emotional/Perceptual Impact

- **Surrealism**: The image looks real but the colors are fundamentally wrong → cognitive dissonance.
- **Alien landscape**: Familiar scenes become unfamiliar — Earth looks like another planet.
- **Dream/nightmare quality**: The palette mimics how some people experience dreams or psychedelic states.
- **Hyper-visibility**: Vegetation health becomes immediately legible — healthy = magenta, stressed = dull.
- **Military reconnaissance aesthetic**: This was the film's original purpose — identifying camouflaged objects by detecting live vegetation vs. dead material or paint.

## Comparison: True-Color IR vs. Aerochrome False-Color

| | True-Color IR (e.g., digital IR with hot mirror removed) | Aerochrome False-Color |
|---|---|---|
| Foliage | Red/rust, sometimes pink | **Magenta, hot pink, neon red** |
| Sky | Deep blue to near-black | **Cyan, turquoise, cerulean** |
| Palette feel | "Martian red" landscapes | "Candy-colored alien dreamscape" |
| Skin tones | Pale, slightly yellow | **Waxy, ghostly, unsettling** |

## Notable Example Descriptions from r/analog

- **Yosemite Valley** (2,992 pts): Granite cliffs cream-white against magenta pine forests and cyan skies. The iconic valley becomes unrecognizably alien.
- **Botanical Gardens, Sydney** (2,681 pts): Tropical foliage explodes in pink/magenta; architecture renders pale cream; shadows are deep navy.
- **The Dolomites on Aerochrome** (3,273 pts): Jagged limestone peaks appear cream/pale against hot magenta alpine meadows under turquoise sky.
- **Swiss Avenue** (2,604 pts): Suburban trees in Dallas transformed into a psychedelic fever dream — manicured lawns become neon pink carpets.
- **Autumn/fall foliage** consistently reported as the most photogenic subject — leaf color variation creates magenta-to-pink-to-purple gradients.
