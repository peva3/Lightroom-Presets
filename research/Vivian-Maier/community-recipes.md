# Community Recipes — Vivian Maier

## Known Approaches

### From r/streetphotography and r/analog
- Square crop (1:1) is often cited as essential to the Maier aesthetic — the composition is designed for it
- Rolleiflex TLR waist-level finder gave her a unique perspective (shooting from chest height)
- Self-portrait through reflections is widely attempted by fans

### From YouTube Tutorials
- Medium format tonal richness is the goal — smooth gradients, rich midtones
- Contrast moderate — not as flat as Cartier-Bresson, not as punchy as Adams
- Grain present but fine (Rolleiflex negatives were larger, grain less apparent)

### From Flickr/Social Media Groups
- Maier-inspired groups emphasize the square frame + street subject combination
- Reflection/self-portrait composites are popular homage techniques

### Community Consensus
- The look is more about composition (square, chest-level, reflections) than processing
- Tonal balance should favor midtones over extremes
- Grain should be fine-medium format, not pushed-35mm
