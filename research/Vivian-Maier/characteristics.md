# Vivian Maier

## Biography
- **Name**: Vivian Dorothy Maier (1926–2009)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Era**: 1950s–1990s (discovered posthumously 2007)

## Philosophy
- Compulsive street photographer; nanny by profession, artist in secret
- Never exhibited or published during her lifetime
- Obsessive documentarian of urban life — Chicago, New York, world travel
- Self-portraits via reflections and shadows were a recurring motif
- ~150,000 negatives discovered at auction

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: Rolleiflex 2.8F TLR (medium format square), later Leica IIIc
- **Film**: Kodak Tri-X, Verichrome Pan, Ilford, various color films later
- **Format**: Predominantly 6x6 square (Rolleiflex)
- **Processing**: Standard commercial processing; no special techniques

## Visual Signature
- **Square format aesthetic**: Central compositions, balanced geometries
- **Rich midtones**: Medium format delivered smooth tonal transitions
- **Self-portraits in reflections**: Mirrors, windows, chrome surfaces
- **Street encounters**: Direct but unobtrusive, subjects rarely pose
- **Deep shadows with detail**: Shadow regions retain information, not crushed
- **Warm presence**: Even her B&W work has a human warmth through tonal balance

## Iconic Images
- Self-portrait with mirror (1950s)
- Chicago street scenes with children
- New York street photography

## Preset Design Notes
- Medium format tonal richness — moderate contrast, balanced GrayMixer
- Slight bias toward warmer grays via Orange/Red push in mixer
- Subtle grain (medium format Tri-X is finer than 35mm)
- Shadows preserved, not crushed
