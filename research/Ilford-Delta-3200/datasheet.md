# Ilford Delta 3200 — Official Datasheet Summary

> **Source:** Ilford Photo (Harman Technology) — Delta 3200 Professional Technical Data Sheet F25, July 2018 revision.

---

## Film Type

- **Name:** ILFORD DELTA 3200 PROFESSIONAL
- **Type:** Panchromatic black & white negative film
- **Nominal sensitivity:** EI 3200/36° (ISO 3200)
- **True ISO speed:** **~ISO 1000–1250** (ISO speed measured per ISO 5800 standard)
  - Delta 3200 is a nominally ISO 1000 film designed to be push-processed to EI 3200
  - The ISO on the box (3200) is the _recommended exposure index_, not the measured ISO speed
- **Technology:** Core-Shell™ silver halide crystal technology (epitaxial grain structure)
- **Formats available:** 35mm (36 exp), 120 roll film
- **Base:** 0.125mm acetate (35mm) / 0.110mm clear polyester (120)
- **Anti-halation:** Integral dye — magenta/purple tint washes out during processing

---

## Spectral Sensitivity

- **Panchromatic** — sensitised to all visible wavelengths
- Extended red sensitivity (to ~660nm)
- Slightly reduced green sensitivity relative to human eye (orthopanchromatic bias)
- No UV or IR sensitivity beyond normal panchromatic range

---

## Characteristic Curve (Density vs. Log Exposure)

_Delta 3200 has a characteristically long, straight-line curve with a soft toe and extended shoulder — designed to maintain shadow detail in extreme low light while compressing highlights gently._

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Curve shape | Long straight-line with soft toe |
| D-min (base+fog) | ~0.10–0.15 |
| Contrast index (CI) | ~0.56 (ISO developer) |
| Gamma | ~0.65–0.70 |
| Latitude | ~±2 stops at box speed |
| Shoulder onset | Relatively early — highlights roll off gently |

**Key curve characteristics at EI 3200:**
- Shadows fall on the toe of the curve → crushed blacks
- Midtones sit on the steep straight-line portion → good tonal separation
- Highlights enter the shoulder early → soft, never blown whites

## Grain Characteristics

- **RMS Granularity:** ~20–22 (measured at ISO 1000)
  - For comparison: Delta 100 = ~7, HP5+ = ~15, Delta 3200 = ~20
  - When pushed to EI 3200, effective grain increases significantly
- **Grain morphology:** Core-Shell epitaxial crystals — tabular/semi-tabular with cubic components
- **Visual appearance:** Pronounced, "golf-ball-sized" grain especially at EI 3200+
- Grain is most visible in midtones (Zone V–VII) and becomes a dominant aesthetic feature
- In 35mm, grain is unmistakable and defines the film's identity
- In 120 medium format, grain is present but significantly less intrusive

## Storage & Handling

- Store at 13°C or lower in original packaging
- Load/unload in **total darkness** (not just subdued light)
- High-speed films are more susceptible to cosmic radiation — avoid long-term storage
- Process as soon as possible after exposure
- Ideal for situations where flash is prohibited (concerts, museums, night architecture)

---

## Key Technical Takeaways for Digital Emulation

1. **True ISO is ~1000** — the film is engineered to be pushed. Shadow detail is already compromised at box speed.
2. **Grain is massive and intentional** — it's a feature, not a flaw. Don't smooth it out.
3. **The characteristic curve has a soft toe and early shoulder** — blacks crush, highlights roll off gently. This means low global contrast with compressed dynamic range.
4. **DD-X and Microphen** are the recommended developers — they optimise speed while maintaining tonal range.
5. **Push to 6400+** dramatically increases grain and contrast with further shadow loss.
