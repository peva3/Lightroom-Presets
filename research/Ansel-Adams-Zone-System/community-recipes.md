# Community Recipes — Ansel Adams Zone System

## Known Approaches

### From r/analog and r/photography
- Red filter simulation in B&W conversion is the most commonly discussed Adams technique
- Users report `Blue -60 to -80` and `Red +30 to +50` as standard red filter emulation
- Some use a physical red filter on digital, then convert to B&W — captures the lens flare too

### From YouTube Tutorials
- Jamie Windsor (800K+): Recommends blue channel reduction for sky, with judicious contrast
- Thomas Heaton: Pushes contrast moderately, warns against making everything Ansel Adams dramatic

### From Fuji X Weekly / Analog Emulation Sites
- Acros + Red filter is the closest in-camera starting point
- Some add grain for texture but note Adams hated grain (large format negated it)

### Community Consensus
- Full tonal range is the goal — no clipping at either end
- Red filter effect through color mixer is preferred over physical filter for digital
- Shadows should be deep but retain texture (Zone I–II, not Zone 0)
- The magic is in the midtone separation, not the extremes
