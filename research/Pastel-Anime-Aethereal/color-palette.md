# Color Palette — Pastel Anime Aethereal

Anime art colour theory, key visual palettes, hex codes, and the pigment origins behind the Pastel Anime Aethereal look.

---

## 1. The Ghibli Colour DNA

The Pastel Anime Aethereal photo grade directly inherits its colour logic from Studio Ghibli's background painting tradition. Background art director **Kazuo Oga** (*My Neighbor Totoro, Spirited Away, Princess Mononoke*) painted with poster colour (gouache) using a deliberately limited palette.

### Oga's Principle

> "With a small number of colours, you can create a wide range of colours through subtle mixing." — Kazuo Oga

He used approximately **21–24 colours** total, not 100+. Restraint creates harmony.

## 3. Anime Key Visual Colour Theory

The "anime key visual" (アニメキービジュアル) refers to the primary promotional illustration for an anime film or series. These have consistent colour conventions that inform the Pastel Anime Aethereal photo grade:

### The Key Visual Formula

| Element | Colour Treatment | Hex Range |
|---------|-----------------|-----------|
| Sky | Bright cyan to teal, often with lens flare or light bloom | `#5BC0EB` – `#8DD7F7` |
| Clouds | White with warm peach/pink underside | `#FFF5EE` – `#FFDAB9` |
| Grass / Foliage | Vibrant yellow-green to mint | `#7EC850` – `#98D89E` |
| Shadows | Purple or blue-purple tint, never black | `#6B5B8A` – `#8B7DAF` |
| Skin | Warm peach, slightly desaturated | `#FDDCC6` – `#FCE4D4` |
| Highlights / Rim Light | Warm gold or cool cyan depending on time-of-day | `#FFE5A3` or `#C5F0FF` |
| Deepest Darks | Dark purple-brown, not pure #000000 | `#2D1B2E` – `#2E1E2A` |

### Makoto Shinkai Palette (Your Name, Weathering With You, Suzume)

Shinkai's films are the most direct reference for the teal-sky / pink-shadow half of this aesthetic:

- **Sky:** Aggressively cyan `#4AB8E8` with lens flare bloom to `#A8E6FF`
- **Clouds:** Volumetric, warm underside tint `#FFD4B8`
- **Golden Hour:** Warm orange-pink bloom `#FF8C69` to `#FFB89A`
- **Shadow Colour:** Blue-purple `#5B6E9E` to `#7080B0`
- **Night Sky:** Deep purple-blue with visible stars `#1A1A3E` graded to `#3D2B5A`
- **City Lights at Night:** Warm orange `#FF9E60` against cool purple shadows `#4B3A6E`

## 6. The Colour Wheel Logic

The Pastel Anime Aethereal palette is built on a **split-complementary** colour structure:

```
PRIMARY WARM AXIS:
  Pink (H345) → Peach (H30) → Gold (H45)
  (skin, highlights, warm shadows)

PRIMARY COOL AXIS:
  Teal (H195) → Mint (H150) → Lavender (H260)
  (sky, foliage, cool shadow tint)

CONTRAST PAIR:
  Teal Sky (H195)  ←→  Pink Shadow (H345)
  (~150° apart on colour wheel — near-complementary)

SECONDARY HARMONY:
  Mint Green (H150)  ←→  Soft Pink (H345)
  (~195° apart — split-complementary tension)
```

### Saturation Map

| Zone | Saturation Level | Reason |
|------|-----------------|--------|
| Sky | Moderate (S 40–60) | Needs to read as "teal" without looking artificial |
| Greenery | Low-Moderate (S 25–45) | Pastel = desaturated from real green |
| Shadows | Low (S 10–25) | Subtle tint, not obvious colour cast |
| Skin | Low-Moderate (S 15–30) | Natural, not orange |
| Highlights | Low (S 10–20) | Near-white with subtle warmth |

### Luminance Map

| Zone | Luminance Level | Reason |
|------|----------------|--------|
| Sky | High (L 65–85) | Bright, anime-style sky |
| Greenery | High-Mid (L 55–75) | Lit foliage, not dark forest |
| Shadows | Low-Mid (L 15–35) | Lifted, never pure black |
| Skin | High (L 75–90) | Bright, glowing skin |
| Highlights | Very High (L 85–98) | Near-clipping but retained |

## 7. Pigment to Pixel: Why Gouache Matters

The Pastel Anime Aethereal look is not just "teal and pink sliders." Its soft, matte, lifted quality comes from the material properties of **gouache / poster colour**:

1. **No true black** — Gouache's darkest tone is about 85–90% density (not 100% like acrylic or oil). This maps to the lifted black point in the Lightroom grade.

2. **Matte finish** — No specular highlights. Gouache light areas are pale opaque paint, not shiny reflections. This maps to compressed highlights in Lightroom.

3. **Limited palette** — Oga's ~21-colour restriction means every colour in the frame is related by mixing. This creates natural colour harmony that a digital grade must simulate through global HSL shifts.

4. **Wet-into-wet blending** — Soft, diffuse colour transitions with no hard edges. This maps to negative clarity, negative dehaze, and the "soft" quality of the grade.

5. **White mixing desaturates** — In gouache, adding white (poster colour White #51) cools and desaturates any pigment. This is the physical origin of the "pastel" look — literally, pastel = pigment + white.

## 8. AI Prompt Colour Vocabulary

The "anime key visual" prompt aesthetic in Midjourney/DALL-E provides a vocabulary that has fed back into photo editing:

### Common Prompt Colour Terms (and what they produce)

| Prompt Term | Visual Effect |
|-------------|---------------|
| "anime key visual" | High-contrast hero shot composition, bright saturated colours with distinct rim light |
| "pastel colour palette" | Soft, desaturated, pink/mint/lavender dominant |
| "teal sky" | Cyan-shifted blue sky with volumetric clouds |
| "warm beige/mint tones" | Earthy warmth in buildings/ground, cool mint in nature |
| "glossy idol-card aesthetic" | High-key bright, pink-dominant, shiny highlights |
| "luminous blue palette" | Cyan/teal dominant with glow effects |
| "Studio Ghibli background" | Soft gouache-like tonality, warm/cool balance, natural colours |
| "Makoto Shinkai sky" | Hyper-saturated cyan blue with dramatic golden hour light |

### The "Anime Key Visual" Template

From AI prompt databases (PromptBase, Hugging Face datasets, Midjourney community), the standard anime key visual prompt template consistently requests:

> "anime key visual, [subject], teal sky background, lush landscape, pastel colour palette, soft shadows, painterly digital illustration, trending pixiv fanbox"

This template has directly influenced the vocabulary photographers and editors use to describe the Pastel Anime Aethereal look.
