### Recipe → Lightroom Translation

50D is the ultra-clean daylight cinema stock — near-grainless, low contrast, pristine color. Translation priorities: very low contrast (flattest Vision3), microscopic grain, clean daylight color, gentle highlight shoulder.

#### Basic Panel
- **Exposure**: +0.30 (slight brightness for ISO 50 baseline)
- **Contrast**: +8 (very low — 50D is the flattest Vision3 stock)
- **Highlights**: -35 (very long shoulder — near-impossible to blow)
- **Shadows**: +25 (excellent shadow detail — low base fog)
- **Whites**: -10 (gentle white compression)
- **Blacks**: -8 (very gentle — near-zero base fog means clean blacks)

#### Color Grading
- **Highlights**: Hue 45°, Sat 5 (very subtle warmth)
- **Shadows**: Hue 210°, Sat 5 (very subtle cool shadow)
- **Blending**: 50
- **Balance**: +10 (slight warm bias)

#### Grain (Slide-like — near-invisible: Amount 0-15)
- **Amount**: 12 (near-invisible — finest cinema stock)
- **Size**: 15 (microscopic)
- **Roughness**: 40 (very regular)

#### STYLEGUIDE Compliance
- No Calibration
- No Temperature/Tint
- Sharpness=10, Clarity=0, Texture=0, Dehaze=0 (grain protection)
- LuminanceSmoothing=0
- Vibrance 0, Saturation -3 (gap = 3)
- Blacks -8 ≥ -30

## Community Consensus

50D is highly regarded in both cinema and stills communities:
1. **"The medium format of motion picture"**: 50D's grainlessness approaches large format quality
2. **Exposure flexibility**: Can overexpose 6+ stops without highlight clipping
3. **Color purity**: Most accurate color of any Vision3 stock — used for commercial/product work
4. **Flat baseline**: 50D is designed for maximum post-production flexibility — grading adds the "look"
5. **Still photography crossover**: Popular among stills photographers who process ECN-2 at home

## Cinematography.com Insights

- "50D doesn't push well — it's too flat. The push mostly adds grain in shadows." (cinematographer consensus)
- "50D is for controlled lighting. It's too slow for available light unless you're in direct sun."
- "The cleanest film I've ever shot. It makes digital look noisy."
