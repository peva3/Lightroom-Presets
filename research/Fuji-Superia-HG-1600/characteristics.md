# Fuji Superia HG 1600 — Film Characteristics

> "High Grade" Superia variant — premium high-speed consumer film. Finer grain than standard Superia 1600, better skin tones, less green shadow cast. Fuji's answer to demanding low-light color negative photography.

## Historical Context

Superia HG 1600 was introduced as the premium tier in Fuji's high-speed consumer line. The "HG" (High Grade) designation indicated superior grain structure, improved color reproduction, and better skin tone rendering compared to the standard Superia 1600. It was positioned between consumer Superia and professional Fuji Pro films.

The film was particularly popular in Japan and Asia, where it competed with Kodak's high-speed offerings. The 4th color layer technology (shared across the Superia line) provided better color accuracy under fluorescent and mixed lighting — a key selling point for indoor and evening photography.

Discontinued as part of Fuji's film line consolidation in the late 2000s/early 2010s.

## Key Color Signatures

1. **Grain**: The defining HG advantage — noticeably finer than standard 1600.
2. **Skin tones**: Improved warmth vs standard Superia. Less pale, more natural.
3. **Shadow color**: Reduced green cast. Neutralized toward cool (not green).
4. **Greens**: Fuji teal shift present but milder than standard Superia 1600.
5. **Contrast**: Consumer-grade moderate contrast, punchy but not harsh.
6. **Blues**: Clean, slightly saturated cyan skies — classic Fuji.

## Fuji Superia Family Color Palette

The Superia family shares a recognizable color signature:
- **Greens**: Shifted toward teal/cyan (Fuji's 4th color layer technology)
- **Blues**: Clean, cyan-leaning (not warm/cobalt like Kodak)
- **Shadows**: Cool with slight green/cyan bias
- **Skin**: Neutral to slightly cool (warmer in HG variants)
- **Overall**: Clean, modern, slightly cool balance
