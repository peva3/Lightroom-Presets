# Fuji Superia HG 1600 — Community Recipes

> Compiled from t3mujinpack .dtstyle reference data, Reddit (r/analog, r/Lightroom), and photography forums. The "High Grade" Superia variant offers finer grain and better skin tones than the standard 1600.

---

## t3mujinpack .dtstyle Reference

The t3mujinpack collection includes Superia HG 1600 as a Darktable style. Key characteristics extracted:

- **Purpose**: Premium high-speed consumer film emulation
- **Profile base**: Standard color matrix with Fuji cool bias
- **Grain**: Finer structure than standard Superia 1600 (HG designation)
- **Color**: More balanced than standard Superia — reduced green shadow cast
- **Skin tones**: Warmer rendering than standard Superia

## Community Consensus Values

### Contrast & Tone
- Contrast: +8 to +12 (consumer-grade moderate punch)
- Blacks: 0 to +5 (moderate, not lifted)
- Highlights: -40 to -50
- Shadows: +25 to +35
- Exposure: +0.20 to +0.35 (slightly bright for shadow detail)

### Color & Saturation
- Global Saturation: +3 to +8 (moderate consumer film saturation)
- Vibrance: 0 to +5 (within 5 of Saturation)
- Green Saturation: -15 (Fuji green suppression)
- Blue Saturation: +5 to +10 (Fuji cyan skies)
- Aqua Saturation: -5 to -10 (water/sky control)
- Red Saturation: -3 to -5 (mild red suppression)

### Color Grading
- Highlights: Neutral-warm (Hue 35-45°, Sat 5-10)
- Shadows: Cool (Hue 190-210°, Sat 4-8) — less green than standard Superia
- Blending: 50

### Grain
- Amount: 35-45 (ISO 1600, HG = finer)
- Size: 30-35 (smaller than standard)
- Roughness: 45-55

## Key Differentiator from Standard Superia 1600

The HG variant's primary improvements:
1. **Finer grain**: Grain size reduced ~20% vs standard
2. **Warmer skin**: Orange channel warmth +5 to +8 vs standard
3. **Neutralized shadows**: Green shadow cast reduced by ~50%
4. **Better mixed-light**: Improved color accuracy under fluorescent/WB variation
