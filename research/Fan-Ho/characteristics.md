# Fan Ho

## Biography
- **Name**: Fan Ho (1931–2016)
- **Nationality**: Hong Kong
- **Era**: 1950s–1960s (active photography), later film director

## Philosophy
- "The beauty of light and shadow" — geometric light as primary subject
- Hong Kong as a canvas of intersecting lines, beams, and silhouettes
- Patient waiting: sometimes hours for the right light shaft or pedestrian
- Photography as poetry — each image a haiku of urban geometry
- Later became an acclaimed film director

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: Rolleiflex TLR (medium format, square)
- **Film**: Kodak Tri-X, Ilford FP4
- **Processing**: Heavy darkroom manipulation — compositing, burning/dodging
- **Printing**: Known to composite multiple negatives, dramatically darken skies, add light beams in darkroom

## Visual Signature
- **Extreme geometric contrast**: Backlit silhouettes against bright geometric light shafts
- **Deepest possible blacks**: Large portions of Zone 0 — pure black with no detail
- **Luminous geometric light**: Shafts of light cutting through dark urban canyons
- **Silhouette as subject**: People reduced to graphic shapes within light geometry
- **Minimal midtones**: Images live in blacks and highlights; midtones are transitional
- **Smoke/haze as light tracer**: Incense, steam, and street smoke give light beams physical presence

## Iconic Images
- *Approaching Shadow* (1954)
- *Private* (1960)
- *On the Stage of Life* (1954)

## Preset Design Notes
- Highest contrast in the collection — deep blacks against bright highlights
- Blue channel pulled very negative for dramatic sky darkening
- Minimal shadow lift — shadows should GO black
- Strong whites push for light beam luminosity
- Grain adds texture to large dark areas where sensor noise would be ugly
