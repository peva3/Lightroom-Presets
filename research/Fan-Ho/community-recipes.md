# Community Recipes — Fan Ho

## Known Approaches

### From r/analog and r/photography
- Fan Ho's heavy darkroom manipulation is well-documented — this isn't "straight" photography
- Multiple negative compositing was common in his work
- Users discuss how to achieve the geometric light effect through dodging/burning

### From YouTube Tutorials
- "Fan Ho style" tutorials focus on:
  - Heavy vignetting to isolate light beams
  - Crushing blacks aggressively
  - Using radial gradients to simulate light shafts
  - Negative dehaze for smoke/haze effect (some tutorials do the opposite and add dehaze)

### From Instagram/Social Media
- Hong Kong street photographers regularly cite Fan Ho as inspiration
- The geometric light + silhouette combination is widely imitated
- Fog/mist in Hong Kong street shots is a key element

### Community Consensus
- Few presets attempt the Fan Ho look — it relies heavily on the image content (light beams, fog, geometry)
- The preset works best as a contrast/shadow base; local adjustments (radial filters) are needed for the light beam effect
- Black point must be aggressive — Fan Ho's shadows are genuinely black
