# Kodak Recording Film 2475 — Community Recipes

## Community Notes (from forums)
- No dedicated Fuji X Weekly or t3mujinpack recipe exists for 2475 Recording Film
- The film was essentially unavailable to the public — law enforcement and military only
- Some photographers obtained expired bulk rolls in the 1990s and early 2000s
- When shot at EI 1000 and developed in D-19: massive grain, high contrast, dramatic look
- Pushing to EI 4000 produced golf-ball grain — usable only for documentary/art effect
- The extended red sensitivity was designed for nighttime surveillance — skin tones look unnatural

## Surveillance Film Characteristics
- Designed for high-speed, low-light documentation — not aesthetics
- Extended red sensitivity for artificial light (sodium vapor, tungsten common in surveillance)
- Primary requirement: get the image at any cost (noise/grain was acceptable)
- Comparison films: Ilford Surveillance (later), Kodak Hawkeye surveillance films
- The aesthetic was entirely unintentional — a byproduct of the film's technical requirements

## LR Preset Design Notes
- Massive grain is the defining characteristic: GrainAmount=60, Size=55, Roughness=75
- High contrast (+25) with compressed shadows and highlights — the surveillance look
- Extended red sensitivity: GrayMixer Red +25 for brightened skin (surveillance requirement)
- Deep blacks (-15) but not crushed — shadow detail matters in surveillance
- Significant highlight reduction (-40) — the fast surveillance film had poor highlight handling
- Moderate shadow lift (+20) to pull detail from dark areas
- All STYLEGUIDE rules: Sharpness=10, Clarity/Texture/Dehaze=0, no Calibration, no WB
- The massive grain + extended red sensitivity creates a distinctive surveillance aesthetic
