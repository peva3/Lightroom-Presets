# Community Recipes — Joel Meyerowitz

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/landscapephotography
- Meyerowitz's Cape Cod light studies are frequently referenced in golden hour discussions
- The transition from street to landscape photography is studied as an artistic evolution
- Large format color work is the focus — the 8x10 perspective is essential to the look

### From YouTube Tutorials
- "Golden hour light" tutorials overlap heavily with Meyerowitz's palette:
  - Warm highlights (Hue 40-50, Sat 10-15)
  - Bright exposure with soft highlight rolloff
  - Airy, open shadows
- Thomas Heaton and Mark Denney (400K+) occasionally reference Cape Light as a benchmark

### From Landscape Photography Communities
- Meyerowitz's "Cape Light" is considered a masterwork of color landscape photography
- The light quality comes from: time of day (golden hour), location (Cape Cod sea/beach), large format capture
- Digital attempts to replicate often over-saturate — the actual look is bright and luminous, not saturated

### Community Consensus
- Bright, airy, golden warm — not saturated, not orange, genuinely golden
- Soft highlight rolloff (Highlights pulled negative + Whites moderate)
- Lifted shadows to maintain visibility in bright scenes
- This preset loves natural light — it's for golden hour and coastal images
