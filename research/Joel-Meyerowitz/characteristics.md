# Joel Meyerowitz

## Biography
- **Name**: Joel Meyerowitz (born 1938)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Era**: 1960s–present

## Philosophy
- Pioneer of color street photography alongside Eggleston and Shore
- "The camera is a license to be curious."
- After 9/11, was the only photographer granted unrestricted access to Ground Zero
- Transitioned from 35mm street to large format landscape/light studies
- Cape Cod light as lifelong obsession: "The light there is like no other place."

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: Leica M (street work), 8x10 Deardorff (Cape Cod light studies)
- **Film**: Kodachrome, Ektachrome, Portra, various stocks
- **Process**: From instinctive 35mm street shooting to contemplative large format
- **Printing**: Cibachrome from slides, later inkjet

## Visual Signature
- **Cape Cod golden light**: The defining characteristic of his later masterwork. Warm, hazy, almost tangible — light you can feel.
- **Airy spaciousness**: Large format gives room around the subject. Empty Cape Cod beaches, vast sky, tiny figure.
- **Warm, golden undertone**: Not orange, not amber — genuine golden hour gold. Yellow shifted toward gold.
- **Soft highlight rolloff**: Bright skies that hold detail without harsh clipping — the large format advantage
- **Atmospheric distance**: Distant objects soften naturally — no clarity or dehaze tricks needed
- **From street to seascape**: His early street work is crisp and immediate; his late light work is meditative and slow. The preset captures the late style.

## Iconic Images
- *Cape Light* (1978 book)
- *Ground Zero* series (2001–2002)
- *Provincetown* series

## Preset Design Notes
- Positive exposure for the bright, airy feel
- Slightly negative contrast to compress the tonal range gently
- Warm highlight color grading for the golden light signature
- Shadows lifted and warmed (golden light wraps everywhere)
- Minimal HSL intervention — let the light carry the color
- No grain (large format), no clarity
- Documentary Blending at 50 for natural look
