# Fuji Superia 1600 / Press 1600 — Technical Datasheet

## Overview

- **Brand:** Fujifilm
- **Product Names:** Fujicolor Superia 1600 (consumer), Fujicolor Press 1600 (professional), Fujicolor Natura 1600 (Japan market)
- **Type:** Color negative (C-41 process)
- **Speed:** ISO 1600/33°
- **Format:** 135 (35mm) only — 36 exposures
- **Balance:** Daylight
- **Introduction:** Announced at Photokina September 2000, widely available 2001
- **Discontinued:** Outside Japan 2016; Natura 1600 (Japan) announced October 2017, final stock through mid-2018
- **Emulsion Code:** CU
- **Successor:** None — no high-speed consumer color negative film currently exists on the market

## Layer Technology

- **4th Color Layer:** Proprietary cyan-sensitive fourth layer designed to improve color reproduction under fluorescent lighting, matching human eye response more accurately than conventional 3-layer emulsions
- **Nano-structured Sigma (Σ) Grain Technology:** Newly developed for this film speed class; finer grain structure than previous high-speed films

## Official Fujifilm Feature Claims

- "Super high-speed versatility — amazing results even in the most demanding situations"
- "Fine grain — remarkably sharp detail for a film in this speed category"
- "Excellent processing stability — consistent, high quality results"
- Marketed for compact zoom lens cameras and flash photography under low light conditions

## Professional vs Consumer Distinction

Press 1600 and Superia 1600 share **identical emulsions** (same product code CU). The difference:
- Press 1600: Refrigerated from manufacture to delivery; tighter quality control tolerances; aimed at press/journalism professionals
- Superia 1600: Standard consumer handling; identical emulsion but potentially slightly "riper" at purchase due to warmer storage

## Measured Characteristics

| Parameter | Value / Notes |
|-----------|--------------|
| **RMS Granularity** | ~13-15 (estimated; relatively high grain by design) |
| **Resolution** | ~63 lines/mm (TOC 1.6:1) estimated — lower than slower films, expected for ISO 1600 |
| **Exposure Latitude** | -1 to +3 stops usable; -1 to +2 stops excellent |
| **Push Capability** | Good results at EI 3200 with +1 push processing; usable at EI 6400 with +2 push (heavy grain/shadow loss) |
| **Spectral Sensitivity** | 4-layer: red, green, blue + cyan-sensitive 4th layer; 400-700nm range |
| **D-max (blue)** | Higher than average — contributes to cool shadow cast |
| **Contrast** | Moderate-high consumer contrast curve; punchier than pro neg films (Pro 400H, Pro 160NS) |

## Physical Characteristics

- **Base:** Cellulose triacetate, gray base tint
- **Anti-halation:** Rem-Jet-free; integral anti-halation undercoat (standard C-41 compatible)
- **Storage:** Recommend refrigerated storage below 13°C (55°F); allow 3+ hours to acclimate before opening

## Known Quirks

- Slight cyan/blue cast in shadows (the "Fuji cool" look)
- Pronounced grain in blue skies and even-toned areas — considered _pleasing_ and atmospheric
- Greens render with extra saturation/vibrancy due to 4th layer technology
- Overexposing ⅔ to 1 stop (shooting at EI 1000–1250) is common practice to tighten grain and deepen saturation
- Poor reciprocity characteristics for long exposures (not recommended beyond ~1 second without testing)

## Packaging Evolution

- Original: Superia 1600 packaging in Fuji green/white design (early 2000s)
- Mid-era: Updated Superia branding with blue accent
- Japan: Rebranded as "Natura 1600" paired with Fuji Natura compact cameras (NP system with automatic flash)
- Final: Natura 1600 discontinued 2017; single rolls only after multipack discontinuation Oct 2016

## Current Status

**Fully discontinued worldwide.** Final Natura 1600 stock expired approximately February 2019 (manufactured ~2017 with 2-year expiry). Occasionally found on secondary market as expired film. No direct replacement exists — Portra 800 (+1 push) or Lomo 800 are the closest currently-produced alternatives, both one stop slower.
