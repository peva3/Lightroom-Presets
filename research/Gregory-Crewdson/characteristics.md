# Gregory Crewdson

## Biography
- **Name**: Gregory Crewdson (born 1962)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Era**: 1980s–present

## Philosophy
- Photographs as single-frame movies — cinematic production values applied to still images
- Full film crews: director of photography, lighting designers, set builders, casting
- "I'm interested in the psychology of a place. Something has happened, or is about to happen."
- Suburban America as a stage for mystery and dread
- Inspired by Spielberg, Lynch, Hopper (painter), and Hitchcock

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: 8x10 large format view camera
- **Production**: Hollywood-style sets, often entire blocks closed for shoots
- **Lighting**: Elaborate cinematic lighting — HMI, tungsten, practical lights within frame
- **Post**: Heavy digital compositing; often 50+ layers per image
- **Printing**: Massive prints (up to 9 feet wide) for gallery installation

## Visual Signature
- **Cinematic teal/cool shadows**: The defining color move. Shadows go blue/teal.
- **Warm practical lights**: Table lamps, streetlights, headlights glow warm against the cool night.
- **Staged surrealism**: Everyday suburban scenes that are slightly "off" — a room flooded, a car in a living room
- **Atmospheric haze**: Fog, mist, and rain create volumetric light and depth
- **Deep focus**: 8x10 format means everything from near to far is sharp
- **Twilight/dusk palette**: The blue hour between day and night — neither fully lit nor fully dark

## Iconic Images
- *Twilight* series (1998–2002)
- *Beneath the Roses* (2003–2008)
- *Cathedral of the Pines* (2013–2014)

## Preset Design Notes
- Classic teal/orange Hollywood split — blue/teal shadows, warm highlights
- Green shifted hard toward yellow/olive (the "Crewdson green")
- Saturation pulled from greens and blues to control the teal intensity
- Slight underexposure for twilight atmosphere
- Dehaze slightly positive for atmosphere control
- Blending at 75 for cinematic color separation
- Midtone warmth injection to bridge the warm/cool split
