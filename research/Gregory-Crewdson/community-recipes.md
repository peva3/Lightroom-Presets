# Community Recipes — Gregory Crewdson

## Known Approaches

### From r/cinematography and r/postprocessing
- Crewdson's work is discussed more in cinematography circles than still photography
- The "Crewdson look" = teal shadows + warm practical lights + atmospheric haze
- His lighting diagrams are studied by cinematographers as much as still photographers

### From YouTube Tutorials
- Mango Street (1M+): Teal and orange tutorials frequently cite Crewdson as the still-photo exemplar
- Chris Hau (500K+): Cinematic color grading tutorials use Crewdson as a reference for blues in shadows
- Common recipe: Color Grade shadows at 210/15-20, highlights at 35-40/10-15, blending 70-80

### From Color Grading Forums
- The Crewdson green: shifted heavily toward yellow/brown (Hue -50 to -70)
- Atmospheric haze is considered essential — negative dehaze, or physical haze on set
- Deep focus (no bokeh) is part of the look — 8x10 large format

### Community Consensus
- The teal/orange split is the foundation — more teal than orange (unlike Hollywood, which is 50/50)
- Green must be desaturated and shifted toward brown
- Atmospheric haze softens the transitions
- Works best on twilight/blue-hour images with practical lights in frame
