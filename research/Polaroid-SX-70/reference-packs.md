# Reference Packs — Paid Lightroom Presets Featuring Polaroid / SX-70 Looks

A survey of commercial preset packs that include Polaroid, instant film, or SX-70-inspired aesthetics. Compiled from preset marketplace research, community reviews, and product listings.

---

## Major Commercial Preset Makers

### 1. VSCO
**Status**: Moved from standalone presets to membership-only app (VSCO Membership)

VSCO was the original driver of film-emulation presets for Lightroom. Their early desktop preset packs (now discontinued as standalone products) included film stocks that inspired the Polaroid/instant-film look:

| Pack | Films | SX-70 Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **VSCO Film 01** | Modern film stocks (Portra, Fuji, etc.) | Some low-contrast, warm presets can approximate instant film |
| **VSCO Film 02** | Classic/alternative stocks | Includes expired/alternative film emulations with faded looks |
| **VSCO Film 05** | Archetypes (artistic/experimental) | Includes "Polaroid" and "Instant" archetype presets |
| **VSCO Film 07** | Eclectic (cinema, cross-process, experimental) | Strong cross-process and faded vintage presets |

**Polaroid-adjacent VSCO presets (from community consensus)**:
- C1–C3 (vibrant classic with warmth)
- F1–F3 (muted, low-contrast)
- M1–M3 (soft, faded, slightly warm)
- P1–P9 (instant film pack — specifically "Instant" archetypes with lifted blacks and warm cast)
- HB1–HB2 (soft contrast, HujiCam-inspired)

**Note**: As of 2024, VSCO no longer sells Lightroom desktop presets. Their current product is a mobile app subscription. The old Film packs may still be found via third-party sellers or legacy accounts.

### 2. Mastin Labs
**Status**: Active — premium film emulation presets for Lightroom/Capture One

**Price**: ~$99–$149 per pack

Mastin Labs specialises in high-accuracy film stock emulation. While they do not sell a dedicated "Polaroid" pack, their film emulations can be adjusted to approach the instant film aesthetic:

| Pack | Emulated Films | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Portra Original** | Kodak Portra 160/400/800 | Warm, soft, natural — closest to SX-70's colour palette with adjustments |
| **Portra Pushed** | Portra pushed 1–3 stops | Higher contrast but warm tones |
| **Fuji Original** | Fuji 400H, 160C | Cooler, less Polaroid-like |
| **Ilford Original** | Ilford B&W films | Good B&W starting point |
| **Adventure Everyday** | Vintage/expired film looks | Most Polaroid-adjacent of their packs; includes faded, warm, low-contrast presets |

**SX-70 relevance**: Mastin Labs presets are known for realistic skin tones and natural warmth, making the Portra packs a good base. However, they lack the specific lifted-blacks, vignetting, and chemical-edge character of true SX-70 emulation.

### 3. RNI (Really Nice Images) — RNI Films / RNI All Films
**Status**: Active — desktop LR/Aperture/C1 presets + mobile app

**Price**: ~$99–$149 per pack; mobile app free with in-app purchases

RNI is widely considered the closest competitor to VSCO for film emulation quality. Their packs include:

| Pack | Films | SX-70 Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **RNI All Films 5 Pro** | Comprehensive film library (negative, slide, instant, B&W) | Includes specific **"Instant Film"** category presets |
| **RNI Instant Film pack** | Polaroid, Instax, peel-apart film emulations | Directly relevant — dedicated instant film presets |
| **RNI Vintage pack** | Expired, faded, cross-processed films | Good for the "aged SX-70" look |

**Instant film presets in RNI (from product descriptions)**:
- Polaroid SX-70 style presets
- Fujifilm Instax emulations
- Faded "memory" presets with lifted blacks
- Dedicated "Polaroid Frame" presets with warm cast + vignette + softness

## Comparison: What Makes a "Good" Polaroid/SX-70 Emulation Preset?

Based on community consensus and technical analysis of actual SX-70 film characteristics:

| Feature | Budget Presets ($3–$15) | Mid-Range Presets ($20–$50) | Premium Presets ($75–$150) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lifted blacks | Usually included | Included | Carefully tuned |
| Warm colour cast | Basic WB shift | Split toning + calibration | Multi-point colour grading |
| Green tint bias | Rare | Sometimes | Often included |
| Soft clarity | Usually included | Included | Refined (texture vs clarity vs dehaze balance) |
| Vignette | Basic | Tuned midpoint/feather | Matched to SX-70 lens profile |
| Grain pattern | Generic | Tuned size/roughness | Custom grain profiles |
| Tone curve | Simple black lift | Parametric + point curve | Per-channel RGB curves |
| Calibration panel | Not used | Sometimes used | Often tuned for film colour science |
| Chemical-edge artifacts | Never | Rare | Very rare |
| White border | Not included | Sometimes (Photoshop action) | Sometimes (layered template) |

## Recommendations by Use Case

### For Quick Social Media / Casual Use:
- Mobile apps: **VSCO**, **POLY**, **NOMO CAM** (fast, single-tap Polaroid look + frame)
- Budget desktop presets: **Etsy** or **Creative Market** Polaroid packs ($5–$15)
- Free: **PresetLove** or Reddit community shares

### For Serious Photography / Client Work:
- **RNI All Films 5 Pro** — professional instant film emulation, Capture One + Lightroom
- **VSCO Film 05/07** (if you can find the legacy installers) — Archetypes/Eclectic packs
- Custom preset built from the community recipe in `community-recipes.md`

### For the Most Accurate SX-70 Emulation:
No single paid pack perfectly replicates all five pillars of the SX-70 aesthetic (soft contrast, warm-yellow cast, vignetting, chemical artifacts, softness). The best approach:
1. Use **RNI Instant Film** presets as a base colour profile
2. Layer with custom tone curve (lifted blacks, compressed highlights)
3. Add calibration-panel green-tint adjustment
4. Apply custom vignette with soft midpoint
5. Add grain matching SX-70 characteristics
6. Composite white border in Photoshop or export plugin

## Market Trends (2024–2026)

- **Decline of desktop preset packs**: The market has shifted from Lightroom Classic desktop presets toward Lightroom CC mobile profiles and app-based filters
- **Rise of AI-enhanced presets**: Newer products include AI subject detection and adaptive adjustments (e.g., Luminar Neo, which has a Polaroid preset in its film library)
- **Subscription fatigue**: Users are increasingly seeking one-time purchase options over subscriptions, driving traffic to Etsy/Gumroad creators
- **Polaroid brand licensing**: Official Polaroid-branded app filters (via Polaroid B.V.) exist in mobile apps but not as Lightroom presets
- **Film renaissance**: Growing interest in authentic film photography is driving demand for more accurate film emulation rather than "inspired-by" presets
