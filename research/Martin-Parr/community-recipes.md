# Community Recipes — Martin Parr

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/streetphotography
- Parr's ring flash aesthetic is the most discussed technical element
- The "Parr color" — saturated, consumer-bright, unflattering — is both celebrated and critiqued
- Ring flash = shadowless, flat, saturated — the opposite of "good lighting"

### From YouTube Tutorials
- "Martin Parr style" tutorials are rare but focus on:
  - Pop color saturation (red, yellow, blue primary colors)
  - Bright exposure with flash-like fill
  - Ring flash approximation via shadow lift + low contrast in shadows
- Some street photography tutorials reference Parr for composition (close, confrontational)

### From Magnum/Photojournalism Resources
- Parr's work divides opinion — some see cruelty, others see anthropological truth
- The color saturation IS the commentary — you can't separate technique from meaning
- His later digital work maintains the same color philosophy despite different tools

### Community Consensus
- High saturation in primary consumer colors (red, yellow, blue, magenta)
- Bright, punchy exposure — this is a flash aesthetic
- Green desaturation (consumer environments have plastic green, not natural green)
- The preset is intentionally "too much" in the LaChapelle vein, but for social documentary
