# Martin Parr

## Biography
- **Name**: Martin Parr (born 1952)
- **Nationality**: British
- **Era**: 1970s–present

## Philosophy
- "My interest is in the way we live, the things we believe in, the way we entertain ourselves."
- Photographs Britishness — seaside holidays, tea parties, bargain shops, bad taste
- Consumer culture as anthropological study
- "Humour is a way of getting people to look at things they'd rather not see."
- Magnum Photos member (controversial at the time — some members thought his work was cruel)

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: 35mm camera, Plaubel Makina medium format, ring flash
- **Film**: Kodak Ektachrome, Fujichrome, Kodak color negative; now digital
- **Ring flash**: His defining technique — macro ring flash for even, shadowless, saturated illumination
- **Color**: Saturated consumer colors — the plastic-bright palette of cheap products and tourism

## Visual Signature
- **Ring flash pop**: Shadowless, flat lighting that saturates colors and eliminates depth. Like a specimen under a microscope.
- **Saturated consumer colors**: Red tomato sauce, blue beach towels, yellow mustard, green artificial grass — high-chroma consumer palette.
- **British kitsch**: Union Jack merchandise, novelty teapots, seaside postcards — the stuff that makes you cringe.
- **Close and confrontational**: Wide-angle intimacy; subjects unaware or uncaring of the camera inches away
- **No romance, no flattery**: No golden hour, no soft focus, no beauty lighting. Just the harsh truth of how things look.
- **Social commentary through color**: The saturation IS the critique. The garishness is the point.

## Iconic Images
- *The Last Resort* (1986) — New Brighton seaside
- *Common Sense* (1999) — global consumer culture
- *Autoportrait* (auto-portrait series)

## Preset Design Notes
- High saturation and vibrance — the defining pop/kitsch aesthetic
- Bright exposure with strong contrast for punch
- Warm highlight push + cool shadow push for color contrast
- Red, yellow, magenta boosted (the kitsch color spectrum)
- Green desaturated (digital green looks too natural; consumer green is different)
- Ring flash look approximated via shadow lift (eliminating deep shadows)
- Blending at 75 for clean, stylized color separation
