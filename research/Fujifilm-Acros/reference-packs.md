# Reference Packs — Paid Preset/Profile Packs That Include Acros

## Overview

Multiple commercial preset/profile developers offer Acros emulations. These range from full Lightroom profiles (that modify the underlying rendering pipeline, including tone curves) to simpler slider-based presets. For the most accurate Acros emulation in Lightroom, **profiles** (`.dcp` or `.xmp` with embedded profiles) are significantly superior to slider-only presets.

---

## 1. Cobalt Image — Fujifilm Digital Emulations

**Product**: [Cobalt Image Fujifilm Profiles](https://www.cobalt-image.com/)
**Price**: ~$40–70 per pack; full bundles available

### What They Offer
Cobalt Image is the most technically rigorous option. Their profiles are created by measuring actual camera output with a spectrophotometer and building mathematical models of the rendering pipeline.

- **Fuji Film Simulation profiles** including all four Acros variants (Acros, Acros+R, Acros+Y, Acros+G)
- Profiles are available for most major camera brands (Sony, Canon, Nikon, Leica, Panasonic, etc.) — **not** just Fuji cameras
- This is the #1 recommendation on r/fujifilm for getting authentic Acros in Lightroom

### Accuracy
Considered the most accurate third-party Acros emulation available. Matches the in-camera JPEG tonality and color response very closely. r/fujifilm frequent recommendation.

### Limitations
- Per-camera calibration means you buy profiles for your specific camera body
- Some reports of slight differences with extreme exposure adjustments vs. in-camera JPEG
- No grain simulation (you add grain separately in Lightroom)

## 2. RNI (Really Nice Images) — RNI All Films 5

**Product**: RNI All Films 5 (Lightroom/Capture One profiles)
**Price**: ~$100–150 for the full pack

### What They Offer
RNI All Films 5 is one of the most comprehensive film emulation packages. It includes profiles for dozens of film stocks.

- **Fuji Neopan Acros 100** — film profile (not the in-camera simulation, but the actual film stock)
- Multiple development variants (standard, pushed, pulled)
- Available for Lightroom Classic, Lightroom CC, and Capture One
- Works on RAW files from any camera brand

### Accuracy
Highly respected. RNI profiles are created from real film measurements. The Acros profile is a film stock emulation, not an in-camera simulation profile — so it may look slightly different from Fuji's in-camera Acros output.

### Strengths
- Covers far more film stocks than just Acros
- Includes grain, halation, and other analog artifacts
- Good documentation and community support

## 3. Exposure / Exposure X7 (formerly Alien Skin Exposure)

**Product**: Exposure X7 (standalone editor + Lightroom/Photoshop plugin)
**Price**: ~$149 (one-time purchase, not subscription)

### What They Offer
Exposure has one of the largest libraries of film emulations, including B&W films.

- **Fuji Neopan Acros 100** — film preset
- Multiple development and printing variants
- Extensive grain, vignetting, and border tools
- Works as a standalone program or Lightroom/Photoshop plugin

### Historical Note
Ritchie Roesch (Fuji X Weekly) mentioned in his Acros recipe article that he originally used Alien Skin Exposure with a Delta 100 preset, then matched his in-camera Acros settings to that look. This suggests Exposure's B&W film presets have been influential in the Fuji community.

### Strengths
- Very comprehensive B&W film library
- Excellent grain engine
- Non-subscription

## 4. Mastin Labs — Ilford Original (B&W Preset Pack)

**Product**: Mastin Labs Ilford Original
**Price**: ~$99 per pack

### What They Offer
Mastin Labs focuses on easy-to-use presets that produce consistent, professional results. Their B&W pack is based on Ilford films.

- **Ilford Delta 100** (fine grain, similar to Acros in aesthetic)
- **Ilford HP5** (classic 400-speed)
- **Ilford FP4** (classic 125-speed)
- **Ilford Pan F** (ultra-fine grain 50-speed)

### Does Not Include Acros
Mastin Labs does **not** currently include a dedicated Fuji Neopan Acros preset. However, their **Ilford Delta 100** preset is a very close aesthetic analogue — both are modern, fine-grain, tabular-grain films with smooth tonality and excellent sharpness. For Lightroom users seeking an Acros-like look, Mastin Labs Ilford Delta 100 is often cited as the closest alternative.

### Strengths
- Excellent out-of-box results
- Good for event/wedding photographers (consistent batch processing)
- Simple, paint-by-numbers workflow

## 9. OneClickWonders / Photographers' Wallets / Etsy Sellers

**General category**: Independent preset sellers on Etsy, Gumroad, etc.

Many independent creators sell "Fuji Acros Lightroom Presets." Quality varies enormously. Most are slider-only presets that approximate the Acros look rather than using profiles. Approach with caution — the most reliable options remain Cobalt Image (profiles) and RNI (profiles).

## Recommendations by Use Case

### For maximum Acros accuracy (any camera brand in Lightroom):
**Cobalt Image Fujifilm Profiles** — get the Acros pack for your specific camera body. Uses .dcp profiles that modify the rendering pipeline.

### For broad film stock emulation (including Acros + many others):
**RNI All Films 5** — comprehensive library, well-respected, works on any camera.

### For Fuji X/GFX shooters who want in-camera Acros:
Use the in-camera simulation directly — no Lightroom preset can fully match it. Consider shooting RAW+JPEG and using the Acros JPEG as a reference for RAW processing, OR use the Fuji X Weekly recipes programmed directly into the camera.

### For the absolute most accurate B&W grain engine:
**DxO FilmPack** or **Nik Silver Efex Pro** — DxO's grain algorithm is widely considered industry-leading.

### For free/open source:
**PictureFX Acros II** — community-developed, includes LUTs and presets.

## Important: "Profile" vs "Preset" vs "LUT" for Acros Emulation

- **.dcp Profile (Camera Profile)**: Modifies Lightroom's underlying rendering. Controls color/tone at a fundamental level. Cobalt Image uses this. Most accurate.
- **.xmp Preset (Slider Preset)**: Sets Lightroom sliders to specific values. Does not change the fundamental rendering pipeline. Limited accuracy.
- **LUT (Look-Up Table)**: Direct color transform, commonly used in video. Can be applied in Photoshop or via LUT plugins. PictureFX uses this.

**For Lightroom Classic**, .dcp profiles are the gold standard for film emulation accuracy. Slider-only presets can never fully replicate a film stock or film simulation because they cannot modify the underlying tone curve mapping that profiles control.
