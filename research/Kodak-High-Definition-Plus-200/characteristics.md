# Kodak High Definition Plus 200 — Aesthetic Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

## Overview

Kodak High Definition Plus 200 (HD 200) was Kodak's fine-grain consumer ISO 200 color negative film, marketed for sharpness and clarity above all else. The "High Definition" branding signified T-Grain emulsion technology optimized for resolving power rather than speed. It was positioned between Kodak Gold 100/200 and the professional Ektar line as a sharper alternative to standard consumer films. Discontinued in the mid-2000s. This document serves as a reference for creating a Lightroom preset that emulates High Definition Plus 200.

## Core Aesthetic Signature

### Color Palette

- **Primary Color Cast**: Neutral-warm, less golden than Gold 200. The HD line prioritized color accuracy over warmth.
- **Reds**: Clean, accurate, slightly subdued compared to Gold 200. Less orange-shifted.
- **Yellows & Oranges**: Natural rendering — not the heavy golden boost of Gold films. More restrained warmth.
- **Greens**: Clean and well-separated. T-Grain technology provided excellent green channel resolution for foliage.
- **Blues**: Accurate sky blues with good luminance separation. Less darkening than Gold/Ultramax.
- **Skin Tones**: Neutral and flattering. Less golden than Gold 200, more accurate than Ultramax. Good for consumer portraiture.

### Contrast & Tonality

- **Overall Contrast**: Medium. Less punchy than Gold 200 — the HD line sacrificed contrast for resolution and latitude.
- **Highlight Roll-off**: Smooth and controlled. T-Grain emulsions handle highlights more gracefully.
- **Shadow Detail**: Good. Fine grain in shadows preserves detail that coarser consumer films lose.
- **Midtone Contrast**: Moderate — images feel "sharp" through resolution rather than contrast.

### Grain

- **Grain Level**: Very fine for a consumer ISO 200 film. T-Grain technology made this the sharpest consumer color film Kodak produced.
- **Grain Character**: Fine, uniform, nearly invisible at normal print sizes. The "high definition" advantage.
- **Comparison**: Finer than Gold 200. Approaching Ektar 100 levels of grain suppression.

### Exposure Behavior

- **Box Speed (ISO 200)**: Sharp, clean, neutral colors. Best for general daylight photography.
- **Overexposure (+1 stop, EI 100)**: Even finer grain, slightly pastel rendering in highlights.
- **Underexposure (-1 stop)**: Still usable — fine grain means shadow noise is less objectionable than consumer 400 films.

## Summary

```
Neutral-warm color balance (less golden than Gold 200)
Medium contrast with excellent highlight control
Ultra-fine T-Grain for maximum sharpness
Clean, accurate skin tones
The sharpest consumer color negative film Kodak made
```
