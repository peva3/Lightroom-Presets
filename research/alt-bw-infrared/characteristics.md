# B&W Infrared Lightroom Preset — Complete Aesthetic Breakdown

## Core Aesthetic Signature

The bw infrared Lightroom preset recreates this look digitally using Lightroom's color grading tools for authentic film emulation. Black-and-white infrared photography captures near-infrared light (700–900nm) instead of visible light. Since vegetation strongly reflects IR (the Wood Effect), foliage appears glowing white, while blue skies (which scatter very little IR) appear nearly black. Water absorbs IR and appears dark. The combination creates surreal, high-contrast landscapes where trees seem to glow against dramatic dark skies.

## Historical Context

**Era:** 1910s (first IR film) | 1930s (commercial) | 1960s–present (artistic use)



## Visual Characteristics

Luminous white foliage — the defining characteristic (Wood Effect). Very dark, dramatic skies even on sunny days. Bright white clouds against near-black sky. Dark water bodies. Milky, porcelain-like skin tones. Deep, opaque shadows. Extremely graphic, high-contrast look. Fine grain from IR film stocks.

## Emotional & Artistic Vocabulary

Surreal, apocalyptic, sublime. IR landscapes show a world transformed — familiar nature rendered alien. The extreme sky-foliage contrast creates a sense of cosmic drama.

## Technical Notes for Digital Emulation

This preset is designed to capture the essential visual character of the b&w infrared process using Lightroom's slider-based toolset. It prioritizes the emotional/aesthetic impact over perfect chemical accuracy, which would require a 3D LUT or DCP camera profile.

### Limitations of Slider-Based Simulation
- True process-specific spectral sensitivity cannot be replicated with sliders
- Chemical stain patterns are simulated through grain and color grading
- Paper/plate texture is approximated through grain settings
