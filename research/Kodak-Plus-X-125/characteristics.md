# Kodak Plus-X 125 — Aesthetic Characteristics

## Overview

Kodak Plus-X 125 was an iconic mid-speed black-and-white negative film, introduced in 1954 and manufactured continuously until its discontinuation in 2011. ISO 125 (later Plus-X Pan, ISO 125), it occupied the sweet spot between slow fine-grain films (Panatomic-X) and fast photojournalism films (Tri-X). Plus-X was the workhorse of mid-century editorial, portrait, and fine art photography — the "medium format of 35mm" in terms of tonal refinement.

## Core Aesthetic Signature

### Contrast & Tonality

- **Overall Contrast**: Medium. Plus-X produces a full, rich tonal scale without the aggressive contrast of Tri-X. Midtones are open and luminous.
- **Highlight Rendering**: Excellent separation in the upper zones (VII–IX). Highlights retain texture and detail without blowing out.
- **Shadow Rendering**: Good shadow detail with smooth toe. Blacks are deep but not crushed — Zone I–II separation is better than Tri-X.
- **Midtone Character**: The defining trait. Plus-X's midtones (Zones IV–VI) are creamy, smooth, and beautifully separated. This is why portrait photographers loved it.
- **Curve Shape**: Classic S-curve with a long linear midtone section and gentle toe/shoulder. Not as steep as Tri-X, not as flat as technical films.

### Grain

- **Grain Level**: Fine. RMS granularity ~7 (vs. Tri-X ~11). Significantly finer than any ISO 400 film.
- **Grain Character**: Tight, regular, classic cubic grain. Smooth even in 35mm at modest enlargements.
- **Comparison**: Finer than HP5+, finer than Tri-X. Similar to Ilford FP4+.

### Spectral Sensitivity

- **Type**: Panchromatic with balanced spectral response.
- **Red**: Moderate sensitivity — skin tones render naturally without excessive lightening.
- **Blue**: Moderate sensitivity — skies render with medium density without filters.
- **Green**: Good sensitivity — foliage renders naturally.
- **Yellow/Orange**: Strong — warm tones render lighter, which flatters skin.

### Developer Character

- **D-76 1:1**: The classic reference — balanced contrast, fine grain, full tonality.
- **HC-110 (Dil B)**: Slightly more contrast, crisp midtones, popular for scanning.
- **Rodinal 1:50**: Distinctive edge sharpness (acutance), larger grain character.

## Summary

```
Mid-speed ISO 125 — the refined sweet spot
Medium contrast with creamy, luminous midtones
Fine, tight grain — significantly cleaner than ISO 400 films
Excellent highlight and shadow detail retention
The portrait photographer's B&W film
```
