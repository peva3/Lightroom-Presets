# Kodak Plus-X 125 — Lightroom Preset Settings & Recipes

### FxW Recipe → Lightroom Translation

Plus-X 125 was the refined mid-speed B&W film — creamy midtones, fine grain, medium contrast. Translation priorities: balanced tonality, smooth midtone gradation, fine grain, moderate contrast.

#### Basic Panel
- **Exposure**: 0 (neutral baseline — mid-speed film at box speed)
- **Contrast**: +20 (medium contrast — more than HP5, less than Tri-X)
- **Highlights**: -20 (controlled — Plus-X highlight detail is excellent)
- **Shadows**: +15 (open shadows — Plus-X's toe is gentle)
- **Whites**: +10 (clean white point)
- **Blacks**: -15 (rich but not crushed — Zones I-II retain separation)

#### B&W Mix (ConvertToGrayscale=True, Grayscale blend)
| Color | Value | Rationale |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Red | +10 | Slight skin lightening, moderate sky darkening |
| Orange | +10 | Key skin tone — warm, not harsh |
| Yellow | +5 | Subtle warm highlight separation |
| Green | -5 | Moderate foliage darkening |
| Aqua | -5 | Subtle sky depth |
| Blue | -15 | Moderate blue sky darkening (less dramatic than Tri-X) |
| Purple | 0 | Neutral |
| Magenta | 0 | Neutral |

#### Color Grading (Subtle warm/cool split for paper-like tone)
- **Highlights**: Hue 45°, Sat 3 (very subtle warm paper base)
- **Shadows**: Hue 225°, Sat 5 (subtle cool ink)
- **Blending**: 50
- **Balance**: -10 (slight shadow bias)

#### Grain (STYLEGUIDE classic B&W: Amount 50-65, but Plus-X is fine-grain: 20-30, Size 15-25)
- **Amount**: 25 (fine — mid-speed classic)
- **Size**: 22 (small particles)
- **Roughness**: 50 (regular structure)

#### STYLEGUIDE Compliance
- No Calibration
- No Temperature/Tint
- Sharpness=10, Clarity=0, Texture=0, Dehaze=0 (grain protection)
- LuminanceSmoothing=0
- ProcessVersion 15.4, Treatment=Monochrome, ConvertToGrayscale=True
- Adobe Monochrome Look UUID 0C09521111114111B1115456789ABCDE
- Blacks -15 ≥ -30

## Community Consensus

Plus-X 125 is one of the most mourned discontinued B&W films:
1. **The "lost sweet spot"**: ISO 125 was fast enough for general use, slow enough for fine grain
2. **Creamy midtones**: The most-loved characteristic — smooth tonal transitions
3. **Versatile**: Worked beautifully in D-76, HC-110, Rodinal, and XTOL
4. **Portrait film**: The go-to B&W portrait film before T-Max 100
5. **Tonal refinement**: Similar to Ilford FP4+ but with a distinctive Kodak tonality
