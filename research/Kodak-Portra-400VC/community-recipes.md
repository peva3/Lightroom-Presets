# Kodak Portra 400VC — Lightroom Preset Settings & Recipes

> Compiled from t3mujinpack .dtstyle reference data, Reddit (r/Lightroom, r/analog), and community forums. The 400VC was the workhorse wedding film for photographers who wanted Portra skin tones with more punch.

---

## t3mujinpack .dtstyle Reference

The t3mujinpack collection includes Portra 400VC as a Darktable style. Key characteristics extracted:

- **Purpose**: Pre-2010 vivid color 400-speed Portra
- **Profile base**: Standard color matrix
- **Contrast**: Positive (higher than Portra 400)
- **Saturation**: Elevated globally
- **Skin bias**: Strong warmth in orange/red channels
- **Grain**: Medium structure for 400 speed

## Community Consensus Values

### Contrast & Tone
- Contrast: Positive (+2 to +5) — punchier than Portra 400's -7.5
- Blacks: +5 to +10 (moderately lifted)
- Highlights: -45 to -55 (shoulder roll-off)
- Shadows: +25 to +35
- Exposure: +0.35 to +0.50 (slight overexposure sweet spot)

### Color & Saturation
- Global Saturation: +5 to +10
- Vibrance: +3 to +5 (within 5 of Saturation)
- Green Saturation: -15 to -20
- Blue Saturation: -10 to -15
- Red/Orange Saturation: +5 to +10 (warmth and skin saturation)
- Orange Luminance: +10 to +15 (skin brightness)

### Color Grading
- Highlights: Warm golden (Hue 40-50°, Sat 10-15)
- Shadows: Cool teal (Hue 200-220°, Sat 5-10)
- Blending: 50

### Grain
- Amount: 25-35
- Size: 25-30
- Roughness: 45-55

## Key Differentiator from Portra 400

Community consensus: Portra 400VC is Portra 400 with **positive contrast** and **warmer skin saturation**. The standard Portra 400 preset uses negative contrast (-7.5) for soft tonal transitions and moderate saturation. 400VC flips the contrast sign and adds saturation, creating a noticeably different rendering despite sharing the same Portra DNA.
