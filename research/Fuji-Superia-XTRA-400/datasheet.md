# Fuji Superia X-TRA 400 — Datasheet & Technical Specifications

## Official Designation

- **Product Code**: FUJICOLOR SUPERIA X-TRA 400 [CH]
- **Type**: Daylight-type color negative film
- **Ref. No.**: AF3-0217E (final revision), formerly AF3-0262E
- **Manufacturer**: Fujifilm Corporation, Tokyo, Japan
- **Introduced**: 1998 (replacing Super G Plus 400)
- **Updated**: 2003 (Super Fine-Σ Grain), 2006 (New Super Uniform Fine Grain Technology)
- **Discontinued**: April 2024 (all markets)
- **Replacement**: "Fujifilm 400" (contract-manufactured by Kodak, significantly different color palette)

## Key Technologies

### 4th Color Layer
Fujifilm's proprietary 4th cyan-sensitive color layer technology. Adds a cyan-forming layer to the standard RGB sensitization, improving color accuracy under mixed lighting (particularly fluorescent). This is what gives Superia its distinctive color handling — it balances out green fluorescent casts without filter use.

### New Super Uniform Fine Grain Technology
Introduced in 2006 update. Uses flat hexagonal silver halide grains with large surface area that retain sufficient volume of photosensitive pigment. Photons are accumulated in grain periphery and efficiently concentrated to form latent image. Result: dramatic reduction in grain size with no loss in sensitivity (nano-technology approach).

### Color Correction
- **No filters needed** for daylight or electronic flash
- **Fluorescent lighting**: Greenish tint corrected during printing — no correction filters required during shooting
- **Tungsten (3200K)**: Use Wratten No.80A filter, +2 stops compensation
- **Post-processing masking color**: Slight reddish tint compared to previous Superia X-TRA 400

## Exposure Guidelines

### Daylight Exposure Guide (ISO 400)
| Conditions | Aperture | Shutter |
|---|---|---|
| Bright Sun | f/16 | 1/500 |
| Hazy Sun | f/11 | 1/500 |
| Cloudy Bright | f/8 | 1/500 |
| Cloudy Day or Open Shade | f/5.6 | 1/250 |

### Low Light Exposure Guide
| Conditions | Aperture | Shutter |
|---|---|---|
| Indoor Daytime / Fluorescent | f/2.8–4 | 1/60 |
| Evening Scenes | f/2–2.8 | 1/30 |
| Night Scenes | f/2.8–4 | 1/60 |
| Night (under Fluorescent) | f/2–2.8 | 1/30 |

### Electronic Flash
- Flash guide number ÷ flash-to-subject distance (meters) = aperture
- Auto flash: Set ISO to 400
- Shutter speeds slower than 1/60 may cause color imbalances from ambient light

### Reciprocity Compensation
| Exposure Time | 1/4000–2 sec | 4 sec | 16 sec | 64 sec |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Correction | None | +1/3 stop | +2/3 stop | +1 stop |

## Film Structure

```
Protective Layer
Blue Sensitive Layer + Colorless Yellow Coupler
Yellow Filter Layer
Green Sensitive Layer + Yellow Colored Magenta Coupler
Interlayer
Red Sensitive Layer + Red Colored Cyan Coupler
Interlayer + Antihalation Layer
Film Base (Cellulose Triacetate)
```

### Post-Processing Dye Formation
- Yellow dye from Blue-sensitive layer
- Magenta dye from Green-sensitive layer (with colored coupler)
- Cyan dye from Red-sensitive layer (with colored coupler)

## Processing Data

### C-41 / CN-16 Compatible
Film is designed for standard C-41 processing. Minimal replenishment rate adjustment required. Same processing characteristics as previous SUPERIA X-TRA 400.

### Exposure Reference
18% gray card through RED Status M filter should yield density 0.75–0.95 (optimal exposure + processing).

## Storage

### Unprocessed Film
- **Short-term**: ≤15°C
- **Long-term**: ≤0°C, sealed in plastic bags
- Allow 3+ hours (refrigerated) or 6+ hours (frozen) to reach room temperature before opening

### Processed Film
- **Dark storage** in non-airtight containers (paper, plastic, metal)
- Annual airing for one dry day to prevent TAC base decomposition
- **>20 years preservation**: ≤10°C, 30–50% RH
- **10–20 years preservation**: ≤25°C, 30–50% RH

- Box color: Blue

## Spectral Sensitivity (from characteristic curves)
- **Blue Sensitive Layer**: Peak ~450nm
- **Green Sensitive Layer**: Peak ~550nm
- **Red Sensitive Layer**: Peak ~650nm, extended sensitivity into deep red
- Significant cyan-forming layer overlap in the 480–520nm region (4th layer technology)
