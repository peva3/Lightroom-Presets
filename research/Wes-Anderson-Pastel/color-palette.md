# Wes Anderson Color Palettes — Per-Film Hex Codes & HSL Values

## Overview

This document catalogs the specific color palettes used in Wes Anderson's most visually iconic films, with hex codes and HSL values derived from film frame color extraction, production design references, and cinematographer interviews.

## Moonrise Kingdom (2012)

**Cinematographer**: Robert Yeoman, ASC
**Film Stock**: Kodak Vision3 200T, Super 16mm
**Aspect Ratio**: 1.85:1

The warmest, most nostalgic Anderson palette. Heavily yellow-green-gold.

### Core Palette

| Role | Color Name | Hex | HSL | Notes |
|------|-----------|-----|-----|-------|
| Khaki Scout yellow | Scout Mustard | `#D4A84B` | H:40 S:62 L:56 | Uniforms, kerchiefs |
| Suzy's dress pink | Suzy Pink | `#E8B4B8` | H:355 S:53 L:81 | Suzy's sundress |
| Autumn grass | Wheat Grass | `#C4A84B` | H:49 S:49 L:53 | Fields, meadows |
| Sky blue | 1965 Sky | `#9FC4D4` | H:200 S:35 L:73 | Paled blue sky |
| Warm wood | Cabin Brown | `#8B6B4A` | H:30 S:30 L:42 | Buildings, interiors |
| Beige khaki | Canvas | `#D4C4A8` | H:38 S:34 L:75 | Tent canvas |
| Scout green | Scout Green | `#7B8B5A` | H:75 S:22 L:45 | Uniform accents |
| Lighthouse red | Beacon Red | `#C4453A` | H:5 S:55 L:50 | The red lighthouse |

### Key Color Theme

- **Dominant warm cast**: Yellow-green-gold across the entire frame.
- **Punctuation color**: Red (Suzy's binoculars, lighthouse, record player).
- **Atmosphere**: Nostalgic 1965 New England island, "end of summer" light.

## Asteroid City (2023)

**Cinematographer**: Robert Yeoman, ASC
**Film Stock**: Kodak 35mm
**Aspect Ratio**: 2.35:1 (widescreen) and 1.37:1 (Academy)

Atomic Age desert palette — turquoise, coral, and muted sand tones. Inspired by 1950s postcards and Kodachrome.

### Core Palette

| Role | Color Name | Hex | HSL | Notes |
|------|-----------|-----|-----|-------|
| Desert sky teal | Atomic Teal | `#6BB5B5` | H:180 S:36 L:56 | The dominant sky/set color |
| Coral/peach | Retro Coral | `#E8947B` | H:12 S:71 L:70 | Costumes, accents |
| Sand/beige | Desert Sand | `#D4C4A8` | H:38 S:34 L:75 | Landscape, architecture |
| Pale yellow | Atomic Yellow | `#E8D48B` | H:42 S:67 L:73 | 1950s diner, accents |
| Dusty blue | Cadet Blue | `#8FA4B5` | H:208 S:22 L:64 | Military/science uniforms |
| Mint green | Soda Fountain | `#A8D4C4` | H:158 S:34 L:75 | Diner details |
| Warm orange | Canyon Orange | `#D47B5A` | H:18 S:59 L:59 | Rock formations |
| Pink | 50s Pink | `#E8B4C4` | H:345 S:53 L:81 | Costumes, signage |

### Key Color Theme

- **Teal dominates**: The entire film is graded toward a turquoise/teal cast.
- **Orange is coral/peach, not yellow**: Distinct from Moonrise Kingdom's yellow-gold.
- **Whites are warm cream**: Not pure white—always shifted toward ivory or ecru.
- **1950s Kodachrome reference**: Slightly heightened saturation with a cool bias.

### Color Temperature Split

```
Highlights: Warm cream/peach (~5500K + magenta bias)
Midtones: Neutral-cool (~6000K)
Shadows: Teal/cyan (~200° hue, desaturated)
```

## Cross-Film Color Signatures

### Anderson's Red

Anderson uses red consistently across films as a character signifier (trauma, passion, regression to childhood).

| Film | Red Context | Hex | HSL |
|------|------------|-----|-----|
| Royal Tenenbaums | Chas's tracksuit | `#CC2A1D` | H:4 S:75 L:46 |
| Life Aquatic | Team Zissou beanies | `#D42A1D` | H:3 S:75 L:47 |
| Moonrise Kingdom | Lighthouse, binoculars | `#C4453A` | H:5 S:55 L:50 |
| Grand Budapest | Elevator, carpet | `#C0362C` | H:4 S:63 L:46 |

**Red consistency**: Pure red, hue ~3-5 degrees, saturation 55-75%, luminance 46-50%.

### Anderson's Yellow

Yellow communicates naivety, childhood, and nostalgia.

| Film | Yellow Context | Hex | HSL |
|------|---------------|-----|-----|
| Moonrise Kingdom | Scout uniforms, fields | `#D4A84B` | H:40 S:62 L:56 |
| Darjeeling Limited | The train, luggage | `#D4B84B` | H:44 S:62 L:56 |
| Grand Budapest | Gold trim, desserts | `#D4A843` | H:42 S:63 L:55 |

**Yellow consistency**: Warm gold-yellow, hue ~40-44, saturation ~62%, luminance ~55%.

## Wes Anderson Color Palette Generators & Resources

Community tools for extracting or generating Anderson palettes:

1. **Wes Anderson Palettes** (wesandersonpalettes.tumblr.com — archived): Compiled hex palettes from every frame of every film.
2. **Coolors.co**: Search "Wes Anderson" for user-created palettes from film frames.
3. **Adobe Color**: Community-submitted Anderson palettes under "film" and "cinematic" themes.
4. **Shotdeck.com**: Search film frames by color value — the professional tool used by colorists.
5. **Film Grab**: Frame-by-frame color extraction from films (formerly at film-grab.com).
