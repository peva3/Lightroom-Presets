# Kodachrome Blue — Characteristics

## Film Background
Kodachrome (1935-2009) was the first commercially successful color film and used a unique K-14 process where dye couplers were added during development rather than embedded in the emulsion. Kodachrome is actually a monochrome film at capture — color is formed by dye transfer during processing. This unique structure makes it the hardest film to scan; without proper color correction, scans exhibit a pronounced blue cast.

## Key Aesthetic
- Emphasizes "Kodachrome blue" sky signature
- Deep cyan-blue tones throughout
- Suppressed warm tones (reds/oranges slightly desaturated)
- Cool overall white balance (6500K with strong blue shift)
- Eterna Bleach Bypass base for high contrast + distinct color separation
- National Geographic mid-century color palette
