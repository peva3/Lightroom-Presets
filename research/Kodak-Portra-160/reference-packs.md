## 1. Mastin Labs — Portra Original

**Product:** Portra Original Lightroom Desktop Presets  
**Price:** $99 (regular) / $49 (sale)  
**URL:** `mastinlabs.com/products/portra-original`  
**Contains:** Portra 160, Portra 400, Portra 800 + 18 AI/Specialty Tools

### How Mastin Labs Describes Portra 160
> *"Subtle and neutral, Portra 160 is a flexible look with medium contrast and low saturation, making it ideal for even daylight portraits with exceptionally smooth skin tones and superb color reproduction."*

### Key Product Details
- **Three film presets** in one pack (160/400/800)
- Includes **Mastin 3-Step Workflow:** Apply preset → Adjust exposure → Fine-tune white balance
- **6 Tone Profiles** inspired by Fuji Frontier scanner
- **2 Grain Settings** (35mm, medium format)
- **AI Tools:** Spotlight, Light & Airy Assist, Dark & Moody Assist, Skysave, Skin Smoothing
- **Specialty Tools:** Orange Reduction, Strobe Soften, Dynamic Noise Reduction, Lens Correction
- Compatible with Lightroom Classic 8.0+, Lightroom CC 3.0+, ACR 10.3+
- Compatible with all camera brands and raw formats

### Community Reception
- Widely regarded as the industry standard for professional wedding/portrait photographers
- Portra 160 preset praised for skin tones; some users find it *too* subtle and need to add contrast
- The included Tone Profiles (Frontier scanner emulation) are a key differentiator
- Some r/Lightroom users prefer Alex Ruskman's presets as "more velvety" at a lower price point

## 2. RNI (Really Nice Images) — All Films 5

**Product:** RNI All Films 5 (Pro/Lite)  
**Price:** ~$90–150 (varies by tier)  
**Contains:** Comprehensive film stock library including Portra 160 variants

### How RNI Describes Portra 160
RNI profiles Portra 160 across multiple scanner interpretations. Their approach:
- **Frontier version:** Cooler, slightly cyan-leaning, softer highlight roll-off
- **Noritsu version:** Warmer, slightly more saturated, punchier midtones
- **Varying exposure offsets** (box speed, +1, +2 stops)

### Key Differentiator
RNI uses **camera profiles** (dcp/dcp profiles) rather than pure presets, which provides more accurate per-camera color response. This is significant because:
- ACR/LR rendering differs between camera brands (Canon vs. Sony vs. Nikon vs. Fuji)
- Profile-based emulation accounts for sensor spectral response differences

### Community Reception
- r/Lightroom comparison threads show RNI vs. Color Precision comparisons
- RNI Portra 400 compared favorably; Portra 160 quality depends on scanner variant chosen
- Users on r/Lightroom have posted side-by-side comparisons (see r/Lightroom post "RNI vs Color Precision — Portra 400 comparison")

## 4. VSCO Film 06 — The Discontinued Benchmark

**Product:** VSCO Film 06 (Alternative Process / Push/Pull pack)  
**Status:** **Discontinued** — no longer sold by VSCO  
**Contained:** Portra 160, Portra 160+, Portra 400, Portra 800, and many others

### Why VSCO 06 Remains the Reference
Despite being discontinued ~2019, VSCO Film 06 is *still* the most-referenced pack in community discussions. The Portra 160+ preset in particular is the benchmark against which all others are compared.

**Key VSCO Portra 160 preset characteristics (reverse-engineered):**
- Custom camera profiles (DCP) for accurate per-brand color
- Separate presets for Portra 160 (box speed) and Portra 160+ (overexposed + pushed)
- Grain calibration matched to actual film scans
- Fade curves developed from optical RA-4 print measurements

**Community sentiment:**
- > *"I've been using a few VSCO presets that I tweaked (mostly Portra 160+400 from the 06 pack)"* — r/Lightroom user
- > *"Looking for an old VSCO preset 'Portra 160+'"* — r/Lightroom (2022)
- > *"VSCO KODAK Portra 160, 400 and 800 (in search of)"* — r/Lightroom (2019)
- VSCO 06 A1 = Kodak Portra 160+ (+1 Tint) mapped to the mobile app

## Summary — Which Pack to Buy for Portra 160?

| Priority | Pack | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| **Most accurate** | RNI All Films 5 (Frontier variant) | Profile-based, scanner-specific, per-camera calibration |
| **Best workflow** | Mastin Labs Portra Original | 3-step workflow, AI tools, grain/tone controls, best support |
| **Best value** | Alex Ruskman 35mm Pack | 200+ XMP for ~$20, strong Portra 160 emulation |
| **Historical benchmark** | VSCO Film 06 | Discontinued, but the gold standard for community comparison |
| **Scanner variety** | Color Precision | Separate Noritsu/Frontier variants for different looks |

**Universal warning from community:** No preset is "apply and done" for Portra 160. The film's soft contrast and skin rendition are highly dependent on exposure and base white balance. Every preset needs per-image adjustment of Exposure, WB, and Tone Curve.
