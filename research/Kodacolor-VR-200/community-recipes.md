# Kodacolor VR 200 — Community Lightroom Recipes

> **Compiled from:** Fuji X Weekly (Thomas Schwab / Ritchie Roesch), r/analog, r/Lightroom, r/postprocessing.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy

Kodacolor VR 200 emulation requires:
1. Very warm global cast (golden-amber)
2. Soft/low contrast — old print feel
3. Prominent, rough 1980s consumer grain
4. Faded/desaturated cool tones
5. Lifted blacks (no true black)

## 3. Community Slider Ranges

### Basic Panel
- Exposure: +0.05 to +0.15
- Contrast: -10 to -20 (soft, old print)
- Highlights: -30 to -40
- Shadows: +15 to +25
- Whites: -10 to -20
- Blacks: +10 to +20 (lifted, milky)

### HSL — Faded 80s Palette
- Red Sat: 0 to +5 (subdued)
- Orange Sat: +5 to +10 (warm dominant)
- Yellow Sat: +5 to +10
- Green Sat: -10 to -20 (muted)
- Blue Sat: -15 to -25 (heavily desat)
- Magenta Sat: -20 to -30 (suppressed)
- Orange Lum: +5 to +15
- Green Lum: -5 to -10

### Color Grading (warming substitute for 3000K WB)
- Shadows: H200-210, Sat 10-15 (teal shadow for warm/teal dynamic)
- Highlights: H40-50, Sat 10-15 (golden)
- Balance: -20 to -30 (shadow bias)

### Grain
- Amount: 40-50 (prominent 1980s consumer grain)
- Size: 30-40
- Roughness: 55-65

---

## 4. Common Mistakes

1. **Too much contrast** — 80s consumer prints were soft
2. **True blacks** — blacks should be warm/dark brown, not black
3. **Strong blues** — blue sky should be muted cyan, not deep blue
4. **Clean grain** — the grain is essential to the aged look
5. **Too much saturation** — Color -4 in recipe means noticeably desaturated
