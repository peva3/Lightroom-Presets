# CineStill 400D — Community Lightroom Recipes

> **Compiled from:** Fuji X Weekly (Ritchie Roesch & Nestor Pool), r/analog, r/Lightroom, r/postprocessing, YouTube creators.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy

CineStill 400D emulation in Lightroom centers on:
1. Warm daylight cinematic palette (not teal/orange heavy)
2. Flat contrast with gentle highlight roll-off
3. Fine grain with cinematic texture
4. Red/magenta bias in midtones
5. Reduced clarity for halation-like softness

## 3. Community Slider Ranges

### Basic Panel
- Exposure: 0 to +0.30
- Contrast: -5 to -10 (cinema flat)
- Highlights: -40 to -50
- Shadows: +10 to +20
- Whites: -5 to +5
- Blacks: 0 to +10

### HSL Adjustments
- Red Sat: +5 to +15 (warm reds)
- Orange Sat: +5 to +15
- Yellow Sat: 0 to +5
- Green Sat: -5 to -15 (muted)
- Blue Sat: -5 to -10 (slight mute)
- Orange Lum: +5 to +15

### Color Grading
- Shadows: H200-210, Sat 5-10 (slight cool)
- Highlights: H35-45, Sat 5-10 (warm)
- Blending: 50

### Grain
- Amount: 25-35 (fine cinema grain)
- Size: 20-25
- Roughness: 40-50

---

## 4. Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Too much contrast** — 400D scans flat; heavy contrast kills cinema look
2. **Overdone teal/orange** — This isn't a blockbuster grade, it's subtle
3. **No grain** — Fine grain is part of the aesthetic
4. **Clarity abuse** — Negative clarity for bloom should be simulated via Highlights/Shadows, not Clarity+1
5. **Bright saturated greens** — Cinema stock renders greens subdued
