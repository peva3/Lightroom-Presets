# CineStill 400D — Full Aesthetic Breakdown

## Overview

CineStill 400D is a daylight-balanced cinema color negative film adapted for C41 processing, created by removing the Remjet layer from Kodak Vision3 250D motion picture stock. The "D" stands for "Dynamic" (not "Daylight"), referring to its large push-processing latitude. Unlike traditional still-photography films, 400D scans flat and requires post-processing — a characteristic inherited from cinema workflows where color grading is expected.

The defining visual trait is **red halation around bright highlights**, caused by the missing Remjet layer which normally absorbs light scatter. This produces a dreamy, cinematic glow unique to CineStill films.

## 1. Color Palette

| Channel | Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Reds** | Rich, warm cinema reds; prominent in skin tones |
| **Oranges** | Warm and cinematic; golden-hour skin |
| **Yellows** | Golden-warm, daylight cinematic palette |
| **Greens** | Muted, slightly warm; not vivid |
| **Blues** | Clean, slightly cool; unclipped |
| **Shadows** | Slightly cool/teal cinematic balance |

### Color Temperature
- Daylight balanced (~5500K)
- Warm overall character from Vision3 color science
- Slight red/magenta lean in midtones (v2 recipe characteristic)

---

## 2. Contrast Characteristics

- **Moderate contrast** — cinema films are designed to scan flat
- Gentle highlight roll-off (Vision3 heritage)
- Shadow detail well preserved
- Designed for post-production grading (flat starting point)

---

## 3. Grain Structure

- Fine grain (Vision3 250D base is very fine-grain cinema stock)
- Finer than consumer still films
- Visible grain from C41 cross-processing (ECN-2 chemicals vs C-41)
- Adds cinematic texture without being dominant

---

## 4. Halation Character

The missing Remjet layer creates the signature:
- **Strong red halation** around bright point light sources
- Glow around specular highlights
- Dreamy, cinematic bloom
- Most visible in night/backlit scenes

## 5. Fuji X Weekly Reference Recipe

**Source:** [Fuji X Weekly — CineStill 400D v2](https://fujixweekly.com/2023/01/17/cinestill-400d-v2-a-fujifilm-x-trans-iv-v-film-simulation-recipe/) (Ritchie Roesch & Nestor Pool)

- Film Simulation: Astia
- Grain: Strong, Small
- Color Chrome Effect: Strong; Color Chrome FX Blue: Weak
- WB: Fluorescent 1 (-2 Red, +4 Blue)
- DR200; Highlight -2; Shadow 0; Color +2
- Sharpness -2; High ISO NR -4; Clarity -4
- Exposure Compensation: +1/3 to +1

This recipe is described as having less contrast and leaning more red/purple than v1. Best suited for golden hour photography, urban/street scenes.

## 6. Summary

> **CineStill 400D is a daylight-balanced cinema film adapted for C41, producing warm cinematic colors with fine grain, flat scanning contrast, and signature red halation around highlights from the removed Remjet layer.**
