## The Fuji C200 Connection

Agfa Vista 200 was **manufactured by Fujifilm** and sold under the AgfaPhoto brand through a licensing agreement. The physical film cassettes bore Fuji edge-code markings identical to Fuji C200 — in fact, the negatives literally read "C200" along the rebate edge.

However, significant community debate exists about whether it was **exactly** the same emulsion:

### Evidence It Was C200:
- Negatives say "C200" on the rebate (confirmed by r/AnalogCommunity users)
- Cassette number codes matched Fuji C200
- Wide consensus in film community
- Japan Camera Hunter (2018): "It's a poorly-kept secret that Vista was actually produced by our favorite Japanese film manufacturer"

### Evidence It Was Different:
- Side-by-side Photrio tests (May 2018) showed AgfaPhoto Vista 200 rendering different color balance vs. current Fuji C200 — Vista was described as "better balanced" and "looks best from those four"
- r/AnalogCommunity: "The colors are 'harder' on C200, the shadows are too green/magenta/brown"
- Veteran shooters noted Vista looked more like **Fuji Superia 200** than C200
- One theory: AgfaPhoto Vista 200 was an **older formulation of Fuji C200** or a **slightly tweaked emulsion** made specifically for the AgfaPhoto contract
- Another theory from Nodda Duma (Photrio): "It could be the same emulsion but stored for a different period of time prior to being shipped out. Emulsions continue to ripen after being coated."

### Most Likely Reality:
Agfa Vista 200 was a Fuji-manufactured C-41 color negative film based on the C200/Superia architecture, possibly with a slightly different emulsion formulation or aging protocol that produced its characteristic "more vibrant/balanced" look that many shooters preferred over the standard C200 consumer offering.

## Rumored Technical Specifications

Since no official AgfaPhoto-published datasheet exists (the film was a rebrand), specifications are inferred from Fuji C200:

- **ISO Sensitivity:** ISO 200/24°
- **Color Balance:** Daylight (5500K)
- **Process:** C-41 standard
- **Grain:** Consumer-grade, moderate visible grain; described as slightly more grain than C200
- **Sharpness:** Consumer-grade; good but not exceptional
- **Exposure Latitude:** Wide — known to handle overexposure well. Some users shot at EI 100 for "awesome colours"
- **Push Capability:** Known to push well — examples at EI 800 exist with good results
- **Shelf Life:** Similar to C200; 14-year-expired rolls still produce images (with heavy color shifts/light leaks)
- **Contrast:** Medium-high consumer contrast — described as "poppy"
- **Saturation:** Over-the-top consumer saturation, especially in reds and primary colors

## Pricing History

- UK (Poundland): **£1 per roll** (24 exp) — this was the legendary deal
- Germany drugstores (DM/Rossmann): ~€2.50 for 24 exp ISO 200
- Hungary (Mueller): 3-pack of 400 ASA for ~€9
- After discontinuation (2018+): expired rolls on eBay for **$15/roll** (up from ~$6)
- 2002 France: 1.90 euros for a roll of consumer color film

## Discontinuation

- Production stopped **early 2018**, confirmed by Japan Camera Hunter (March 2018)
- Both Vista 200 and Vista 400 discontinued simultaneously
- Poundland (UK) stopped stocking in late 2017 — early warning sign
- Stock dwindled to "Sumatran Rhinoceros numbers" in Tokyo by March 2018
- Part of Fujifilm's broader retreat from the consumer color negative film market

## Recommended Alternatives (per community)

- **Kodak ColorPlus 200** — similar budget tier, warm tones
- **Kodak Gold 200** — slightly warmer, more grain
- **Fujicolor C200** — the closest surviving relative (though described as not the same)
- **Kodak UltraMax 400** — one stop faster, similar consumer punch
