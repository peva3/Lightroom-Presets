# Light and Airy Fine Art — Reference Preset Packs

A comprehensive survey of paid preset packs, profiles, and editing tools that target the Light and Airy Fine Art aesthetic. Sorted by prominence in the wedding/portrait fine-art community.

---

## Tier 1: Industry Standards (Premium / High-End)

### REFINED x KT Merry
- **Creator**: KT Merry (fine-art wedding & editorial photographer) × Marta Locklear / REFINED Co
- **Price**: $175
- **Format**: Desktop (Lightroom/ACR), Mobile, Capture One
- **Description**: The gold standard for film-matching fine-art presets. Designed to match digital images to KT Merry's film scans. Five base presets with variable intensity. Specifically built for hybrid film+digital wedding workflows. Emulates the look of scanned film with lab-level accuracy.
- **Why it's cited**: KT Merry is a recognized fine-art photographer; the presets were years in development; widely recommended in wedding photography circles.
- **URL**: refinedco.com/products/ktmerry

### Mastin Labs — Fuji Original
- **Creator**: Kirk Mastin
- **Price**: $99
- **Format**: Lightroom Desktop, Capture One, Mobile
- **Description**: The definitive light-and-airy film emulation pack. Contains Fuji 400H (Neutral + Blue variants), Fuji 160NS, and Fuji 800Z presets. 400H is *the* signature light & airy film stock — pastel tones, minty greens, cyan undertones, low saturation. Includes the Mastin 3-Step Workflow and Magic Toolkit.
- **Reddit mentions**: Frequently cited in r/WeddingPhotography as one of the few preset packs "actually worth it."
- **URL**: mastinlabs.com

### DVLOP — Jose Villa Presets
- **Creator**: Jose Villa (legendary fine-art film wedding photographer) × DVLOP
- **Description**: Jose Villa is the godfather of fine-art wedding photography. His DVLOP pack emulates the Fuji 400H look with both Noritsu and Frontier scanner profiles. Often mentioned alongside Mastin Labs as the best option for achieving the fine-art film look.
- **Note**: DVLOP uses a two-profile system (base + scanner) that produces very authentic film emulations.
- **Reddit mentions**: "You can check the new Jose Villa sets from DVLOP and they are very similar, they used both scanners."

### Visual Flow — Pastel Pack
- **Creator**: Pye Jirsa (SLR Lounge) × DVLOP
- **Price**: Part of Visual Flow suite
- **Format**: Lightroom (XMP, DNG for mobile), ACR
- **Description**: Lighting Condition-Based Development system. 10 presets each optimized for a specific lighting scenario (Soft Light, Hard Light, Backlit, HDR Natural, Tungsten, etc.). Warm pastel tones, lifted shadows, creamy skin tones. Tested across 10,000+ real images. Trusted by 50,000+ photographers.
- **Unique feature**: Lighting-specific presets that don't break across different shooting conditions.
- **URL**: vfpresets.com/pastel-lightroom-presets-pack/

### Visual Flow — Pure Pack
- **Price**: Part of Visual Flow suite
- **Description**: Cleaner, more neutral version of Pastel. Cooler, editorial brightness. Same lighting-condition system. Preferred by photographers who want "bright and clean" without the warm pastel color cast.

## Tier 2: Popular / Widely Used

- **Price**: $95–$175 per collection
- **Format**: Lightroom Desktop & Mobile, Capture One, Video LUTs
- **Collections**: REFINED I, II, III, IV; REFINED x Caroline Tran; REFINED x Tec Petaja; REFINED x Ali Harper; REFINED Kodak Gold; REFINED Ektar; REFINED Velvetier
- **Description**: Broadest light & airy catalog outside of Mastin. Each REFINED generation (I–IV) offers different flavors — from clean and editorial to warm and filmic. All grounded in photographic principles, not trendy color grades.
- **URL**: refinedco.com/collections/light-and-airy-presets

### Greater Than Gatsby — Watercolor Film Collection

- **Format**: Lightroom CC & Classic, ACR
- **Description**: Emulates Fuji Pro 400H with "minty tones" and pastel undertones. 23 Artistic Film Presets + 10 GTG Standard Film Presets (grain, sharpen variants). Emphasis on soft and peachy skin tones. Bright and airy with "hints of pastel undertones."

### Mandy Ford — Fine-Art Lightroom Presets

- **Format**: Lightroom Desktop (XMP)

Game-changing editing toolkit for difficult lighting scenarios. 35 intensity presets & adjustments + 11 undertone profiles + exclusive editing community. Intensity-slider capable. Designed for wedding-day lighting: reception, indoor, outdoor, mixed.

One of the top-rated brands on Etsy/Creative Market since 2016. Trusted by 750,000+ users. Featured by Arrows and Bow, Life & Sprinkles, Adobe.

## What Reddit Recommends

From the r/AskPhotography megathread (162 upvotes, Jul 2024):

**Worth buying:**
- Mastin Labs / RNI — "the only filmic preset packs I find useful"
- Emmett Sparling Violet Collection — "useful for one-click scenarios"
- Adrien Sanguinneti — "more mellowed, cinematic"
- Sam Hurd Gainstage — "solid one-click alternative"

**Disappointing:**
- Peter McKinnon — "extreme color play, exposure tuned all over the place"
- James Popsys — "unusable, won't get his signature soft blown out look"
- Fropack — "similar to PM presets, bad"
- Alex Ruskman — "heavily advertised, overhyped"

**General advice:**
- Fix white balance after applying any preset (many don't touch it).
- Adjust exposure until lighting feels natural before judging.
- Use the Amount slider to dial back intensity.
- A preset that works on soft window light will blow highlights in backlit midday sun — lighting matters more than the preset name.

## The "Dirty Boots & Messy Hair" and "G-Presets" Notes

- **Dirty Boots & Messy Hair**: Historically referenced in wedding photography communities as a fine-art/light-and-airy style preset maker. Their dedicated preset page (dirtybootsandmessyhair.com/presets) is currently offline/redirecting. They were known for organic, earthy takes on the light and airy look — slightly warmer and more rustic than the pure film-emulation packs. Worth monitoring for relaunch.

- **G-Presets**: One of the most-cited preset brands in the wedding photography world. Known for a signature light-and-airy fine-art look with warm, golden undertones. Multiple collections target different flavors of the aesthetic. The gpresets.com domain appears to be under maintenance or has moved. They remain heavily name-dropped in forums as a top-tier option alongside Mastin Labs, REFINED, and DVLOP.

- **Other names worth knowing**: Archipelago (moody but has light variants), Noble Presets, VSCO (pioneering, now less relevant), Dawn Charles (popular in wedding circles), Phil Chester (fine-art style), India Earl (popular among portrait/wedding photographers).
