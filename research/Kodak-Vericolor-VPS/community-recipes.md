## Community Consensus

### Defining Characteristics
1. **Ultra-low contrast** — the flattest professional film Kodak made
2. **Warm shadow bias** — unique brown warmth in dark areas
3. **1970s aesthetic** — the look of vintage wedding albums
4. **Strong grain** — more prominent than Vericolor III

### Ritchie Roesch's Notes
The author notes the recipe is actually closer to Vericolor II (1977) than VPS (1979), but the naming convention was chosen for recognition. The recipe is "quite versatile — excellent for sunny daylight, golden hour, overcast, natural light indoors, and even certain nighttime situations."

### Lightroom Community Values

From Reddit and community discussions:

```
Contrast: -35 to -45 (ultra-low)
Blacks: +20 to +30 (extremely lifted)
Shadows: +35 to +45 (strong shadow lift)
Highlights: -25 to -35 (mild roll-off)
Saturation: -5 to +5 (pastel)
Color Grading Shadows: Warm brown (Hue 25-40°, Sat 5-10)
Grain: Amount 30-40, Size 30-40, Roughness 50-60
```

### Key Warning
Community users note that this preset can appear "washed out" on modern high-contrast digital images. It thrives on soft, even lighting. Overcast days, open shade, and studio flash produce the best results.
