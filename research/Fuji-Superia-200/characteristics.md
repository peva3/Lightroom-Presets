# Fuji Superia 200 — Aesthetic Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

## Overview

Fuji Superia 200 was the **mid-speed consumer color negative film** in Fujifilm's Superia lineup — positioned between the fine-grain Superia 100 and the versatile Superia 400. It offered a balanced combination of the Fuji consumer palette (cool bias, green shadows, magenta-reds) with good speed for everyday use and moderate grain. It occupied a sweet spot in the lineup that neither 100 nor 400 quite matched. This document serves as a reference for creating a Lightroom preset that emulates Superia 200.

## Core Aesthetic Signature

### The Fuji Cool-Neutral Balance

Superia 200 sits at the intersection of the Superia family's characteristics:
- **Cooler than Kodak** but **warmer than Superia 100** in practice
- The cool bias is present but more balanced than 100
- Clean white rendering with a very slight neutral-cool cast

### Grain: The "Just Right" Consumer Texture

- **Fine-to-moderate grain** — finer than Superia 400, coarser than 100
- Visible on close inspection but not distracting
- Pleasant organic texture at normal viewing sizes
- Typical Fuji consumer grain character — slightly irregular, not perfectly uniform

### Green Shadow Cast

- **Moderate green shadow** — more pronounced than Superia 100, less than Superia 400
- The signature Fuji family trait at a moderate intensity
- Creates a subtle cool undercurrent without the "swampy" character of 400
- Most visible in underexposed areas and overcast conditions

### Magenta-Red Rendering

- Reds lean magenta-crimson, characteristic of Fuji consumer films
- **Stronger red punch than 100**, slightly less aggressive than 400
- Good red saturation without bleeding
- Skin tones rendered warmly within a cooler overall frame

### Greens

- Cool, authentic green — the Fuji look
- Richer than 100, comparable to 400 in saturation
- Not pushed toward yellow (unlike Kodak)

### Blues

- Strong, saturated blues typical of Fuji consumer dyes
- Clean sky rendering with subtle depth
- Less blue channel noise than Superia 400
- Slight cyan influence in cooler light

### Skin Tones

- More natural than Superia 400
- Warmer than Superia 100's cooler rendering
- The most balanced skin tones in the consumer Superia lineup
- Good results with flash (4th color layer helps)

### Contrast Profile

- **Moderate consumer contrast**
- Good midtone separation
- Between Superia 100 (higher contrast) and 400 (more compressed)
- Shadows lift slightly, preserving detail

### Exposure Latitude

- Good latitude within ±2 stops
- +1/3 to +2/3 overexposure recommended
- Handles underexposure better than 100
- Box speed produces excellent results in mixed conditions

## Fuji X Weekly / t3mujinpack Data

Based on Superia 100 recipe with adjustments for the 200 speed:
- Slightly more grain than 100
- Slightly warmer than 100
- More contrast compression than 100 (faster film = less inherent contrast)
- Similar green shadow cast intensity

## Comparison to Key Films

| Characteristic | Superia 100 | Superia 200 | Superia 400 | Kodak Gold 200 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ISO | 100 | 200 | 400 | 200 |
| Color Cast | Cool | Cool-neutral | Cool | Warm golden |
| Grain | Very fine | Fine | Fine-medium | Moderate |
| Green Shadows | Subtle | Moderate | Pronounced | None |
| Red Rendering | Magenta-red | Magenta-red | Magenta-red | Orange-red |
| Contrast | Higher | Moderate | Moderate | Moderate-high |
| Status | Discontinued | Discontinued | Discontinued 2024 | Available |

## Best Use Cases

- General-purpose daylight photography
- Travel and casual shooting
- Mixed indoor/outdoor use
- Subjects requiring good detail with moderate grain
- Any situation where Superia 100 is too slow and Superia 400 too grainy

## Weaknesses

- Discontinued and rare
- Less distinctive than 100 or 400 (the "middle child" effect)
- No longer available even as expired stock in most markets
- Not as well documented as 400

## Summary

> Fuji Superia 200 delivered the **balanced heart of the Superia lineup** — the cool Fuji consumer palette with fine grain, moderate contrast, and the characteristic green shadow cast at a moderate intensity. It occupied the sweet spot between the fine-grain purity of Superia 100 and the punchy character of Superia 400, making it the most versatile all-purpose Superia film for everyday photography.
