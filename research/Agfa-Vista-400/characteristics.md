# Agfa Vista 400 — Aesthetic Characteristics for Lightroom Presets

## Overview

Agfa Vista 400 was the **fastest film in the Agfa Vista consumer lineup** — the high-speed companion to Vista 100 and 200. Like all Vista films, it was manufactured by Fujifilm and sold under the AgfaPhoto brand at drugstore prices. At ISO 400, it traded the fine grain of slower Vistas for extra speed and notably warmer color rendering, making it a versatile all-purpose consumer film. This document serves as a reference for creating a Lightroom preset that emulates Vista 400.

## Core Aesthetic Signature

### The Warmest Vista

Unlike the cool-neutral Vista 100 and slightly warm Vista 200, Vista 400 has a **distinctly warm color cast**:
- Stronger golden-yellow bias than any other Vista speed
- Warmer whites with cream/ivory character
- More similar to Kodak consumer films in temperature than to Fuji
- This warmth is anomalous in the Fuji-made Vista line — possibly from different emulsion chemistry

### Primary Color Saturation

Like all Vista films, primaries are saturated and vibrant:
- **Reds are explosive** — possibly the most saturated reds in the Vista line
- The red channel is deep, vibrant, and slightly warm-leaning (unlike Vista 200's crimson)
- Blues maintain strong saturation despite warmer overall cast
- Yellows are amplified by the warm bias

### Grain Structure

- **Moderate, visible grain** — expected for ISO 400 consumer film
- Coarser than both Vista 100 and 200
- Pleasant organic texture rather than harsh digital noise
- Similar grain intensity to Fuji Superia 400
- Most visible in midtones and open skies

### Shadow Character

- **Warm brown shadows** — closer to Kodak Gold than Fuji Superia
- Less green cast than equivalent Fuji-branded consumer films
- Shadow compression is moderate; detail preserved
- The warm shadow character complements the overall golden bias

### Skin Tones

- Warm, flattering skin rendering
- More golden than Vista 200
- Good for caucasian skin tones in natural light
- Slightly less accurate for darker skin tones in warm light

### Contrast Profile

- **Moderate consumer contrast** — slightly lower than Vista 200
- The higher speed trades some contrast for latitude
- Good midtone pop despite moderate overall contrast
- Clean highlight roll-off

### Exposure Latitude

- Good latitude for consumer 400 film
- Handles +1 to +2 stops overexposure well
- Overexposure warms and softens the image further
- Rate at EI 200-320 for best saturation and reduced grain
- Box speed produces punchy, grainier results

## Comparison to Key Films

| Characteristic | Vista 100 | Vista 200 | Vista 400 | Superia X-TRA 400 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ISO | 100 | 200 | 400 | 400 |
| Color Cast | Cool-neutral | Slightly warm | Warm | Cool |
| Grain | Very fine | Fine | Moderate | Fine-medium |
| Red Saturation | High | Very high | Very high | High |
| Shadow Color | Cool | Slightly warm | Warm brown | Green |
| Contrast | Higher | Moderate-high | Moderate | Moderate |
| Status | Discontinued | Discontinued | Discontinued | Discontinued 2024 |

## Best Use Cases

- General-purpose everyday photography
- Overcast and lower-light conditions
- Portrait photography (warm, flattering skin)
- Flash photography (good consumer flash results)
- Travel and casual shooting

## Weaknesses

- Warmer than other Vista films — may conflict with cool scenes
- Visible grain limits enlargement beyond 8x10
- Green shadows (Fuji trait) are replaced by warm brown shadows (less distinctive)
- Discontinued and increasingly expensive as expired stock
- Less "unique" look than Vista 200

## Summary

> Agfa Vista 400 produced images with a **warm golden cast**, **moderate visible grain**, **explosive primary color saturation** (especially deep, vibrant reds), and **warm brown shadows**. It was the warmest, speediest, and most versatile Agfa Vista — the practical everyday choice for photographers who needed ISO 400 speed with the Vista family's characteristically saturated consumer color.
