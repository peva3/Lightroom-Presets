# Kodak Portra 800 - Technical Datasheet

**Source**: Kodak Publication E-190 (superseded by E-4040 in 2006), ManualsLib, Kodak Alaris product page

## Overview

KODAK PROFESSIONAL PORTRA 800 Film is a color negative film (C-41 process) with the highest film speed in the Portra family. It delivers well-balanced color saturation, very fine grain, and best-in-class underexposure latitude.

- **Film Type**: Color Negative (C-41)
- **ISO**: 800 (daylight balanced)
- **Formats**: 35mm, 120, sheet
- **Emulsion Technology**: T-GRAIN emulsions, Vision3-based (oldest Portra formulation)
- **Brand Color**: Approx #B8866B (warm beige/brown cast)

## Key Official Specifications

- **Single Channel Printing**: Same scanner setup for all PORTRA Films
- **True-to-Speed ISO 800**: Shoot at box speed in all lighting conditions
- **Exposure Latitude**: Forgiveness for up to 2 stops underexposure or 3 stops overexposure
- **Emulsion Overcoat**: Enhanced scanning performance
- **Neutral Flesh-Tone Reproduction**: Medium color saturation with neutral balanced tone scale

## Characteristic Curves (from Kodak E-190 datasheet)

### At EI 800 (Box Speed - Normal Process)
- **Densitometry**: Status M, Log H Ref -1.74
- **Exposure**: Daylight
- **Process**: Normal C-41
- Three curves showing B (blue), G (green), R (red) density vs. log exposure (lux-seconds)
- Curves span from approximately -4.0 to +1.0 log exposure range

### At EI 1600 (Push +1 - 1 Stop Push Process)
- Same densitometry and exposure conditions
- Process: 1 Stop Push
- Characteristic curves shift accordingly for pushed development
- Increased contrast and grain vs box speed

### At EI 3200 (Push +2 - 2 Stop Push Process)
- Same densitometry and exposure conditions
- Process: 2 Stop Push
- Most pronounced contrast of all three settings
- Significant grain increase but Kodak officially documents this EI

## Spectral Sensitivity Curves

**Effective Exposure**: 1/200 sec, Daylight, Status M densitometry, Density: 0.2 above D-min

Three layers measured:
- **Yellow-Forming Layer** (Blue-sensitive): Peak sensitivity approximately 350-500nm range
- **Magenta-Forming Layer** (Green-sensitive): Peak sensitivity approximately 450-600nm range
- **Cyan-Forming Layer** (Red-sensitive): Peak sensitivity approximately 550-700nm range

Sensitivity scale: 0.0 to 4.0 (log) where sensitivity = reciprocal of exposure (erg/cm²) required to produce specified density

## Spectral-Dye-Density Curves

Process: C-41, Status M densitometry
- **Midscale Neutral**: Shows the spectral absorption of dyes for a neutral gray exposure
- **Minimum Density (D-min)**: Shows the base + fog coloration across wavelengths 400-750nm
- B, G, R curves show density from 0.0 to approximately 2.5 across 400-700nm

D-min provides insight into the film base color which contributes to the overall warm bias of Portra films.

## Modulation Transfer Function (MTF)

- **Exposure**: Daylight
- **Process**: C-41
- **Spatial Frequency Range**: 1 to 200 cycles/mm
- Response measured in % (1 to 200 on log scale)
- Demonstrates the film's ability to reproduce fine detail at various spatial frequencies
- Higher MTF at low frequencies = good contrast in larger details
- Roll-off at higher frequencies determines perceived sharpness/grain

## Storage and Handling

- Store unexposed film at 13°C (55°F) or lower
- For long-term storage (6+ months), keep at -18°C to -23°C (0°F to -10°F)
- Allow film to warm to room temperature before opening (prevent condensation)
- Process promptly after exposure
- Keep exposed film cool and dry if processing is delayed

## Processing

- **Standard process**: C-41 (KODAK FLEXICOLOR chemicals)
- Development time and temperature per standard C-41 specifications
- Push processing requires extended development time per lab specifications
- Push +1: approximately 30% longer development
- Push +2: approximately 60% longer development (exact times vary by lab)

## Reciprocity Failure Compensation

For long exposures, Portra 800 requires reciprocity compensation. As one of the faster films, it has better reciprocity characteristics than slower emulsions:
- Exposures 1-10 seconds: +0.5 to +1 stop compensation recommended
- Exposures 10-100 seconds: +1.5 to 2+ stops compensation typical
(The exact reciprocity failure curve is film-stock specific; Portra 800 generally handles long exposures better than Portra 160/400 due to its higher speed emulsion.)

## Comparison to Other Portra Films

| Property | Portra 160 | Portra 400 | Portra 800 |
|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Grain | Finest | Very fine | Fine (most visible) |
| Saturation | Medium | Medium-high | High (richest at box speed) |
| Contrast | Low-medium | Medium | Medium-high |
| Latitude | Excellent | Excellent | Excellent (best underexposure) |
| Skin tones | Natural | Natural-warm | Warm-natural |
| Base ISO | 160 | 400 | 800 |

Note: Portra 800 is the oldest emulsion design in the current Portra lineup and is based on Kodak Vision3 technology, giving it more character and personality compared to the 2006-reformulated 160 and 400 emulsions.
