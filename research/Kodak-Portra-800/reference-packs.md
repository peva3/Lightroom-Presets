## Mastin Labs - Portra Pushed Pack

**Product**: Portra Pushed Lightroom Desktop Presets
**Price**: $99 (often on sale for $49)
**Contains**: 6 film presets, 18 AI & Specialty Tools (Mastin Magic Toolkit)

Mastin Labs has a dedicated "Portra Pushed" pack specifically targeting pushed Portra film looks, including Portra 800 at +1 and +2 stops.

### Individual Preset Descriptions (from Mastin Labs product page)

**Portra 800+1**
- *Description*: "Balanced and natural, Portra 800+1 is a medium saturated look with higher contrast and purple shifts in the shadows for a stylized and edgy feeling."
- *Ideal for*: Boudoir, indoor lifestyle, adventure elopements
- *Characteristics*: Medium saturation, higher contrast, purple shadow cast
- *Mood*: Dark & Moody collection

**Portra 800+2**
- *Description*: "This look takes the look of Portra 800+1 and pushes the shadows even further for the ultimate moody look."
- *Characteristics*: Deepest shadows, most dramatic contrast
- *Mood*: Ultra dark and moody

**Portra 160+1**
- Neutral with medium contrast, flexible look, low saturation, tones lean reds and brown

**Portra 400+1**
- Warm-toned, medium contrast, extremely flexible, deep rich color with clean tones

**Portra 160+2**
- Progression on 160+1, increased red tones, flatness in shadows

**Portra 400+2**
- Evolution on 400+1, increased red and orange shifts in shadows, editorial look

### Key Observations from Mastin's Portra 800 Approach:
- They explicitly note **purple shifts in shadows** as a Portra 800 characteristic
- The pushed versions are considered "dark and moody" - higher contrast than standard Portra
- Portra 800 is positioned as the most dramatic, edgy look in the Portra family
- Skin tones remain "clean and natural" even with the dramatic shadow treatment (per testimonial)
- The Frontier scanner tone profiles provide a specific film lab color response that's distinct from Noritsu

## ColorGrade.org - Portra 800 Description

**Summary**: Professional color negative film, fastest in Portra family, exceptional image quality and fine grain structure at ISO 800.

**Key Characteristics Documented**:
- Remarkable ability to maintain fine grain and accurate skin tones despite high speed
- Excels in low-light where slower films struggle
- Color palette leans warm, natural tones with subtle saturation
- Wide exposure latitude, forgiving metering errors
- Handles highlight and shadow detail better than competing stocks
- Performs beautifully in golden hour and indoor lighting
- Can be pushed to ISO 1600 or 3200 with acceptable results
- When shot at box speed or overexposed by a stop: dreamy, soft images with creamy skin tones
- Premium price point, reliable workhorse, industry standard

## VSCO Film (Historical Reference)

VSCO Film (Visual Supply Co) was the industry-standard film emulation pack (discontinued in 2019).

### VSCO Portra 800 Profile Characteristics (from community documentation):
- VSCO categorized Portra 800 in their "Portra" family alongside 160 and 400
- VSCO Portra 800 was known for warmer tones than VSCO Portra 400
- Stronger grain profile than their 400 preset
- Higher contrast curve with deeper blacks
- VSCO's approach used camera-calibrated profiles (one per camera model) for accuracy
- Their Portra 800 profile was particularly popular for wedding and portrait work
- VSCO film packs are no longer sold but legacy users maintain them

### VSCO Portra 800 Tool Kit (approximate from legacy documentation):
- Camera-specific base profile
- Grain preset calibrated to match Portra 800 RMS granularity
- Tone curve matched to characteristic curves from Kodak datasheet
- Color shifts based on spectral sensitivity analysis

## RNI (Really Nice Images) - Portra 800 Notes

RNI (Really Nice Images) produces high-end film emulation profiles.

### RNI Portra 800 General Approach (from community reviews):
- RNI offers Portra 800 profiles in their "RNI All Films" packs
- Known for more subtle, natural color treatment compared to Mastin
- RNI Portra 800 profiles focus on accurate color reproduction over dramatic styling
- Multiple scanner variants (Noritsu and Frontier emulations)
- Noritsu variant: cleaner, more neutral, slightly cooler
- Frontier variant: warmer, more contrast, stronger color
- RNI profiles typically require less adjustment after application than VSCO or Mastin

## Summary: Commercial Pack Approach to Portra 800

| Feature | Mastin Labs | VSCO (Legacy) | RNI | Notes |
|---------|-------------|---------------|-----|-------|
| Warmth | Strong (stylized) | Moderate (natural) | Subtle (accurate) | Portra 800 baseline is warm |
| Contrast | High (dark & moody) | Medium-high | Medium | Push processing adds more |
| Grain | 2 settings (35mm/MF) | Film-matched | Multiple options | Grain is a key differentiator |
| Shadow tint | Purple-magenta | Neutral-warm | Slightly cool | Mastin explicitly calls out purple |
| Skin tones | Clean even when pushed | Natural | Accurate | Skin tone is the priority for all |
| Scanner emulation | Frontier profiles | Not disclosed | Frontier + Noritsu | Frontier = warmer/more contrast |

### Common Threads Across All Packs:
1. **Warm color temperature bias** (warmer than digital baseline)
2. **Creamy, luminous skin tones** (orange/red luminance lifted)
3. **Pleasing grain** (organic, not digital-looking)
4. **Compressed highlights with shadow detail** (film latitude)
5. **Green desaturation** (characteristic Kodak/Portra trait)
6. **Higher contrast at box speed** compared to Portra 400
7. **Purple/magenta undertones in deep shadows** (especially at push)

### When Portra 800 is Recommended by Packs:

- Wedding receptions
- Boudoir photography
- Indoor lifestyle
- Adventure/outdoor elopements
- Concert photography
- Any high-ISO scenario where flash isn't ideal
- Street photography at night
- Golden hour with subject backlit
