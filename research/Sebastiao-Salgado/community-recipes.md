# Community Recipes — Sebastião Salgado

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/photojournalism
- "Salgado contrast" is a known editing search term — dramatic but never crushed
- Pushed Tri-X in D-76 or HC-110 is the analog starting point discussed on r/analog
- Highlights should glow — this is the #1 requested characteristic

### From YouTube Tutorials
- Several tutorials specifically named "Salgado Style B&W" or "Genesis Style"
- Common approach: high contrast, deep shadows, lift the white point
- Texture slider used for skin/earth detail — but careful not to oversharpen

### From Workshops/Courses
- Salgado's own workflow (as revealed in interviews): Tri-X pushed 1–2 stops, selenium toned prints
- Long tonal scale despite high contrast — this is the print technique, hard to replicate in one preset

### Community Consensus
- The "Salgado look" means dramatic contrast with preserved highlight detail
- Sky treatment is critical — blue channel pulled significantly
- Grain adds gravitas — pushed Tri-X structure
- Not for every image; works best on images with strong light/dark division
