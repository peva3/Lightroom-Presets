# Sebastião Salgado

## Biography
- **Name**: Sebastião Ribeiro Salgado (born 1944)
- **Nationality**: Brazilian
- **Era**: 1970s–present

## Philosophy
- Long-term documentary projects (Genesis, Workers, Migrations, Sahel)
- Photography as witness — bearing testimony to human dignity and environmental crisis
- "The picture is not made by the photographer — the picture is more or less good depending on the relationship you have with the people you photograph."
- Former economist (PhD) who abandoned economics for photography

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: Leica M and R series, Pentax 645, Canon EOS
- **Film**: Kodak Tri-X 400 (his signature), later T-Max 3200 for low light; recently digital (Canon)
- **Processing**: Pushed Tri-X for contrast, deep shadow work
- **Printing**: Meticulous darkroom work with heavy burning/dodging; selenium toned

## Visual Signature
- **Biblical luminance**: Highlights that seem to glow from within. Zones VIII–IX placed deliberately.
- **Dramatic skies**: Deep grays with cloud separation — the sky is a character.
- **Deep, rich blacks**: Shadows fall to near-black (Zone I–II) but preserve edge detail.
- **Texture obsession**: Skin, earth, rock, water — every surface is tactile.
- **Grand scale**: Wide-angle intimacy with epic environmental context.
- **High contrast**: Not HDR — deep shadows against luminous highlights, with midtone bridge.

## Iconic Images
- *Gold Mine, Serra Pelada* (1986)
- *Genesis* series (2004–2011)
- *Workers* series (1986–1992)

## Preset Design Notes
- Dramatic contrast with luminous highlight preservation
- Blue channel pulled down significantly for sky drama (like a mild red filter)
- Shadows deep but with a sense of detail at the edge
- Grain present and structured — the pushed-Tri-X look
- Highlights bright but not clipped; Whites slider used carefully
