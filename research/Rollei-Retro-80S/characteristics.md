# Rollei Retro 80S — Film Characteristics

## Overview
Rollei Retro 80S is a super-panchromatic (extended red sensitivity) fine-grain black-and-white film produced by Agfa-Gevaert (Belgium) and sold under the Rollei/Maco brand. It is based on Agfa Aviphot aerial surveillance film emulsion, giving it unique spectral sensitivity characteristics that extend into near-infrared. ISO 80 under normal conditions; ISO 200+ with IR filtration.

## Key Characteristics
- **ISO**: 80 (nominal); can be used at ISO 25-200 depending on developer
- **Grain**: Fine, well-controlled — excellent for a film with extended spectral sensitivity
- **Sharpness**: Very good — aerial surveillance heritage means high-resolution design
- **Contrast**: Moderate — designed for aerial work where tonal separation is critical
- **Spectral Sensitivity**: Super-panchromatic — extended red + near-IR sensitivity
  - Traditional pan films cut off around 650nm; Retro 80S extends to ~750nm
  - This means reds render VERY bright, skies go dramatically dark without filters
- **Exposure Latitude**: Moderate — extended sensitivity requires careful exposure
- **Development**: Works well in standard developers; Rodinal and HC-110 are popular
- **Anti-Halation Layer**: Clear polyester base — produces halation effects around bright highlights (similar to Cinestill)

## Historical Context
- Based on Agfa Aviphot Pan 80 aerial film emulsion
- Agfa-Gevaert (Belgium) produces the master rolls; Rollei/Maco converts and distributes
- Part of Rollei's "Retro" line alongside Retro 400S (also Aviphot-based)
- Highly regarded in the artistic photography community for its unique rendering
- The clear base creates distinctive halation that some photographers seek out

## Visual Signature
- Dramatically dark blue skies without any filter — the super-panchromatic sensitivity does this naturally
- Bright, almost glowing foliage (chlorophyll reflects strongly in near-IR)
- Skin tones render slightly lighter and smoother (extended red penetration)
- Halation around bright highlights creates an ethereal, vintage glow
- Fine grain belies the aerial surveillance origins
- A "dreamy but sharp" aesthetic unique to super-panchromatic films
