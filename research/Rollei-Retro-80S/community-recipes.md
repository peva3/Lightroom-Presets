# Rollei Retro 80S — Community Recipes

## Primary Source: Fuji X Weekly (Ritchie Roesch)
No dedicated Fuji X Weekly recipe exists for Rollei Retro 80S as a standard (non-IR) film. However, Ritchie's "Agfa 400S" recipe (the faster sister film) for full-spectrum IR cameras provides reference values for the super-panchromatic aesthetic.

## Community Notes (from forums)
- Photrio users consistently note the dramatic sky darkening without filters
- The clear polyester base causes halation around bright highlights — similar to Cinestill but B&W
- Many photographers shoot it with a yellow or orange filter for even more dramatic sky effects
- At EI 25 with an R72 (720nm) IR filter, it becomes a true infrared B&W film — a versatile dual-purpose stock
- Development in Rodinal 1+50 produces excellent acutance with controlled grain
- The extended red sensitivity makes skin tones look luminous — flattering for portraiture
- Some forum users note a slight green base tint on the negatives

## t3mujinpack Reference
The t3mujinpack project does not include Rollei Retro films, but their channel mixer approach can be adapted. Super-panchromatic films require significantly boosted red channel weighting.

## LR Preset Design Notes
- Super-panchromatic GrayMixer: Red +35, Blue -30 (bright reds, dark skies) — the defining characteristic
- Fine grain: GrainAmount=25, Size=20, Roughness=45
- Moderate contrast (+20) — not as punchy as Retro 400S
- Controlled highlights (-15) for the aerial film's highlight handling
- Modest shadow lift (+12)
- All STYLEGUIDE rules: Sharpness=10 with grain, Clarity/Texture/Dehaze=0, no Calibration, no WB
- The super-panchromatic GrayMixer values are the key — they create the distinctive sky darkening
- Can be paired with an orange filter (55mm equivalent) for even more dramatic results
