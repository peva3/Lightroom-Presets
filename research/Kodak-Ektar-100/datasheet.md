# Kodak Ektar 100 — Official Datasheet

> Source: Kodak Professional Ektar 100 Film Technical Data (E-4046, February 2016) & E-4051

## Overview

Kodak Professional Ektar 100 Film is the **world's finest-grain color negative film**. With ISO 100 speed, high saturation, and ultra-vivid color, this film offers the finest, smoothest grain of any color negative film available today.

- **Type:** Color negative (C-41 process)
- **Speed:** ISO 100/21° (daylight balanced)
- **Available formats:** 35mm, 120, 4×5, 8×10 sheets
- **Introduced:** September 2008 (relaunch; original Ektar line ran 1989–1997)

## Grain & Image Structure

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Print Grain Index (PGI)** | **<25** (finest of any color negative film) |
| **Sharpness** | Extreme; measured 154 lp/mm at 1000:1 contrast (Zeiss test) |
| **Resolution** | 160 lp/mm (as measured independently, matching/beating Velvia 50) |

At ISO 100, Ektar delivers **16× less visible grain** than a typical ISO 400 consumer film (PGI 25 vs PGI 48+).

### RMS-to-PGI Conversion

- RMS ≈ (PGI / 0.5335) ^ (1 / 2.8669)
- Ektar 100 PGI <25 → estimated RMS ≈ **3.7–3.9**
- For comparison: Velvia 50 RMS = 9 (but slide film RMS is ~2.5× inflated vs print film)

## Characteristic Curves

| Channel | Max Density | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Blue** | 3.1 | High contrast in blue layer |
| **Green** | 2.5 | Moderate contrast |
| **Red** | 2.0 | Lower contrast — reds are rich but not blown |

The steep blue curve and lower red curve are key to Ektar's signature: **brilliant sky blues + warm/rich earth tones without red oversaturation**.

## Spectral Sensitivity

- **Blue layer:** Peak sensitivity ~450–480 nm; designed for vivid blue sky reproduction
- **Green layer:** Peak sensitivity ~540–560 nm; strong green separation for landscape
- **Red layer:** Peak sensitivity ~640–660 nm; shifted slightly warm for enhanced earth tones
- **Overall:** Daylight-balanced (5500K); use 80A filter for tungsten (3200K), EI 25

## Spectral Dye Density Curves

Ektar 100 employs Kodak's modern T-GRAIN emulsion technology with DIR couplers for edge enhancement. The dye density curves show sharp cutoff between color layers, contributing to the "clean, modern" separation between colors that gives Ektar its distinct look — less interlayer crosstalk than traditional films.

## Modulation Transfer Function (MTF)

- High MTF at all spatial frequencies — sharpness exceeds most competing C-41 films
- MTF at 100% crosses at approximately 25 lp/mm (independent measures)
- Contrast at 1000:1 ≈ 154 lp/mm

## Exposure & Latitude

| Condition | Range |
|---|---|
| **Underexposure** | –1 stop (tight — DO NOT underexpose Ektar!) |
| **Overexposure** | +3 stops (more forgiving) |
| **Optimal** | Box speed ISO 100 |

**Critical:** Ektar 100 has a narrower underexposure tolerance (–1 stop) than Portra (–2 to –3 stops). Shadows block up quickly. Meter carefully. Overexposure up to +3 stops is tolerated well, often producing slightly pastel/pop results.

## Storage & Handling

- Store unexposed film at 21°C (70°F) or lower in original sealed package
- For extended periods, store at –18°C (0°F) or lower
- Process exposed film promptly after shooting

## Processing

- Standard C-41 process
- Designed for excellent scanning characteristics; optimized for digital hybrid workflow
- Compatible with RA-4 optical printing
