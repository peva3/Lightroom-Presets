# Reference Packs — What Paid Preset Packs Say About Ektar 100

> What commercial preset creators publish about their Ektar 100 emulations

## Seim Effects / Gavin Seim — Filmist Pack ($89–138)

**Source:** seimeffects.com/2023/01/13/ektar-100-film-preset-for-lightroom-capture-one-filmist-styles/

### Their Take
Gavin Seim describes Ektar 100 as "a classic among films" and one of the few still available. He characterizes it as:
- More robust than Portra
- Finer than Natura 1600
- More complex than Classic Chrome
- An "all-use film" — not just for landscapes

### Development Process (from his blog)
- Shot Ektar himself in 4×5 for reference
- Watched multiple reviews and processing tutorials to understand different development variants
- Combined multiple interpretations to create a "Gen.2" version
- Key focus: **ultra-detailed curves and HSL adjustments**
- Secret: "refine it tirelessly until it works on all image types"

### Seim's Specific Observations
- Ektar has "complex color magic"
- "Often slightly red tint"
- "Deep colors" — requires careful HSL work
- "Warm skin tones in Ektar 100 look great if processed well"
- "Deep reds and nuanced color gradients"
- "Complex shadow definition, fine grain, and color nuance"

### Their Preset Philosophy
- Unlike most companies that ship once, Seim released free Gen.2 updates after hundreds more hours of work
- The Gen.2 Ektar preset is "much more subtle" than the Gen.1
- Has "natural fine-grain film feel with deep reds and nuanced color gradients"
- Works across landscapes, streets, and portraits

### Pricing
- Full Filmist pack (30+ film presets): $89
- Includes Ektar 100 Gen.2 for Lightroom + Capture One
- Free sampler includes Classic Negative and Portra 160 (not Ektar)

## Presetpro.com / Tim Martin — Complete Lightroom Collection ($89–138)

**Source:** presetpro.com/featured-lightroom-preset-kodak-ektar-100-film-emulation/

### Their Take
- Described as: "rich, vibrant, and detailed aesthetics"
- Uses a **custom profile** (not just preset sliders) to emulate Ektar
- One-click application with room for manual fine-tuning

### Key Features They Emphasize
- **Vivid color reproduction:** brilliant reds, vibrant greens, deep blues
- **Ultra-fine grain replication:** minimal noise
- **High contrast:** dynamic range highlighting both shadow and highlight detail
- **Ideal for:** large prints and high-resolution scans

### Their Technical Approach
- Custom-built profile specifically for Ektar 100
- Adjustments: color, contrast, grain, and tone curve
- Works with Lightroom Classic and Lightroom CC
- Compatible with RAW and JPEG (RAW recommended)

### Pricing
- Complete Lightroom Collection (300+ presets + profiles + brushes): $138
- Film Emulation collection standalone: $89
- Individual presets not sold separately

### Target Use Cases
- Nature and outdoor photography
- Travel photography
- Fashion photography
- Product photography

## Mastin Labs

**Note:** Mastin Labs does not offer a specific "Ektar 100" preset in their main packs. Their film packs cover Portra, Fuji, and Ilford. Some community members use Mastin's Fuji 400H or Portra 160 as a base then adjust toward Ektar with increased saturation and contrast.

Community consensus: "Mastin's Portra 160 +20 saturation +15 contrast + warm white balance = 70% of the way to Ektar."

## VSCO Film

**Note:** VSCO Film (discontinued for current Lightroom, but packs 01–07 were widely used before 2018) did not include Ektar 100. Their Kodak offerings were focused on Portra 160/400/800, Gold 100, Tri-X, and T-Max. Community users who wanted Ektar from VSCO typically started from Kodak Portra 160++ (heavily modified).

## RNI Films (Really Nice Images)

RNI Films includes an Ektar 100 profile in their All Films 5 pack. Their approach:
- Profile-based (not slider-based) film emulation
- Known for accurate color separation matching real film scans
- Ektar 100 profile characterized as: "ultra-vivid, high contrast, warm reds, deep blues"
- Pricing: All Films 5 Pro ~$122

## Common Themes Across All Packs

1. **Saturation is the #1 defining trait** — every pack pushes saturation significantly
2. **Grain is removed or minimal** — respecting Ektar's ultra-fine grain
3. **Warm color bias** — yellows and reds enhanced
4. **High contrast** — steeper tone curves than Portra or Gold
5. **Profile-based beats slider-based** — the better packs use camera profiles for accurate color separation
6. **Skin tones are the weak point** — most packs note Ektar is "landscape-first" and recommend adjustments for portraits
7. **No one has a perfect Ektar** — even Seim did a Gen.2 revision; the look is harder to nail than Portra
