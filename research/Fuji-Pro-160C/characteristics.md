# Fuji Pro 160C — Full Aesthetic Characteristics

> Higher-contrast, higher-saturation professional color negative. The "vivid" sibling in Fujifilm's PRO 160 line.

---

## Executive Summary

Fuji Pro 160C was the higher-contrast, higher-saturation variant of Fujifilm's PRO 160 professional color negative line. Designed for commercial work where more "pop" was desired — fashion editorial, product photography, outdoor. Discontinued, but significant enough that Fujifilm's Reala Ace film simulation was designed in its spirit ("C" = contrast/color).

---

## 1. The Color Palette

| Color Region | Pro 160C Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Whites / Highlights** | Clean, slightly warm-tending. More highlight "pop" than 160NS. |
| **Skin Tones** | Warmer than 160NS, with more saturation. Professional and flattering. |
| **Greens** | Fuller, richer. Not yellow like Portra, not cyan-mint like 400H. |
| **Blues** | More saturated, deeper sky rendering. Not pastel. |
| **Reds** | Richer saturation. Professional grade — not consumer-film punchy. |
| **Shadows** | Deeper than 160NS. Cool bias but less pronounced cyan-green cast. |

---

## 2. Contrast & Tonality

- **Moderate-high contrast** (relative to PRO line — still lower than consumer films)
- Clean shadow-to-highlight separation
- Defined mid-tone structure — subjects "pop"
- Less highlight latitude than 160NS (contrast comes from steeper curves)

---

## 3. Comparison: Pro 160C vs Key Films

| Aspect | Pro 160C | Pro 160NS | Portra 160VC |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Contrast** | Highest | Lowest | Moderate |
| **Saturation** | Higher | Lowest | Higher |
| **Skin tones** | Warmer, saturated | Cool-neutral | Warm, vibrant |
| **Shadows** | Deep, cool | Soft, cyan-green | Deep, warm |

---

## 4. Fuji X Weekly Recipes (2024)

**X-Trans IV/V (PRO Neg. Hi):** DR400, Grain Weak Small, Color Chrome Strong, CCB Weak, WB Auto +1R/-3B, HL +0.5, SH -1.5, Color +4, Sharpness -1, Clarity -2

**Film Dial (Reala Ace):** DR400, Grain Weak Small, Color Chrome Strong, CCB Weak, WB AWP +2R/-4B, HL -1.5, SH -1, Color +3, Sharpness -1, Clarity -2 ("Fujicolor PRO 160C Warm")

---

## 5. Digital Emulation Targets

1. Higher saturation than standard PRO line (Color +4 → Sat +20)
2. Moderate contrast with defined mid-tone structure
3. Warmer white balance bias (PRO Neg. Hi + warm WB shift)
4. Richer blues and greens — not pastel
5. Clean professional skin tones with warmth
6. Fine grain (professional 160-speed)
