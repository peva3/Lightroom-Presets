# Fuji Superia 800 — Aesthetic Characteristics

## Overview

Fuji Superia 800 (also sold as Venus 800 and Fuji Press 800 in some markets) was the **high-speed consumer color negative film** in Fujifilm's Superia lineup — positioned between the versatile Superia 400 and the specialty Superia 1600 (Natura 1600). It delivered the characteristic Fuji consumer palette with extra speed for low-light situations, producing noticeably more grain than 400 but retaining good color fidelity.

## Core Aesthetic Signature

### Cool Cast at Speed

Superia 800 maintains the Fuji consumer film cool bias even at ISO 800 — a achievement for high-speed film. Most high-speed color films warm up significantly, but Superia 800 retains a clean, slightly cool rendering that makes evening and indoor scenes look natural rather than amber-tinted.

### Grain Structure

- **Moderate-to-prominent grain** — the price paid for ISO 800 speed
- Noticeably coarser than Superia 400
- More uniform and structured than Superia 1600's rough grain
- Consumer-grade irregularity gives organic character
- Most visible in blue skies and low-light shadows
- Comparable to Ultramax 400 in grain intensity despite being one stop faster

### Green Shadow Cast

- **Pronounced green shadow** — stronger than Superia 400
- The higher speed amplifies the Fuji family's shadow trait
- Cool green-gray shadows, especially noticeable in low-light
- Creates a moody, atmospheric quality in nighttime scenes

### Magenta-Red Rendering

- Reds maintain their magenta bias even at high speed
- Slightly desaturated compared to 400 due to speed
- Red channel shows more grain than slower stocks
- Magenta push is preserved

### Greens

- Rich, slightly cool greens
- 4th color layer maintains green fidelity at high speed
- Slightly more muted than Superia 400
- Foliage under artificial light shows good color accuracy

### Blues

- Strong blues, characteristic of Fuji consumer dyes
- Skies show visible grain but maintain color saturation
- Night sky renders with a cool, atmospheric blue-black

### Skin Tones

- More natural than expected at ISO 800
- Cool bias can look editorial or clinical
- Flash produces excellent skin (designed for consumer flash use)
- 4th color layer helps under fluorescent lighting

### Contrast Profile

- **Moderate-high consumer contrast**
- More contrast than Superia 400 (compensates for speed)
- Good midtone separation in low light
- Shadows deepen with green cast; highlights roll off cleanly

### Exposure Latitude

- Good for an ISO 800 consumer film
- +1 stop overexposure (EI 400) recommended by many shooters
- Overexposure tightens grain and reduces green shadow cast
- Underexposure produces heavy grain and intense green shadows
- Not recommended for push processing beyond +1

## Best Use Cases

- Evening and low-light photography
- Indoor available-light shooting
- Concert and event photography
- Street photography at dusk
- Flash photography (film was designed for consumer flash)

## Weaknesses

- Visible grain limits enlargement
- Green shadow cast is pronounced
- Cool bias may not suit warm/romantic scenes
- Discontinued and expensive when found
- Less forgiving in mixed lighting than Portra 800

## Summary

> Fuji Superia 800 delivered the **Fuji consumer film cool palette at high speed** — producing images with pronounced green shadows, magenta-biased reds, moderate-to-prominent organic grain, and good color fidelity for an ISO 800 film. It was the practical consumer high-speed option: faster than 400 for low light, but cleaner and more predictable than 1600.
