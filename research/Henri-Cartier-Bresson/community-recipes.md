# Community Recipes — Henri Cartier-Bresson

## Known Approaches

### From r/leica and r/streetphotography
- The key is restraint — "Don't over-edit. HCB wouldn't."
- Light contrast boost, shadows slightly lifted, no aggressive sky treatment
- 50mm field of view matters more than the edit

### From YouTube Tutorials
- Minimalist editing approach: exposure correction + slight contrast
- Grain should be natural 35mm grain, not exaggerated
- No split toning, no color grading (for B&W conversion)

### From Magnum Learning Resources
- The "decisive moment" is about composition + timing, not processing
- Cartier-Bresson's printer (Voja Mitrovic) was known for straightforward printing
- No darkroom heroics — the negative was exposed correctly in-camera

### Community Consensus
- Let the image speak — this preset should be a starting point, not a heavy-handed look
- Natural contrast, natural grain, natural tonality
- If you notice the edit, you've gone too far
