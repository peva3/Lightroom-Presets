# Henri Cartier-Bresson

## Biography
- **Name**: Henri Cartier-Bresson (1908–2004)
- **Nationality**: French
- **Era**: 1930s–1970s

## Philosophy
- "The Decisive Moment" — capturing the fleeting instant where form and content unite
- Photography as an extension of the eye; the camera as a sketchbook
- Anti-cropping: the frame must be composed perfectly in-camera
- Surrealist roots: trained as a painter under André Lhote
- Co-founded Magnum Photos (1947)

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: Leica 35mm rangefinder almost exclusively, 50mm lens
- **Film**: Kodak Tri-X, Ilford HP5, various 35mm stocks
- **Processing**: Simple, no tricks. Let the scene speak.
- **Printing**: Straight prints, minimal manipulation. Trusted his lab.

## Visual Signature
- **Moderate contrast**: Not Ansel Adams dramatic, not flat. Natural tonal transitions.
- **No filters**: Shot what the eye saw. No red filter skies, no polarizers.
- **Natural rendering**: The image should look like what you'd see standing there.
- **Sharp but not aggressive**: Leica glass had beautiful rendering, but no artificial sharpness.
- **Grain visible but not dominant**: 35mm Tri-X grain is part of the texture, but never the subject.

## Iconic Images
- *Behind the Gare Saint-Lazare* (1932)
- *Rue Mouffetard* (1954)
- *Hyères, France* (1932)

## Preset Design Notes
- Conservative GrayMixer shifts — enough for definition, not dramatic
- Moderate contrast with natural falloff
- No grain exaggeration; let existing image texture carry
- No sharpening beyond what the lens delivered
