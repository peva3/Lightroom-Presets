# Ilford Pan F Plus 50 — Aesthetic Characteristics

## Overview

Ilford Pan F Plus 50 is the finest-grain, slowest-speed black-and-white film in current production. It is a traditional cubic-grain emulsion delivering exceptionally fine grain, high resolution, and high contrast — ideal for fine art, portraiture, and studio work where maximum detail and minimal grain are priorities.

## Grain

- **RMS Granularity ~7-8** — among the finest of any traditional B&W film
- Grain is nearly invisible at normal print sizes from 35mm
- At box speed (ISO 50): grain is perceptually absent even in large prints
- Pushed to 100-200: fine grain becomes barely visible
- Traditional cubic-grain structure creates a smooth, even grain pattern

## Contrast & Tonal Characteristics

- **High native contrast** — gamma ~0.70-0.75 in standard developers
- Short, steep characteristic curve with a distinct shoulder
- Deep, rich blacks with excellent D-max
- Clean, brilliant whites
- Less forgiving of exposure errors than medium-speed films
- Tight exposure latitude (~±1 stop)

## Spectral Sensitivity

- Ortho-panchromatic: slightly reduced red sensitivity compared to HP5+
- Excellent separation of closely-related tones
- Skin tones: slightly darker and more sculptural than with extended-red films
- Blue skies: slightly darker than neutral, good cloud separation

## Format Behavior

| Format | Grain Visibility | Character |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|
| 35mm | Nearly invisible up to 11×14" | Maximum detail/resolution |
| 120 | Invisible at all print sizes | Gallery-grade smoothness |
| 4×5 | Completely grainless | Ultimate resolving power |

## Developer Effects

| Developer | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| ID-11/D-76 1:1 | Reference standard — balanced contrast, fine grain |
| Perceptol | Ultra-fine grain, slightly reduced speed (~EI 32) |
| Rodinal 1:50 | Higher acutance, slightly more visible grain |
| Ilfosol 3 | Good sharpness, moderate contrast |

## Best Uses

- Studio portraiture with controlled lighting
- Fine art landscapes (especially in 120)
- Architecture and detail-critical work
- Any situation where maximum resolution and minimal grain are desired
- Not ideal for: low-light, action, street photography (too slow)
