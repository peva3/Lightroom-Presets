# Bergger Pancro 400 — Film Characteristics

## Overview
Bergger Pancro 400 is a French-made black-and-white negative film produced by Bergger Products. Its defining innovation is a dual-emulsion coating: two separate emulsion layers — one fast, one slow — are coated simultaneously on the same base. This creates an extended dynamic range with rich midtones, smooth highlight rolloff, and excellent shadow detail. Introduced around 2016.

## Key Characteristics
- **ISO**: 400 (nominal); can be shot at 200-800 with good results
- **Grain**: Moderate — dual-emulsion design smooths grain appearance despite 400 speed
- **Sharpness**: Good — the dual-layer coating provides natural edge definition
- **Contrast**: Moderate — lower than typical 400-speed films (dual-emulsion compresses overall contrast)
- **Tonal Range**: Exceptional for a 400-speed film — the dual-emulsion captures both shadow detail and highlight separation
- **Spectral Sensitivity**: Traditional panchromatic — balanced across visible spectrum
- **Anti-Halation Layer**: Between the two emulsion layers (unique to Pancro 400), providing excellent halation control
- **Development**: Compatible with all standard developers; Bergger's own Berspeed developer is optimized for it
- **Exposure Latitude**: Very wide — dual-emulsion forgives both under and overexposure

## Historical Context
- Produced in France by Bergger Products (a small, independent manufacturer)
- Bergger was historically a paper manufacturer; Pancro 400 was their first film
- The dual-emulsion approach is unique in modern B&W film production
- Coated on a clear PET base with special anti-halation between layers
- A small-batch, artisanal product — the antithesis of mass-market film

## Visual Signature
- Remarkably smooth tonal transitions — the dual-emulsion approach creates continuous tonality
- Rich, detailed shadows that don't block up
- Clean, smooth highlights that roll off naturally
- Grain is present but refined — less aggressive than HP5+ or Tri-X
- A "large format look in 35mm" — tonality reminiscent of sheet film
- Slightly cool, silvery tonality (PET base, no tint)
- Excels in high-contrast scenes where single-emulsion films would lose detail
- Portrait-friendly 400-speed film with unusually good skin tones
