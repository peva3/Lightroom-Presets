# Bergger Pancro 400 — Community Recipes

## Community Notes (from forums)
- No dedicated Fuji X Weekly recipe exists for Bergger Pancro 400
- Photrio discussions emphasize the unique dual-emulsion construction
- 35mmc (Hamish Gill) has reviewed Pancro 400 — noted the smooth tonality and "large format feel"
- Emulsive has published user reviews comparing Pancro 400 to Ilford HP5+ and Kodak Tri-X 400
- The dual-emulsion design is unique in modern production — no other 400-speed film uses this approach
- Forum consensus: Pancro 400 excels in high-contrast scenes where other 400 films lose detail
- Some users note slightly cooler tonality (clear PET base, neutral gelatin)

## Bergger Product Information
- Dual-emulsion coating: fast layer + slow layer coated simultaneously
- Active gelatin interlayer with anti-halation dye between the two emulsions
- Clear PET base (no gray tint like Kodak/Ilford films)
- Optimized for Berspeed developer but compatible with all standard developers
- ISO 200-800 range with development time adjustments

## LR Preset Design Notes
- Dual-emulsion smooth grain: GrainAmount=35, Size=30, Roughness=50
  - The dual-layer design naturally smooths grain appearance
  - Less aggressive than standard 400-speed cubic grain films
- Lower contrast (+18) — the dual-emulsion compresses overall contrast range
  - Both highlight and shadow detail are preserved
- Balanced panchromatic GrayMixer: Red +20, Orange +15, Blue -22
  - Natural skin tone rendering is a priority
- Modest blacks (-20) — not crushed, the dual-emulsion preserves shadow detail
- Controlled highlights (-20) — smooth rolloff from the slow emulsion layer
- Good shadow lift (+18) — the fast emulsion layer captures shadow detail
- All STYLEGUIDE rules: Sharpness=10 with grain, Clarity/Texture/Dehaze=0, no Calibration, no WB
- The extended dynamic range (moderate contrast + wide Blacks-to-Shadows spread) defines this preset
