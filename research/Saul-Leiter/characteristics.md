# Saul Leiter

## Biography
- **Name**: Saul Leiter (1923–2013)
- **Nationality**: American
- **Era**: 1940s–2000s (rediscovered 2000s)

## Philosophy
- "I believe there is such a thing as a search for beauty."
- "A window covered with raindrops interests me more than a photograph of a famous person."
- Pioneer of color street photography before it was accepted
- Painter first — his photographic color sense came from painting
- Never sought fame; content to make small prints for himself

## Technical Approach
- **Gear**: Leica M, various 35mm cameras, long telephoto lenses
- **Film**: Kodachrome (expired stock — his signature look came partly from outdated film)
- **Shooting through**: Windows, reflections, steam, snow, rain, glass, plastic sheeting
- **Telephoto compression**: Used long lenses for flattened, painterly perspective
- **Printing**: Small prints, later large-format inkjets from his slides

## Visual Signature
- **Through-glass softness**: Reflections on top of subjects, double exposures from window glass
- **Painterly compression**: Long lens use flattens space into abstract color planes
- **Muted, elegant palette**: Expired Kodachrome shifted toward muted greens, ochres, rusts, and deep reds
- **Obscured vision**: Snowflakes, raindrops, fogged windows — the obstruction IS the image
- **Abstract color geometry**: Umbrellas, awnings, cars, coats reduced to color blocks
- **Intimate scale**: Small, quiet moments, never grandiose

## Iconic Images
- *Taxi* (1950s) — through rain-streaked window
- *Snow* series (1950s–60s)
- *Harlem* series (1950s)

## Preset Design Notes
- Muted saturation — negative global saturation with selective color preservation
- Cool shadow tint (teal/blue) for the through-glass/winter atmosphere
- Negative clarity for the soft, painterly quality
- Negative dehaze for atmospheric mist/fog
- Muted greens and blues — expired film palette
- Blending at 75 for stylistic color separation (cinematic/creative classification)
