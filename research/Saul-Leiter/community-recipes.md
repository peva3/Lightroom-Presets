# Community Recipes — Saul Leiter

## Known Approaches

### From r/photography and r/streetphotography
- Leiter's rediscovery in the 2000s sparked intense interest in his color palette
- Expired Kodachrome is frequently discussed — the color shifts from aging are part of the aesthetic
- Shooting through windows/reflections is widely imitated by street photographers

### From YouTube Tutorials
- Several tutorials attempt the "Saul Leiter painterly look":
  - Reduced clarity for softness
  - Muted color palette with preserved reds
  - Cool shadow tints for the winter/glass atmosphere
  - Lens blur overlays to simulate shooting through glass
- Texture reduction mentioned for the painterly quality

### From Art Photography Blogs
- Leiter's abstract color field approach is compared to abstract expressionist painting
- The flattening effect of telephoto + window glass creates the "painterly" compression
- No single slider achieves this — it's a combination of lens choice + shooting conditions + processing

### Community Consensus
- Negative clarity + negative dehaze are the essential digital moves
- Cool shadows (teal/blue) define the atmosphere
- Muted global saturation with preserved warm tones
- Works best on images with natural atmospheric obstruction (fog, rain, glass)
