# Ilford SFX 200 — Aesthetic Characteristics

## Overview

Ilford SFX 200 is a near-infrared extended-red-sensitivity black-and-white film. Unlike true infrared films (Kodak HIE, Rollei Infrared), SFX has extended red sensitivity that peaks around 720nm with a sharp cutoff at 740nm — making it a "near-IR" or "super-panchromatic" film rather than full infrared. With a deep red (R72) filter, it produces the classic infrared look: white foliage, near-black skies, and ethereal, glowing landscapes. Without a filter, it behaves like a moderately extended-red panchromatic film. ISO 200 (without filter), EI 6–12 with R72 filter.

## Core Aesthetic Signature

### Spectral Sensitivity

- **Without filter**: Extended red sensitivity. Slightly lighter skin tones (caucasian), slightly darker blue skies. Be-haves similarly to standard panchromatic film with a subtle red emphasis.
- **With deep red (R72) filter**: The classic IR look. Chlorophyll-rich vegetation (grass, leaves) reflects infrared strongly → bright white "Wood Effect." Blue skies, water, and shadows render near-black. Clouds stand out dramatically against dark skies. The world transforms into an alien, luminous landscape.

### Contrast & Tonality

- **Overall Contrast (no filter)**: Medium. Similar to standard ISO 200 B&W films.
- **With R72 filter**: High contrast. The IR effect naturally separates bright foliage from dark skies, creating dramatic contrast without needing to push development.
- **Highlight Rendering**: Bright vegetation can approach pure white — careful exposure needed to retain texture.
- **Shadow Rendering**: Deep shadows with IR filter — skies and water go near-black quickly.

### Grain

- **Grain Level**: Medium. ISO 200 with extended red sensitivity produces moderate grain.
- **Grain Character**: Regular, slightly more prominent than FP4+ due to the extended emulsion technology.
- **Comparison**: Similar grain to HP5+ at box speed, coarser than FP4+.

### Practical Effects

- **Wood Effect**: The defining IR characteristic. Deciduous trees and grass reflect IR strongly → brilliant white. Conifers reflect less → medium grey. This creates dramatic tonal separation in landscapes.
- **Skin Tones**: Slightly lighter than normal panchromatic rendering. The "porcelain skin" IR portrait look is possible with filtration.
- **Atmospheric Haze Penetration**: IR wavelengths penetrate haze better than visible light — distant mountains and subjects gain clarity.

### Comparison to True IR Films

| Feature | SFX 200 | True IR (Kodak HIE) |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| Peak sensitivity | ~720nm | ~820nm |
| IR look | Moderate "near-IR" | Extreme full IR |
| Wood Effect | Visible, not extreme | Dramatic white |
| Filter needed | R72 for IR effect | R72 or #25 red |
| Handling | Easy — load in subdued light | Difficult — load in total darkness |

## Summary

```
Near-infrared extended-red sensitivity (720nm peak)
Classic IR "Wood Effect" with R72 filter
Medium grain at ISO 200
Dramatic dark skies + luminous white foliage
Also usable as subtle extended-red panchromatic film
```
