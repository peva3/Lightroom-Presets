## Overview

Photography continues to attract enthusiasts and professionals across multiple facets, from cutting-edge AI technologies to the resurgence of historical processes.

## Trend 1: Generative AI in Photography

### Current State
Artificial Intelligence is at the center of photographic discussion. While some photographers remain skeptical, most are embracing AI in various parts of their practice.

### Key Applications
- **Outpainting**: Expanding image backgrounds beyond original frame
- **Inpainting**: Realistic edits to photograph elements
- **Text-to-image generation**: DALL-E 2, Midjourney creating both realistic and surreal imagery
- **Style transfer**: Copying the aesthetic of one image onto another
- **Software integration**: Photoshop AI Generative Fill, AI Generative Expand tools

### Impact on Presets
AI tools can now analyze and replicate editing styles, fundamentally changing how presets are created and used. The line between "preset application" and "AI style transfer" is blurring.

## Trend 2: Sustainability in Photography

### Equipment Trends
- Shift toward equipment built with recycled materials
- Energy-efficient camera and editing hardware
- Green business practices: waste reduction, eco-friendly packaging

### Content Trends
- Environmental documentary projects highlighting human impact
- Educational workshops on eco-friendly photography practices
- Growing market for "green" photography brands

### Implication for Digital Workflows
The sustainability movement reinforces the value of digital presets over physical film consumption, as digital editing eliminates chemical processing waste entirely.

## Trend 3: Drone Photography Expansion

### Technology Drivers
- Higher-quality cameras even in entry-level drones
- Enhanced flight control and stability
- Compact, lightweight designs
- Automated obstacle avoidance
- Improved safety features

### Market Growth
More photographers entering drone photography as barriers lower. Clearer regulations in most countries are reducing uncertainty. Professional applications expanding across real estate, events, and landscape photography.

### Editing Implications
Drone photography creates demand for specific preset styles: aerial landscape looks, top-down flat lays, and HDR processing suitable for high dynamic range aerial scenes.

## Trend 4: Self-Portrait Storytelling

### Creative Direction
- Personal narrative as primary artistic motivation
- Exploration of identity, mental health, and collective experiences
- Therapeutic photography for healing past trauma
- Creative freedom: photographer is both creator and subject
- Technology enabling surreal elements to represent different mental states

### Social Media Connection
Self-portrait storytelling connects photographers with audiences through intimate, authentic content. This drives demand for consistent, recognizable editing styles (presets) that define a creator's visual brand.

## Trend 5: Alternative Photography Techniques

### Resurgence of Historical Processes
- **Cyanotype**: Blue-tinted prints from iron compounds
- **Platinum printing**: Rich tonal range with archival stability
- **Wet plate collodion**: 1850s process on glass or metal
- **Chlorophyll printing**: Sustainable prints using plant pigments
- **Anthotypes**: Images from plant emulsions, completely non-toxic

### Digital-Film Hybrid
Film photography has experienced a resurgence, with many photographers shooting film then scanning for digital post-processing. This creates demand for presets that accurately emulate film stocks in digital workflows.

## Key Takeaways for Preset Development

1. **AI integration is inevitable** — presets should be designed with AI compatibility in mind
2. **Sustainability narrative** — digital presets align with eco-conscious photography values
3. **Film emulation demand** — resurgence of analog aesthetics creates market for authentic film preset packs
4. **Social media consistency** — creators need recognizable, branded editing styles across platforms
5. **Niche aesthetics** — alternative process looks (cyanotype, wet plate) represent untapped preset categories
