# WKW Film References — Key Scenes & Color Analysis Per Film

## Chungking Express (1994)

**Director:** Wong Kar-wai
**Cinematographers:** Christopher Doyle, Andrew Lau
**Runtime:** 98 minutes
**Stock:** 35mm, Kodak Vision 500T
**Locations:** Chungking Mansions, Lan Kwai Fong (Midnight Express), Central-Mid-Levels Escalator

### Key Scenes for Color Reference

#### Scene 1: Opening Chase — Chungking Mansions (00:00-03:00)
- **Style:** Extreme handheld, step-printed, blooming highlights
- **Colors:** Deep fluorescent green shadows, warm practical lights
- **Technique:** Doyle's handheld camera runs through the chaotic Chungking Mansions corridors
- **Notable:** The opening establishes the green-cyan fluorescent aesthetic immediately
- **Lightroom focus:** Deep green blacks, motion blur simulation (not possible in stills), cyan highlight bloom

#### Scene 2: Cop 223 and the Woman in Blonde Wig — Bottoms Up Club (Story 1)
- **Style:** Intimate close-ups, available bar lighting
- **Colors:** Warm amber/gold from bar lighting with green fluorescent in backgrounds
- **Technique:** Mixed color temperatures create depth between foreground (warm) and background (cool)
- **Notable:** The woman's blonde wig reflects warm bar light, creating a golden halo
- **Lightroom focus:** Warm skin tones against green environment, gold highlights

#### Scene 3: Cop 223's Apartment / Pineapple Cans (Story 1)
- **Style:** Static medium shots, intimate close-ups of objects
- **Colors:** Warm domestic tungsten with green exterior light through windows
- **Technique:** Doyle uses practical lamps and window light
- **Notable:** The expiration date on pineapple cans — objects as emotional symbols
- **Lightroom focus:** Warm interior WB, green tint from window fluorescent seepage

#### Scene 4: Midnight Express — Faye Wong Introduction (Story 2)
- **Style:** Handheld around the counter, extreme close-ups
- **Colors:** Warm gold counter light (key), green-blue background (contrast)
- **Technique:** The counter light IS the key light — faces are lit from below by the food counter
- **Notable:** Faye Wong's iconic short hair, "California Dreamin'" playing loudly
- **Lightroom focus:** Gold-yellow midtones, split between warm foreground and cool background

#### Scene 5: Cop 663's Apartment — Faye Sneaks In (Story 2)
- **Style:** Wide shots of small apartment, slow pans
- **Colors:** Mixed — green from outside, warm from inside practicals
- **Technique:** Shot in Christopher Doyle's actual apartment
- **Notable:** Faye redecorating — objects changing subtly, the toy airplane, the goldfish
- **Lightroom focus:** Claustrophobic wide shots with layered color temperatures

#### Scene 6: Escalator — Faye and Cop 663 (Story 2)
- **Style:** Slow motion/step-printed, medium long shots
- **Colors:** Cool blue-grey ambient with warm street lighting
- **Technique:** Shot on the Central-Mid-Levels escalator at night
- **Notable:** The escalator becomes a recurring visual motif — movement without destination
- **Lightroom focus:** Cool night blue, warm streetlamp pops, slight underexposure

#### Scene 7: Midnight Express Closing — Cop 663 Takes Over
- **Style:** Static, composed wide shot
- **Colors:** Same gold counter lighting, now more prominent
- **Technique:** The warm counter light dominates — the "bright" side of Hong Kong
- **Notable:** Cop 663 has transformed the fast food stand — the circularity of the film
- **Lightroom focus:** Warm dominant with cool accents, hopeful gold tones

## Fallen Angels (1995)

**Director:** Wong Kar-wai
**Cinematographer:** Christopher Doyle
**Runtime:** 99 minutes
**Stock:** 35mm, multiple stocks including B&W
**Locations:** Chungking Mansions, night streets, tunnels, McDonald's

### Key Scenes for Color Reference

#### Scene 1: Hitman's Partner's Apartment (Recurring)
- **Style:** Extreme wide-angle, claustrophobic close-ups
- **Colors:** Deep green fluorescent cast, crushed blacks
- **Technique:** 6.8mm-9.8mm wide-angle lenses inches from the subject's face
- **Notable:** The partner's isolation conveyed through distorted lenses — she's alone in every frame
- **Lightroom focus:** Extreme green shadow, wide-angle distortion emulation (digital crop with barrel distortion), heavy contrast

#### Scene 2: Night Streets / Tunnels — Hitman's World
- **Style:** Handheld tracking, wide-angle, out-of-focus transitions
- **Colors:** Green neon, magenta accents, deep black shadows
- **Technique:** Doyle shoots through tunnels and underpasses, using existing fluorescent/neon
- **Notable:** The green filter is at its most extreme here — this is the grunge aesthetic
- **Lightroom focus:** Maximum green calibration shift, neon magenta saturation pop, heavy black crush

#### Scene 3: McDonald's — Hitman Meets Blondie
- **Style:** Static interior shots, fluorescent overhead lighting
- **Colors:** Sickly green-tinged white, McDonald's red accents
- **Technique:** Fluorescent tube lighting from above creates unflattering, alienating light
- **Notable:** "Forget Him" plays over the speaker — the music coincides with the partner's breakdown
- **Lightroom focus:** Green-white fluorescent, red pop from brand colors, fluorescent color cast on skin

#### Scene 4: Ho Chi-mo's Night "Business" (Story 2)
- **Style:** Kinetic handheld, B&W inserts, grainy texture
- **Colors:** Color sections: deep green night; B&W sections: high contrast grain
- **Technique:** Multiple film stock changes — the Wikipedia entry confirms "different film stocks throughout the film and also a number of scenes where the film switches to black and white inserts covered in a grainy 'noise' effect"
- **Notable:** Takeshi Kaneshiro's mute character forces ice cream on strangers — absurdist comedy in the darkness
- **Lightroom focus:** Extreme grain, B&W high-contrast for some scenes, green night for others

#### Scene 5: Motorcycle Finale — Partner and Ho Chi-mo
- **Style:** Wide-angle from motorcycle, motion blur, sunrise
- **Colors:** Transition from green/black night to warm dawn
- **Technique:** Camera mounted on the motorcycle — wide-angle lens captures both riders and the road
- **Notable:** "Only You" by The Flying Pickets — the only hopeful moment in the film
- **Lightroom focus:** Warm sunrise transition, motion blur (slow shutter analogy), emotional warmth breaking through the green

#### Scene 6: Kai Tak Airport Footage
- **Style:** Documentary-style B&W and color inserts
- **Colors:** Varied — some B&W, some color-saturated
- **Technique:** Mixed stocks create textural variety
- **Notable:** Kai Tak's approach through Kowloon — the famous low-altitude flights over the city
- **Lightroom focus:** Documentary/archival texture, mixed color treatments

## In the Mood for Love (2000)

**Director:** Wong Kar-wai
**Cinematographers:** Christopher Doyle, Mark Lee Ping-bin (Doyle departed mid-shoot)
**Runtime:** 98 minutes
**Stock:** 35mm
**Locations:** 1960s Hong Kong apartment building, Singapore, Angkor Wat

### Key Scenes for Color Reference

#### Scene 1: The Hallway (Recurring Throughout)
- **Style:** Slow tracking shots, narrow corridor framing
- **Colors:** Deep red (curtains/walls), warm amber (practical lamps), deep shadow
- **Technique:** Characters pass each other in the narrow hallway, the camera tracks with slow, deliberate movement
- **Notable:** The hallway is the central visual motif — every encounter, every glance, every missed moment happens here
- **Lightroom focus:** Red saturation boost, warm amber highlights, deep blacks with detail, slatted light

#### Scene 2: The Noodle Stall
- **Style:** Rain-streaked night, steam, practical lamps
- **Colors:** Warm gold from the stall, cool blue from night rain, steam diffusion
- **Technique:** The steam from the noodle stall creates natural diffusion — soft, hazy atmosphere
- **Notable:** Chow and Su's ritual of buying noodles — always separately, always passing each other
- **Lightroom focus:** Steam/haze effect (negative dehaze + negative clarity), warm gold practical on faces, cool night blue background

#### Scene 3: Hotel Room 2046
- **Style:** Intimate close-ups, red drapes, lamp light
- **Colors:** Crimson red, warm amber, gold highlights, deep shadow
- **Technique:** The red drapes create the dominant color — a womb-like, intimate space
- **Notable:** They write martial arts serials together — the room as creative sanctuary and romantic impossibility
- **Lightroom focus:** Red dominance, gold highlights, skin tones warm but slightly shadowed

#### Scene 4: Rainy Street / Alley
- **Style:** Medium shots, rain, reflections
- **Colors:** Cool blue night, warm streetlamp reflections on wet pavement
- **Technique:** Rain creates natural diffusion and reflection — the city becomes a mirror
- **Notable:** They shelter from rain together — a rare moment of physical proximity
- **Lightroom focus:** Wet pavement reflections (boost clarity locally), cool night blue with warm point sources, cinematic rain atmosphere

#### Scene 5: Singapore — Su Visits Chow's Apartment
- **Style:** Static wide shots, quiet observation
- **Colors:** Warm Southeast Asian light, muted greens, amber interiors
- **Technique:** Different DP (Mark Lee Ping-bin) — more static, contemplative framing
- **Notable:** Su calls Chow but says nothing — the lipstick-stained cigarette in the ashtray
- **Lightroom focus:** Warmer overall balance, muted saturation, contemplative stillness

#### Scene 6: Angkor Wat — The Whispered Secret
- **Style:** Wide landscape shots, contemplative pacing
- **Colors:** Warm stone, overcast sky, monk's saffron robes, natural greens
- **Technique:** The camera pulls back from Chow at the temple wall — the widest, most open shot in the film
- **Notable:** The secret whispered into the stone cavity — sealed with mud, forever unknown
- **Lightroom focus:** Natural color palette (contrast with the artificial Hong Kong palette), warm earth tones, overcast soft light

## Happy Together (1997)

**Director:** Wong Kar-wai
**Cinematographer:** Christopher Doyle
**Runtime:** 96 minutes
**Stock:** 35mm, B&W + Color stocks
**Locations:** Buenos Aires, Iguazu Falls, Taipei

### Key Scenes for Color Reference

#### Scene 1: Opening — B&W Sex Scene / Breakup
- **Style:** Intimate handheld, grainy B&W
- **Colors:** Monochrome — deep blacks, grainy mid-tones, slightly warm whites
- **Technique:** Push-processed B&W stock for high contrast and heavy grain
- **Notable:** The passport shown on screen — nationality and identity are immediate themes
- **Lightroom focus:** B&W treatment with heavy grain, high contrast, deep blacks

#### Scene 2: Iguazu Falls — First Color Shot
- **Style:** Wide landscape, bird's-eye view (on the lamp first, then real)
- **Colors:** Supernatural blue-green water, deep green foliage, white mist
- **Technique:** The first color shot in the film — described as "unrealistic blue and green colouring, giving it a supernatural visual effect" (Wikipedia)
- **Notable:** The falls represent the couple's relationship — powerful, destructive, beautiful, unreachable together
- **Lightroom focus:** Intense aqua/blue saturation, green foliage, misty white highlights, surreal quality

#### Scene 3: The Tango Bar / Kitchen
- **Style:** Intimate close-ups, warm amber light, dance sequences
- **Colors:** Deep amber/gold, red accents, dense shadows
- **Technique:** Practical bar lighting — Astor Piazzolla's tango music drives the visual rhythm
- **Notable:** The tango in the kitchen — domestic intimacy as dance
- **Lightroom focus:** Warm amber dominance, deep warm shadows, gold highlights

#### Scene 4: Fai's Cramped Apartment
- **Style:** Claustrophobic wide shots, dirty mirrors, confined spaces
- **Colors:** Sickly yellow-green fluorescent, dingy whites, warm practical lamps
- **Technique:** The confined apartment represents the couple's trapped relationship
- **Notable:** The iguazu lamp is always on — spinning, rotating, marking time
- **Lightroom focus:** Claustrophobic framing, sick green-yellow fluorescent, warm practical accents

#### Scene 5: Public Bathroom Corridor
- **Style:** High-contrast wide shots, fluorescent lighting
- **Colors:** Harsh green fluorescent, deep shadows, reflective tiles
- **Technique:** The bathroom becomes a space of confinement and marginalization
- **Notable:** Represents the couple's displacement — neither at home in Hong Kong nor Buenos Aires
- **Lightroom focus:** Harsh fluorescent green, tile reflections, institutional lighting feel

#### Scene 6: The Abattoir / Fai's Day Job
- **Style:** Documentary-style observation, clinical white/blue
- **Colors:** Cool clinical white, blood red accents, steel grey
- **Technique:** Fai works at the abattoir — the harsh work contrasts with the romantic tango bar
- **Lightroom focus:** Clinical cool white, desaturated environment, blood red accent

#### Scene 7: Taipei Night Market — Finale
- **Style:** Warm night street photography, handheld
- **Colors:** Warm market lights, red lanterns, gold stall lighting
- **Technique:** Fai visits Chang's family noodle stall — finds peace and belonging
- **Notable:** "Happy Together" plays — the only hopeful note, Fai at peace with his past
- **Lightroom focus:** Warm night market colors, gold/amber dominance, hopeful warm saturation

## Technical Viewing Notes

### Aspect Ratios by Film:
- Chungking Express: 1.85:1
- Fallen Angels: 1.85:1
- In the Mood for Love: 1.66:1 (European ratio, more vertical space)
- Happy Together: 1.85:1

### Restoration Notes:
- The 2020-2021 Criterion 4K restorations, supervised by Wong Kar-wai, made controversial changes:
  - Aspect ratio changes on some films (Fallen Angels went from 1.85:1 to 2.39:1)
  - Color grading was significantly altered — greens were sometimes reduced
  - Wong's statement: "I invite the audience to join me in starting afresh, as these are not the same films, and we are no longer the same audience"
- For preset creation, the ORIGINAL theatrical color timing is the better reference for the "classic WKW look"
- The Criterion DVD/early Blu-ray releases (2008-2012) more closely match the original theatrical color
