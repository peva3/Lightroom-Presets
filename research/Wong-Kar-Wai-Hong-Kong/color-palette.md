# WKW Film-by-Film Color Palette Reference

## Methodology
Hex codes are approximate — derived from analyzing frame grabs, 4K restorations, and community color sampling. Colors vary by scene, transfer/restoration, and display calibration. These represent the *characteristic average* of each film's palette.

## Common Palette Threads Across All WKW Films

### The Recurring 4-Color System

| Role | Color Range | Hex Examples |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Shadow Base** | Deep Green-Teal | `#0a1a15`, `#0d2b33`, `#0a1518` |
| **Mid-tone Environment** | Fluorescent Green-Cyan | `#1a3a2a`, `#152a30`, `#1a3020` |
| **Warm Accent** | Gold/Amber/Red | `#d4a040`, `#c03020`, `#c88030` |
| **Highlight Bloom** | Tinted White/Cream | `#d0f0e0` (green), `#f0d8c0` (warm) |

### The WKW Shadow Color (Most Critical)
The single most important color for the WKW aesthetic is the **fluorescent green-black shadow**:
- Approximate Hex: `#0f2018` to `#142820`
- In HSL terms: Hue ~160°, Saturation ~30-40%, Lightness ~8-12%
- In RGB: G channel is consistently higher than R and B
- This is what gives the images their "green shadow" character
- It's NOT a pure green — it's a green-leaning near-black

### The Doyle Green
Christopher Doyle's characteristic "green":
- In fluorescent-lit interiors: H~150-165°, S~30-50%, L~15-30%
- In neon-lit night scenes: H~170-180°, S~20-40%, L~10-20%
- In underexposed film: H~140-150°, S~25-40%, L~5-15%

## Color Palette for Preset Construction

### Target HSL Ranges for the Preset:

**Greens (Hue 60-100 in Lightroom)**
- Shift toward teal/blue-green (Hue -20 to -40)
- Increase saturation (+20 to +40)
- Reduce luminance (-10 to -20 for depth)

**Aquas/Blues (Hue 180-230 in Lightroom)**
- Shift aqua toward green (Hue -10 to -20)
- Increase blue saturation (+15 to +30)
- Slightly darken luminance (-5 to -15)

**Reds (Hue 0-30 in Lightroom)**
- Shift toward orange-red (for neon authenticity)
- Increase saturation (+10 to +25)
- Slight luminance lift for glow (+5 to +15)

**Oranges (Skin tones)**
- Keep hue relatively neutral
- Slightly reduce saturation (-5 to -15) to keep skin natural
- Slightly lift luminance (+5 to +10)

**Yellows**
- Shift toward gold/amber (Hue -5 to -15)
- Moderate saturation (+10 to +20)
- Lift luminance for practical source glow (+10 to +20)

**Magentas/Purples**
- Increase saturation (+20 to +40) for neon pop
- Shift toward magenta (Hue +10 to +20)
- Lift luminance for neon glow effect
