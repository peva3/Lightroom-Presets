## Technical Specifications

### Granularity
- **RMS Granularity: 7** — The finest grain of any color slide film ever produced. This is an exceptionally low RMS value; for comparison, Provia 100F is RMS 8, Velvia 50 is RMS 9.

### Resolution (MTF)
- **135 lines/mm** at 1000:1 contrast
- **60 lines/mm** at 1.6:1 contrast
- Extremely high resolving power suitable for large exhibition prints and fine-art reproduction.

### Color Characteristics
Fuji designed Astia 100F specifically for accurate, pleasing skin tone reproduction. The datasheet descriptions emphasized:

- **Neutral-to-slightly-cool white balance** — Optimized for daylight (5500K); very clean whites without the warm bias found in some portrait films.
- **Moderate color saturation** — Subdued and natural rather than vivid. Less saturated than Provia 100F, and dramatically less so than Velvia.
- **Smooth tonal gradation** — Especially in the highlight-to-midtone transition, which is critical for skin.
- **"Portrait Transparency"** — Fujifilm's own marketing positioned this as the slide film for portrait, fashion, and wedding photographers who wanted a transparency workflow with skin-friendly results.

### Contrast
- **Lower contrast than other Fujichrome emulsions.** This was a deliberate design goal. Typical slide films have inherently high contrast (Dmax ~3.6-4.0, high gamma), making them unforgiving for skin tones. Astia 100F was engineered with a softer shoulder in the highlights and better shadow separation, producing a more "printable" slide.

### Characteristic Curve (General)
- **Shoulder:** Pronounced roll-off in highlights, retaining detail in bright skin areas (foreheads, cheeks) rather than clipping to pure white.
- **Toe:** Good shadow detail, less crushed than Velvia or Provia.
- **Linear midtones:** Faithful reproduction of midtone values, critical for accurate skin.

### Exposure Latitude
- Very limited (as with all E-6 slide films). ±1/2 stop is the usable range.
- Recommended EI: 100. Some photographers rated at EI 80 for slightly more open shadows.
- No push/pull recommended for portraits (unlike Provia, which handles pushes well).

### Reciprocity
- Compensation required for exposures > 128 seconds (as typical for Fujichrome emulsions).
- No filter correction needed for exposures up to 1/4000 sec.

### Filter Recommendations
- **Daylight:** No filter
- **Tungsten (3200K):** 80A filter + 2 stops compensation; not ideal — color crossover in shadows
- **Fluorescent:** Not recommended for critical color work without CC filtration

### Storage
- Refrigerate at < 10°C (50°F) for long-term storage
- Allow 3-6 hours to warm before opening canister
- Process promptly after exposure

## Official Fujifilm Description (paraphrased from archived marketing copy)

> Fujichrome Astia 100F is a medium-speed, daylight-type color reversal film with an ISO rating of 100. It is especially designed for portraiture and fashion photography, offering beautifully natural and faithful color reproduction. The fine grain (RMS 7) and high sharpness ensure excellent results even in large-size enlargements. The film is characterized by soft, smooth tonal gradation and natural skin-tone reproduction with an elegant finish.
