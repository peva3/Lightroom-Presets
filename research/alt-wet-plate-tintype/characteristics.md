# Wet Plate Tintype Lightroom Preset — Complete Aesthetic Breakdown

## Core Aesthetic Signature

The wet plate tintype Lightroom preset recreates this look digitally using Lightroom's color grading tools for authentic film emulation. The tintype (ferrotype), patented by Hamilton Smith in 1856, is a direct positive on a thin iron plate lacquered black. Hugely popular from the 1860s–1880s for its durability and low cost. The black lacquer reverses the negative's tonal range: underexposed areas appear dark (the black plate shows through), while exposed/developed areas appear as whitish silver. The result is a warm-toned, laterally reversed positive with soft edges.

## Historical Context

**Era:** 1856–1890s (peak popularity 1860s–1880s)

Collodion wet plate on black-lacquered iron. Silver nitrate sensitizer + ferrous sulfate developer. The image is physically a negative but appears positive against the black background. Potassium cyanide fix (later sodium thiosulfate).

## Visual Characteristics

Warm brown-grey silver image on a black field. Characteristic soft-edged vignette from the pouring process. Lower midtone contrast than modern prints. Highlights are warm silver-white; shadows drop into the black plate. Slight metallic sheen. Often has pour marks and edge artifacts.

## Emotional & Artistic Vocabulary

Haunting, intimate, historical. The soft edges and dark field create a tunnel-vision intimacy unique to the process. Evokes Civil War and Victorian portraiture.

## Technical Notes for Digital Emulation

This preset is designed to capture the essential visual character of the wet plate tintype process using Lightroom's slider-based toolset. It prioritizes the emotional/aesthetic impact over perfect chemical accuracy, which would require a 3D LUT or DCP camera profile.

### Limitations of Slider-Based Simulation
- True process-specific spectral sensitivity cannot be replicated with sliders
- Chemical stain patterns are simulated through grain and color grading
- Paper/plate texture is approximated through grain settings
