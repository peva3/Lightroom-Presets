### Recipe → Lightroom Translation

500T is the tungsten-balanced cinema workhorse — rich contrast, olive greens, cinematic S-curve, structured grain. Translation priorities: medium-high contrast, olive green shift, warm midtone depth, cinematic grain. No WB shift (STYLEGUIDE rule) — focus on contrast, color, and grain.

#### Basic Panel
- **Exposure**: +0.20 (cinema film baseline — slight brightness)
- **Contrast**: +28 (medium-high — 500T has more contrast than 250D)
- **Highlights**: -45 (cinematic shoulder roll-off)
- **Shadows**: +20 (open cinema shadow detail)
- **Whites**: -15 (compressed white point — cinema characteristic)
- **Blacks**: -20 (rich deep shadows — Vision3 density)

#### Color Grading
- **Highlights**: Hue 42°, Sat 8 (warm golden cinema highlight)
- **Shadows**: Hue 210°, Sat 10 (cool teal shadow — cinema split tone)
- **Blending**: 50
- **Balance**: +15 (warm highlight bias)

#### Grain (Cinema stock: Amount 20-30)
- **Amount**: 25 (visible structured grain)
- **Size**: 30 (moderate — cinema texture)
- **Roughness**: 50 (regular — Vision3 structure)

#### STYLEGUIDE Compliance
- No Calibration
- No Temperature/Tint
- Sharpness=10, Clarity=0, Texture=0, Dehaze=0 (grain protection)
- LuminanceSmoothing=0
- Vibrance -2, Saturation -5 (gap = 3)
- Blacks -20 ≥ -30

## Reddit r/postprocessing and r/colorists Approaches

Users emulating Vision3 500T for stills:
1. **CineStill 800T preset as starting point**: Remove halation effects, add more contrast, richer blacks
2. **Green shift is key**: The +15 olive shift on green hue is the "movie green" — nearly universal across Vision3 emulations
3. **Contrast note**: 500T has more contrast in ECN-2 than CineStill in C-41
4. **Grain difference**: Without rem-jet, CineStill appears grainier — 500T is finer for its speed
