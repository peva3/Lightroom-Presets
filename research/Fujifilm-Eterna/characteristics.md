# Fujifilm Eterna — Characteristics

## Film Stock Profile
- **Type:** Color negative cinema film
- **Manufacturer:** Fujifilm (Eterna line, now Eterna-Cinema)
- **Status:** Professional cinema stock
- **Format:** 35mm cinema, 16mm
- **Related:** Eterna Vivid 250D, Eterna 400T, Eterna 500T

## Aesthetic Character
- **Primary:** Soft, muted, low-contrast cinematic look with warm cast
- **Contrast:** Very low — designed for digital intermediate (DI) post-production flexibility
- **Saturation:** Moderate, slightly subdued — naturalistic rather than vivid
- **Skin tones:** Warm, natural, slightly golden — the key Eterna characteristic
- **Greens:** Muted, olive-leaning — not vibrant
- **Blues:** Slightly warm, cyan-leaning
- **Shadows:** Open, lifted — cinema latitude
- **Highlights:** Compressed, gentle rolloff — no harsh clipping
- **Overall vibe:** Soft indie film, summer afternoon, naturalistic documentary, European cinema

## Technical Notes
- Eterna was designed for scanning + DI grading, not direct projection
- Very wide dynamic range — 14+ stops
- Low contrast meant to be "corrected" in post, but the soft look became beloved
- Discontinued as a stills film; Eterna Cinema line continues
- "Eterna Summer" recipe from Fuji X Weekly captures the warm, soft character

## Fuji Film Simulation Base
- The Fuji X-T4+ "Eterna Bleach Bypass" film simulation is the digital equivalent
- Eterna (color) recipe uses the Eterna/Cinema profile with specific tuning
- Not to be confused with Eterna Bleach Bypass (silver retention variant)
